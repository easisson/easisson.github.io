1875 Dec 30-Jan 21, 1876

Image Id: 002-0248
Transcription
  • Thu Dec 30:  T. hauled wood and bark. S. sowed & split wood. W. piled bark and graded sloughs.
  • Fri Dec 31:  Very hard wind all last night & rain. W. & T. dug ditch on the Allen "80". N.W. gale & rain in the afternoon. S. sawed wood and pin timber. [margin: Heavy rain last night.]
  • Sat Jan 01:  T. oiled harness. W. & S. made pin, sawed wood, & worked on the shop.
  • Sun Jan 02:  All staid at home. Mr D'Arcys folks, Mrs. Ball, Arthur & Clarence White were here to dinner.
  • Mon Jan 03:  All hands went to the Grand Ball at LaConner. Rained part of the way there and we got pretty wet. Had a good time. [margin: Rainy.]
  • Tue Jan 04:  Arrived home from Ball at noon. Feeling pretty sleepy.
  • Wed Jan 05:  All went down on Allen's line ditching, it is pretty wet. Went down to fix the box by the warehouse at 12 midnight. Too much tide.
  • Thu Jan 06:  Ditching the same as yesterday. Went & fixed the box at 9 oclock. [margin: Cold & Rainy]
  • Fri Jan 07:  Vinz packed the meat over. W. & S. ditching on South "80". T. working on the road. Ditching. Nice day.
  • Sat Jan 08:  Ditching the same as yesterday. [margin: Pleasant day.]
  • Sun Jan 09:  All staid at home. S. went around the dyke and found the "50 ft" slough box open. Went down and fixed it at night. Ball call in the P.M. Am reading "We and our Night on's" [margin: Clear & Frosty night]
  • Mon Jan 10:  S. worked on the road 1/2 day, cutting tree our of the road and sawed word the balance of the day. T. hauled wood. W. worked about the house in the A.M. and went to LaConner in the P.M. [margin: clear & cold]
  • Tue Jan 11:  S. sawed wood and made shakes in the P.M. T. Hauled mangolds. W. at LaConner. [margin: Mercury at 29°]
  • Wed Jan 12:  S. made shakes. T. hauled rails & wood. W. at LaConner.
  • Thu Jan 13:  S. made shakes. Have 800 made. W. & T. dug ditch. T on the road. S. & T. went to Fidalgo Island staid all night at Mr. Bests house. [margin: Clear & pleasant.]
  • Fri Jan 14:  S. & T. went to Whites and then over to Graham's deer hunting. W. done chores, was writing &c. [margin: Rain.]
  • Sat Jan 15:  S. & T. at Graham's. W. dug ditch.
  • Sun Jan 16:  S. & T. came over to Sully's and staid all night at White's.
  • Mon Jan 17:  T. & S. got home at noon. W. dug ditch in the AM. T. worked on road ditching in P.M. and W. & S. were on the Parish claim dyking.
  • Tue Jan 18:  W. & S. worked ditching. In the P.M. worked down on the Parish chain dyking. T. worked on the road. [margin: Mrs. & Miss Leamer came down with Highbarger. Snowed all day.]
  • Wed Jan 19:  S. made pin for fence. W. worked in shop making pins &c. T. oiled harness. Keany(?) was here; his boat was wrecked last night or rather the cargo lost.
  • Thu Jan 20:  W. & S. worked on the Parrish claim. T. worked on the road.
  • Fri Jan 21:  All worked on the Parrish claim dyking. Very cold & disagreeable weather for dyking.

1876 Jan 22-Feb 15

Image Id: 002-0249
Transcription
  • Sat Jan 22:  All worked on the Parrish claim dyking. Al Leamer came down and staid all night. Siegfred called on us in the evening. [margin: Mercury at 23°]
  • Sun Jan 23:  Mr. & Mrs. Leamer started for home this Morning. [margin: Mercury at 20, clear.]
  • Mon Jan 24:  S. cut wood & poles or sleepers for a house out on the McCollum claim. T. hauled timbers for the same with the horses. W. filed saw and cleared away for the house. [margin: Clear & cold]
  • Tue Jan 25:  W. started for Olympia. Trimble went to the station. S. sawed wood.
  • Wed Jan 26:  S. & T. worked in the shop making pins and oiling harness. Billy Wallbuff(?) came up to Trimbles. [margin: Snowed most all day. Billy & Kenneth are elk hunting.]
  • Thu Jan 27:  [diary date 26th] S. had sore thum(?) went up to Trimbles in the morning and swaed wood most of the day. T. cut wood. cold.
  • Fri Jan 28:  [diary date 27th] S. sawed wood. T. oiled harness in the AM and cu wood in the P.M. [margin: cold & blustering Snowy]
  • Sat Jan 29:  [dairy date 28th] S. cut barn timber in the fore noon and split wood in the P.M. T. hauled wood in the A.M. and oiled harness in the P.M. [margin: Rain 1/2 day.]
  • Sun Jan 30:  Windy & Rainy. Staid at home all day. Trimble & hist company called on us in the eve.
  • Mon Jan 31:  Trimble went to the station today. S. & T. cut barn timber. [margin: warm & pleasant.]
  • Tue Feb 01:  s. & T. cut barn timber in the A.M. and worked on the road ditching in the P.M. Whitney came home from Olympia with bad cold. [margin: Strong went wind.]
  • Wed Feb 02:  All worked on the Parrish claim ditching and ome on this side ditching.
  • Thu Feb 03:  S. split wood. T. oiled & washed harness. W. worked at his account in the house. [margin: Rainy day.]
  • Fri Feb 04:  Split wood, worked in the ship. oiled harness. all worked on the Parrish claim in the P.M. [margin: Rain in the A.M.]
  • Sat Feb 05:  Split wood. worked in the road. Worked on the Parrish claim dyking in the P.M.
  • Sun Feb 06:  S. called on Trimble. T. on Ball. The rest staid at home. D'Arcy's folks took dinner with us.
  • Mon Feb 07:  W. & S. made fence on Ball's line in the AM & T dug ditch by the preemptive house. W. filed saw in the P.M. S. & T. split and sawed wood. [margin: Rain in the P.M.]
  • Tue Feb 08:  W. helped filed saws, Guss work. W. & S. worked in the timber sawing shake & shingles, cutting road &c. T. ditch in the forenoon and on road in P.M. Ball's dyke broke again [margin: High tide.]
  • Wed Feb 09: 
  • Thu Feb 10:  Dito yesterday.
  • Fri Feb 11:  W. & S. cutting poles for a wood shed and making pickets. T. hauling them out.
  • Sat Feb 12:  W. & S. worked on the wood shed and house foundation on the McCollum claim. T. hauling stuff with the horses.
  • Sun Feb 13:  Guss went down to Mr. Ball's horse back. the rest staid at home. [margin:Rainy.]
  • Mon Feb 14:  T. hauling mangolds to the barn. W. & S. graded sloughs and dyked on the Parrish claim. [margin: warm & windy.]
  • Tue Feb 15:  S. went to the station and spaded mud in the dyke. W. & T. dyked and dug ditch north of the preemptive house. worked on wood shed a while in the morn.

  • Mon Feb 28:  S. was at the station. W. & T. dug ditch & made fence on Ball's line.

  • Thu Mar 02:  All dyked on the Parrish claim. Tide over the marsh in the morning. [margin: Hard rain last night.]
  • Fri Mar 03:  Dyking on the Parrish claim some, rain. came up and made shingles until night.
  • Sat Mar 04:  All dyking on Parrish claim.
  • Sun Mar 05:  All staid at home. very windy.

  • Tue Mar 07:  Ball's dyke broke again this morning. Worked on the Parrish claim part of the day. [margin: Very high tide. Rainy]
  • Wed Mar 08:  All dyked on the Parrish claim. [Guss went to D'Arcys.]
  • Thu Mar 09:  S. & W. dyked. T. hauled up manure and fixed for a hot bed. Clear & cold. [margin: Hard frost.]
  • Fri Mar 10:  All dyked on the Parrish claim as usual. Clear & pleasant. North Wind. [margin: mercury at 28°]
  • Sat Mar 11:  Went down and staked out the canal in the "Gorman claim". W. & T. dyked in the P.M. on canal. S. made some gates. Got ready to go to LaConner but did not go.
  • Sun Mar 12:  W. & S. went to Ball's saw Smith. T. & Mrs. Leamer were at home all day. Guss was at LaConner. [margin: windy]
  • Mon Mar 13:  S. went to LaConner. Took Smith & Mrs. Leamer. T. hauled lumber &c. with horses. W. staid with the children.
  • Tue Mar 14:  T. hauled timber for frame & wood. W. & S. repaired dyke in the P.M. between big sloughs. Snow in the evening. [margin: S. & Guss came from the station in the A.M.]

1876 Mar 15-Apr 10

Image Id: 002-0251
Transcription
  • Wed Mar 15:  S. worked in the shop making oars. W. & T. worked around the house and split wood &c. Repaired dyke in the P.M. [margin: Rain in AM]
  • Thu Mar 16:  All worked on the canal on Gorman claim. [margin: pleasant]
  • Fri Mar 17:  Snow 8 incheds deep and still snowing. 8 A.M. S. worked at the oars and made shingles. W. & T. split & sawed wood. [margin: Snow all day.]
  • Sat Mar 18:  Went down on the Gorman claim dyking.
  • Sun Mar 19:  Rain all day. T. went up to Ball's in P.M.
  • Mon Mar 20:  All worked down on the Canal.
  • Tue Mar 21:  S. & T. worked on the canal. W. & wife went to the station.
  • Wed Mar 22:  Made shingles & topped out dyke in the A.M. [margin: Rain in A.M.]
  • Thu Mar 23:  All worked on canal. Rainy towards night. Billie(?) was here to breakfast. [margin: Hot bed doing well.]
  • Fri Mar 24:  A pleasant day. W. & S. worked on the canal. T. worked around the house hauling posts(?) &c.
  • Sat Mar 25:  All worked on the canal. Mr. Wallace, Miss Leamer & Miss Bowden came up and staid all night. [margin: Rain last night.]
  • Sun Mar 26:  Wallace & Ida went home in the P.M. Miss Bowden staid all night. Quite pleasant. [margin: Snow & rain last night.]
  • Mon Mar 27:  All worked on the canal. Windy & rainy part of the day. D'Arcy worked on canal.
  • Tue Mar 28:  All worked on canal. Ball went to station. [margin: Very windy.]
  • Wed Mar 29:  Heavy gale this morning with rain & hail. Highbarger worked on canal in P.M. [margin: Tide over the marsh.]
  • Thu Mar 30:  All worked on the canal. Pleasant in the P.M. Highbarger worked all day. [margin: Sullivan was here.]
  • Fri Mar 31:  All worked on Canal. Highbarger with the rest. Took our 26 rods. Ground quite dry but prospect of rain. Highbarger worked.
  • Sat Apr 01:  Clowdy rainy morning. All worked on the canal as usual. Clear in the afternoon. Highbarger also.
  • Sun Apr 02:  Pleasant day. T. S. & Miss Bowden went to Fidalgo. got home at 11 oclock. S. has lame back.
  • Mon Apr 03:  highbarger on canal. Whitney went to the station in P.M. Miss Bowden went with hime. T. worked on canal. W. worked around the house & garde in A.M. S. had lame back. [margin: Pleasant with north wind.]
  • Tue Apr 04:  W. at the station. T. worked on the bridge across Allen slough. W. pruned trees and grafted a few. Jake worked in canal.
  • Wed Apr 05:  W. at the station. Came home at 4 oclcok. S. planted some peas and lettuce. and transplanted the hot bed, T. had a runaway with Roni & Orcam(?) & sled. Then went to harrowing. Highbarger worked on Canal.
  • Thu Apr 06:  W. & T. & Jake graded sloughs. S. plowed sod with "Lona & George". Too soft to walk in the forenoon.
  • Fri Apr 07:  T. & S. plowed. Highbarger & W. graded sloughs.
  • Sat Apr 08:  S. graded sloughs and made a set of frohissbtus(?). The rest worked on the canal. Cold S.E. wind. [margin: Jake worked.]
  • Sun Apr 09:  S. went to Catholic church and up to Pleasant Ridge. The rest staid at home. [margin: Palm Sunday.]
  • Mon Apr 10:  S. at LaConner. The rest worked on canal. Pleasant day. All the farmers plowing around LaConner. [margin: Jake worked. ]

1876 Apr 11-May 1

Image Id: 002-0252
Transcription
  • Tue Apr 11:  W. worked on the canal and finished it except shoveling it out. S. plowed and T. also Highbarger went to the station.
  • Wed Apr 12:  Plowed & graded sloughs. Highbarger worked in the blacksmith shop. Whitney also.
  • Thu Apr 13:  Plowed & graded sloughs. Highbarger harrowed with Dolly & Leline. T. began plowing across Cultis slough. Highbarger began his [r margin: work by the month at noon today. He is to have work every day that it does not rain till Nov 1st at $50.00 per month. We are to plow and harrow an acre of his land for him.] [margin: Rain in A.M.]
  • Fri Apr 14:  Highbarger began harrowing with four horse abreast. S. & T. plowed.
  • Sat Apr 15:  W. went down to Welchins & then over to the "Perry" and transplanted the hotbed in the P.M. Jake grade sloughs. T. plowed & graded sloughs. S. plowed. [margin: Not very drying weather. Went to help get the "Perry" in, at night.]
  • Sun Apr 16:  Got the schooner in this morning & unloaded. T & Jake helped. Got through at 10 P.M.
  • Mon Apr 17:  S. went after the Fanning mill in morning. W. & S. cleaned up and sacked the seed oats we got of Ball. T. hauled mangolds from the lower barn. Jake worked in the shop and graded sloughs in P.M. [margin: Dolly had a colt this morning.]
  • Tue Apr 18:  T. hauled the oats out to the slough. Jake & S. went after them with scow. Graded sloughs and piled lumber in P.M.
  • Wed Apr 19:  Brought the scow around to the ware house easly this morning. Rainy in morning. S. sowed 8 sacks of oats. Jake harrowed. W. piled lumber in A.M., doctored horse in P.M. T. planted some vegetables on the marsh. Allen was here all night. S. plowed in P.M. [margin: Dolly sick. S. & T. went to party in eve at D'Arcy's (8 sks oats.)]
  • Thu Apr 20:  S. plowed. Jake harrowed. W. doctored horse & piled lumber. T. graded sloughs.
  • Fri Apr 21:  S. plowed. Jake harrowed in oats. W. & T. piled lumber. Trimble helped piled lumber a little. Strawberries beginning to blossom. [margin: sowed 6 sacks of oats.]
  • Sat Apr 22:  Sowed 4 sacks of oats. Jake harrowed them in. W. & T. graded sloughs. S. plowed with one team. [margin: Rain last night. very windy.]
  • Sun Apr 23:  Sullivan came after the drill. S. went to LaConner. Miss Bowdin also.
  • Mon Apr 24:  Jake harrowed. T. plowed. S. got home from the station at 2:30 P.M. via Marches. Trimble went to the station. [margin: Rainy in P.M. 16 Pigs(?)]
  • Tue Apr 25:  S. owed seven sacks of oats. Jake harrowed. Rainy in A.M. T. & W. worked around the house and put in a bridge by the cow yard.
  • Wed Apr 26:  S. made shingles. Jake worked in Shop in A.M. at home in P.M. T. made a cultivator. Graded sloughs in P.M. and cleaned out ditch. [margin: Rain in A.M.]
  • Thu Apr 27:  W. graded sloughs. S. & T. plowed. [margin: Jake worked at home.]
  • Fri Apr 28:  Pleasant day. S. hauled posts in the A.M. and plowed in P.M. T. plowed all day. W. fixed well made a gate and graded. sloughs. Jake harrowed. Dolly worked for the first time here since having colt.
  • Sat Apr 29:  Jake worked at home. W. went to Fidalgo Island. Land very wet. S. & T. graded sloughs. Clowdy. [margin: Heavy rain last night.]
  • Sun Apr 30:  Cold & clowdy. W. & wife at Fidalgo. S. & T. at home. Sam'l Calhoun, Wallace & Miss Bowdin were here to dinner. W. came home in the eve.
  • Mon May 01:  S. plowed Jakes Marsh garden. Jake harrowed. T. & W. plowed up land garden and planted potatos, &c. Sowed 2 1/2 acres of oats. [margin: 9 Berkshire pigs.]

1876 May 2-20

Image Id: 002-0253
Transcription

  • Wed May 03:  Sowed 6 sacks of oats. Jake harrowed in oats. S. & T. plowed. W. made a flat boat and drained the lakes on the warehouse lot.
  • Thu May 04:  Rainy in the A.M. Jake worked in shop in the fore noon and for himself in the afternoon. W. worked in the house. S. worked in the house some and graded sloughs in the P.M. T. planted potatos. Worked on harrow some.
  • Fri May 05:  T. went to the station. Jake worked at home. S. & W. worked on bridges across Cultis slo. Mrs. Hyde & Ida came down this evening. [margin: Strawberries in full bloom.]

  • Sun May 07:  A volunteer oats have grown 1 1/4 in per day this week. Timothy in marsh garden stand from 1 ft to 18 inches. Rye over two feet. Winter wheat and volunteer oats mixed n. part of house- 1 ft -18 in. Orchard grass in upland garden 3 ft high. Apple trees in Bloom. Tomato plants budding- [margin: S. & Guss went to the station with Mrs. Hyde & Ida. Mrs. Smith came from Whatcom.]
  • Mon May 08:  Highbarger worked at home. W. & T. plowed. S. at the station. Pleasant. Trimble had all the ponies to plow his garden.

  • Wed May 10:  W. graded sloughs & cleaned grain. Highbarger harrowedin the field by the ware house.
  • Thu May 11:  S. plowed. T. plowed in the marsh garden. Jake harrowed in oats. Fine weather. W. wokred in the garden hoeing, &c. [margin: Sowed 15 bushels black oats.]

  • Sun May 14:  All staid at home. Mr White's folks here. Mrs. Smith & Ollie called in the P.M. Pleasant.
  • Mon May 15:  Trimble helped corduroy road 3 or 4 hours. Mr White's folks went home this morning. Jake worked at home. S. T. harrowed. W. hauled posts. with Celina & Brown(?). [margin: Cold & showery sowed 8 bushels white oats. Sowed 19 bushels B. oats.]
  • Tue May 16:  S. & T. harrowed in oats. Land very soft. Jake worked at home. Mr Best & Mrs Jenkins came over.
  • Wed May 17:  W. went to the station. Guss & Mrs. Smith went with him. T. plowed. Jake harrowed.
  • Thu May 18:  Jake harrowed. T. plowed. Whiteny made bridges across Cultis slough & on Allen line. S. sowed grain & hauled rails & posts. [margin: Sowed 30 bushs oats. Trimble making fence.]
  • Fri May 19:  Jake harrowing on the Allen "80" W. worked in garden & set out 73 tomatos. T. plowing. S. finished hauling rails & posts.
  • Sat May 20:  Jake harrowing on Allen "80". S. harrowed the piece in front of the house. W. planted garden. T. planted garden in A.M. and plowed in P.M. Planted potatos, parsnips, carrots, beets, corn &c. [margin: Sowed 6 bushels oats.]

1876 May 21-Jun 8

Image Id: 002-0254
Transcription
  • Sun May 21:  S. went to Pleasant Ridge. The rest staid at home.
  • Mon May 22:  W. harrowed. Jake & T plowed. S. got home and started for Whatcom via Fidalgo.
  • Tue May 23:  W. went to the station. Jake & T. plowed & harrowed. S. at Whatcom.

  • Fri May 26:  Jake harrowed & began rolling grain with the ponies. T. & S. plowed. W. made gates & graded sloughs. [margin: Red clover in bloom. Rain last night.]
  • Sat May 27:  Jake rolled grain. S. & T. plowed. W. graded sloughs. and helped Trimble fix up the fence. Drove Ball's cattle off from the marsh. Mr. Bests folks and Mr Griffin came over this eve. [margin: Jim MacDonald borrowed the wheel barrows.]
  • Sun May 28:  Pleasant day. Andrews & wife & Mr Allen came up to day. Mrs Bests folks went home this eve. Dave was sick.
  • Mon May 29:  S. & T. plowed. W. graded sloughs and sowed grain. Rain some. Jake harrowed.
  • Tue May 30:  S. went down and sowed oats till ten A.M. T. finished plowing the land by the Preemption house. Jake harrowed in P.M. plowed on the Allen 80 in the A.M. S. & W. ent to the station in the eve. via Stearns boat slough. Mrs. Dodge went with us. [margin: Rain most all last night. Sowed 23 sacks oats.]
  • Wed May 31:  Jake & Trimble harrowed with two teams 7 hours. W. S. & Co. were at Pleasant Ridge to tend the wedding. Edgar & Ida married.
  • Thu Jun 01:  Jake & Trimble finished harrowing the land by the Preemption house. T. worked at gardening. S. & W. got home at 5 P.M.
  • Fri Jun 02:  Very pleasant day. S. & T. plowed on the Allen "80". Jake and W. graded sloughs on the same. Rain in the eve. [margin: Trimble rolled with the ponies.]
  • Sat Jun 03:  Plowed, graded sloughs &c. on the Allen 80. Jake worked. T. set out cabbage in the evening. [margin: Trimble rolled.]
  • Sun Jun 04:  All staid at home. Clarence & Arthur came over and Mr Ball's folks cam over in the P.M. Winter oats & wheat are heading fast. [margin: Plum trees had made a growth of 17 inches.]
  • Mon Jun 05:  Trimble rolling as usual. Highbarger plowed and harrowed. T. plowed and set cabbage. S. dug ditch for slap drain and sowed oats. W. was getting ready to leave the country. [margin: Sowed 18 bushels oats.]
  • Tue Jun 06:  Trimble worked 3 hours. Jake harrowed some and worked in the blacksmith shop. S. & T. butchered a yearling bull and worked about the house helping Whitney pack up etc. Whitney & wife with their boys bid good bye to Bay View & started for S. California. Whitney & Tillinghast agreed to give Sisson deed for 100 acres on demand. [margin: S. & Ida went to station.]
  • Wed Jun 07:  Trimble worked 1/2 day rolling. Tillinghast plowed all day. Highbarger harrowed in A.M. plowed in P.M. Took 2 hours morning in account of rain. Disagreeble wet day. S did not get back. [margin: S & I went up to Pleasant Ridge after things. Rainy day.]
  • Thu Jun 08:  Till & H plowed till 3 OK. then harrowed & J gardening. Trimble rolled S. & wife got back in the eve.

1876 Jun 9-30

Image Id: 002-0255
Transcription
  • Fri Jun 09:  Trimble & jake did not work. T. hauled oats & timber in A.M. and harrowed in oats with three horses in P.M. S. made mosquito bars, dug ditch & sowed oats. [margin: Rainy hard last night. Sowed 12 bu oats.]
