1873 Dec 16-Jan 6, 1874

Image Id: 002-0209
Transcription
  • Tue Dec 16:  Put the floor on the porch. Made an axhandle and some butter(?) handles.
  • Wed Dec 17:  W. & S. went down to work on the slough. It was not thawed enough. Put a door latch on the shop. S. made shakes some for wood house. [margin: W. made extra wagon box]
  • Thu Dec 18:  W. S. & the boys worked on the sloughs. Wheeling mud fixing wings &c. Very pleasant day. T. hauled water(?) on the wagon.
  • Fri Dec 19:  Billy brought our mail for this week to day. Cold south east wind. S. made shakes. W. worked on the dam. T. hauled over 50 bushs of beets.
  • Sat Dec 20:  Sisson made shakes in the fore noon. W. began the wood shed and settled up with Charley & Harvy. After dinner W. & S. made a table. T. hauled two loads of beets. [margin: rain 2 hours]
  • Sun Dec 21:  S. went up to McNutt's. Everything quiet along the line. "Charley Frenchmen" brought us some venison. [margin: ? 18 ins over the marsh]
  • Mon Dec 22:  S. made pickets in the fore noon. After dinner W. & S. repaired dyke and worked on the dams. T. hauled beets. [margin: ? dito yesterday]
  • Tue Dec 23:  Vinz hauled beets as usual. W. & S. wheeled mud a little and then split shakes (340)[margin: highest tide of the season]
  • Wed Dec 24:  All down choping mud on the dam at midnight last night. Vinz hauled one load of beets. W. went down and took the cloth off from the white house. S. went to highbarger and got crowbar mended then went after a stray lumber raft. Cleaned some and put ceiling in the bedroom.
  • Thu Dec 25:  This is Christmas in some places. W. fired trees, filed saws, &c. T. & S. went down to McNutt's to get timber fo an ox yoke. "never done it" Very pleasant.
  • Fri Dec 26:  T. & S. worked on the lagoon wheeling mud. W. & wife went to Sickfreds visiting.
  • Sat Dec 27:  All worked down by the dyke deepening ditches till 3 P.M. Then it began to rain. [margin: Rain 3 hours]
  • Sun Dec 28:  Rained most all day; nothing else worth recording.
  • Mon Dec 29:  All worked on the dyke ditch to day.
  • Tue Dec 30:  Trimble went to the station. Worked on the ditch until it rained. [margin: Rain 3hs]
  • Wed Dec 31:  Worked on ditch a little while in the morn. Worked on the shop some and made ax handles. Sawed wood in the afternoon. [margin: Rained 3 hs]
  • Thu Jan 01:  Worked in the show some on sitting room table. Sawed wood some.
  • Fri Jan 02:  Worked in the shop in the fore noon on bookcase. Afternoon W. S. sawed wood. T. worked in ditch. [margin: Snow & rain 5 hs]
  • Sat Jan 03:  T. & S. sawed wood in fore noon. After noon all worked in the shop & house. Commenced to put cloth on "boys" room. [margin: Rain Snow 4 hs]
  • Sun Jan 04:  Snow went off today clowdy rainy day.
  • Mon Jan 05:  T. & S. worked on the ditch side of the dyke. W. helped wash, filed saw, &c. Very pleasant.
  • Tue Jan 06:  W. & S. went to the station. T. worked in the shop. Received some garden seeds from the Depot. [margin: Rain 5hs High tide]

1874 Jan 7-23

Image Id: 002-0210
Transcription
  • Wed Jan 07:  W & S worked in the shop on bookcase. Afternoon sawed rail cuts. Finish clothes bars and put cloth on sleeping room.
  • Thu Jan 08:  W. & S. went to the "Bob Williams" place and at Siegfreds for lumber which had gone astray. Piled it on the marsh and staked it out. T. gathered turnips and work in the shop. Worked on the ditch some.
  • Fri Jan 09:  W. & S. worked on the ditch most all day. T. hauled a load of beets Turnips and went to Highbargers with the bellcow. [margin: Rain 3hs.]
  • Sat Jan 10:  T hauled a load of beets in the forenoon. and one in the afternoon. W. & S. worked on the ditch and fixed the apron to the large box in the afternoon. The tide was out low last night.
  • Sun Jan 11:  S. went out to the French settlement.
  • Mon Jan 12:  T. worked in the garden digging ditch. W. & S. worked in the shop in the fore noon and on the dyke after dinner. [margin: Rain 2hs]
  • Tue Jan 13:  W. went to the station. T dug ditch in the garden & S. on the marsh. Snowed slightly most all day.
  • Wed Jan 14:  T hauled up wood with the oxen. W & S dug ditch on the marsh. Drained some lakes on the "White" claim.
  • Thu Jan 15:  Rained so we did not go to the ditch. Work on the wood shed and in the shop on bookcase. Heard some robins.
  • Fri Jan 16:  Worked in the shop some and split rails some. S. went up to McNutts to see him. Sick of very high tides.
  • Sat Jan 17:  Highest tide of the season so far. and north wind which scattered the lumber all over the marsh. T dug ditch in the forenoon. W. & S. worked on the bookcase. After dinner W. worked about the house. T. & S. went after a scow load of stones and picked up scattering lumber. McNutts ox died this morning. All went down to the slough in the evening and put stones on the box apron.
  • Sun Jan 18:  Tide nearly three feet over the marsh this morning. McClellan's dyke, Siegfried's dike and the two largest sloughs went out and our farm is covered with salt water. Lumber lost and scow also, etc. etc. Spent the day looking after lost lumber.
  • Mon Jan 19:  W. went to Ben Welches after the boat. T. & S. went to McClellan's and got hist boat and picked up lumber in the fore noon. W. & S. cut wood in the after noon. T. dug ditch in the garden.
  • Tue Jan 20:  Worked in the shop. T dug ditch. S. went to McNutt's after the mail at night.
  • Wed Jan 21:  T. dug ditch & W. & S. worked on the secretary & bookcase. Very cold, ground covered with snow.
  • Thu Jan 22:  Mercury down to 17 this morning. Clear & cold all day. W. & S. worked in the shop as usual. T. put mop boards in the house.
  • Fri Jan 23:  Still cold stormy weather. Worked in the shop. Put the doors on the bookcase. Heard that the Indians had the scow at Samish Island.

1874 Jan 24-Feb 12

Image Id: 002-0211
Transcription
  • Sat Jan 24:  Started to go after the scow but could not get the boat out of the slough for ice. W. & S. worked in the shop on secretary. T. dug ditch.
  • Sun Jan 25:  The Indians brought the scow home and charged $1500 for "mamock". Rainy & cloudy.
  • Mon Jan 26:  Very warm and fine weather for working out of doors. Got the wood shed ready for putting on shakes. T. dug ditch.
  • Tue Jan 27:  S. & Guss went to the station. W. worked around the house and took care of the baby. T. dug ditch &c.
  • Wed Jan 28:  S. went down to the slough and made a dam in the dyke ditch by the stairs. After noon W. & S. worked on the wood shed putting on shakes. T. dug ditch.
  • Thu Jan 29:  All worked in the shop all day on secretary &c. T. took care of the seeds. [margin: Rain 8hs]
  • Fri Jan 30:  Commenced fill drains with cedar. After nooon W. & S. worked on wood shed. Finishing cornering and commenced weather boards. Tides not very high. [margin: Nice weather for work.]
  • Sat Jan 31:  T. filled ditch. W. & S. finished the wood shed and cut wood.
  • Sun Feb 01:  Clowdy dismal day. Nothing occured appropriate for this book.
  • Mon Feb 02:  T. "made" ditches as usual W. & S. made rails.
  • Tue Feb 03:  S. dug ditch on the S.E. corner orchard. W. & S. made rails. Trimble came home. Warm & pleasant. [margin: have about 1600 rails.]
  • Wed Feb 04:  T. cut wood in the shed W. worked on secretary. S. made axhandles, bretle(?) &c. [margin: Rain all day.]
  • Thu Feb 05:  T. cut wood in shed and made an ax handle. W. & S. fired some trees. Worked in shop on Bureau, made shingles &c. Clowdy dull weather. [margin: Rain 8hs]
  • Fri Feb 06:  S. made rails & burned down trees. T. dug ditch. W. was down on the marsh putting in ditch dams, &c. [margin: Rain 2hs]
  • Sat Feb 07:  T. dug ditch and made a dam on the marsh by the stairs. W. & S. made rails and worked in the shop. W. filed the cross cut saw and worked on Bureau. [margin: Rain 3 hs. 2100 rails.]
  • Sun Feb 08:  Mrs Jennings & Ovvie(?) came down to dinner. Rained part of the day.
  • Mon Feb 09:  Very pleasant to work. T. dug ditch as usual. W. & S. cleared a way for the ditch and sawed rail cuts. Fell three large trees.
  • Tue Feb 10:  Whitney & his wife went to the station. T. worked on the ditch and S. made rails in the fore noon and chopped fir trees in the afternoon.
  • Wed Feb 11:  T. dug ditch some. S. cut fir trees & fired a fir log. [margin: Snowed 3hs]
  • Thu Feb 12:  Whitney's folks came back from the station to day at noon. Froze quite hard last night. T. worked at the ditch. S. cut wood. Afternoon W. & S. sawed rail cuts.

1874 Feb 13-Mar 1

Image Id: 002-0212
Transcription
  • Fri Feb 13:  T. worked on ditch as usual. W. & S. piled & burned brush on the orchard site. Pleasant day.
  • Sat Feb 14:  This is Valentine's day. We did not get many. Occupation dito yesterday. Tides have been low so far this month. [margin: 2300 rails up to date.]
  • Sun Feb 15:  Rain in the fore noon. Trimble, Jake & his family took dinner with us.
  • Mon Feb 16:  S. went down to the Scow, fixed the oars and some poles for her. Made rails some. T. worked o ditch. [margin: Rain 3hs]
  • Tue Feb 17:  Jake & mcNutt went to the station with scow after Smith's cattle. Rain in the fore noon. W. & S. made rails in the afternoon. T. worked on the ditch and cut wood shed. [margin: Rain 4 hours. 2800 rails. T. planted some tomatos.]
  • Wed Feb 18:  In the fore noon W. & S. went out on the marsh road and sawed some cedar for ditches. After dinner split rails. Vinz worked on ditch by the orchard bars. Smith & Highbarger arrived this morn with the scow.
  • Thu Feb 19:  Smith came down after the oxen this morning. He said the scow leaked badly. T. cut wood and dug ditch. W. & S. made rails some and worked on the bureau. S. put the apple and pear seed out to freeze. Hard west wind at night. [margin: Rain 5 hs. 3300 rails.]
  • Fri Feb 20:  T. worked on ditch as usual. W. & S. split rails and some wood. Mr. Smith brought the cattle home.
  • Sat Feb 21:  Very pleasant day. Split rails, cut wood and dug ditch. Mr. Butters came along and took dinner with us. [margin: 4000 rails.]
  • Sun Feb 22:  Clear & pleasant. had no company, made no visits. S. went down to the dains at night.
  • Mon Feb 23:  Another fine day. Vinz worked on ditch. W. & S. made rails on the orchard in the fore noon, after noon lost time on a large fir tree.
  • Tue Feb 24:  Worked in the shop in the fore noon on Bureau. made axhandle, split wood &c. Afternoon T. worked on the ditch. W. files the saw & S. burned up fir tops. Tide was out very low in the afternoon now. [margin: Snow 5hs]
  • Wed Feb 25:  Clowdy cool day. W. & S. made 900 stakes out of cedar. T. finished the garden ditch.
  • Thu Feb 26:  Very pleasant day to work out of doors. Worked in the shop and in the house. Stopped up mouse holes and put up ceiling &c. Messrs White & Son & Bicker of Fidalgo called on us also Highbarger. [margin: 51.00 ots(?) Rain in the morn.]
  • Fri Feb 27:  W. & S. made rails in the fore noon. T. hauled the cedar for under drains. Afternoon split and put it in. Mr O'Laughlin's folks were here and staid all night.
  • Sat Feb 28:  S. made a pair of oars of Firs. The rest plit wood and in the afternoon all worked on the ditch filling. [margin: 4hs]
  • Sun Mar 01:  Clowdy day. Tide over the marsh early. Cleared off in the afternoon. Mr. Highbarger call on us.

1874 Mar 2-18

Image Id: 002-0213
Transcription
  • Mon Mar 02:  T. worked on the ox sled. W. & S. worked in shop making picture frames. W. went down to the Pre-emption house for timothy seed. [margin: Snow all day.]
  • Tue Mar 03:  W. & T. went to the station. Highbarger moved today. S. worked in the shop and fixed the stair way. Smith brought the cattle home.
  • Wed Mar 04:  T. hauled wood and drags. W. & S. cut wood all day. fir & willow.
  • Thu Mar 05:  Worked under the shed in the fore noon cutting wood, making sled, &c. After-noon T. dug ditch & the rest cut wood. Quite cool and will freeze tonight. The cattle came up to day for first time in several days. [margin: Snow 4hs.]
  • Fri Mar 06:  W. & S. worked in the shop in the fore noon making picture frames, afternoon cut wood. S. borrowed Trimbles bead plain. T. worked on sled and ditch. [margin: stormy 3 hours]
  • Sat Mar 07:  Worked around the house fixing stuff, drain &c. grading the ground about the house. Afternoon W. made rails and S. sharpened pickets.
  • Sun Mar 08:  Froze quite hard last night Tides low now days. All quiet along the line. [margin: 5600 rails]
  • Mon Mar 09:  Stormy in the morning. Worked in the shop a while and then went to work on the fence. W. made rails in the fore noon. T. dug post holes and S. sharpened pickets.
  • Tue Mar 10:  Clear pleasant day. all worked at the picket fence. Billin brought up the mail.
  • Wed Mar 11:  W. sowed grass seed. Red top clover, timothy, &c. T. & S. worked on the fence, are getting along first rate. [margin: Heard first frog of the season.]
  • Thu Mar 12:  Rained very hard last night and some in the morning. W. went down & seeded the dyke. T. hauled logs off from the orchard site. S. went and made a start on cow oars. Worked in the shop in the afternoon.
  • Fri Mar 13:  T. went to McNutt's to get ox yoke. W. & S. worked on the orchard fence and filled ditch some.
  • Sat Mar 14:  T. worked on his sled. S. finished the scow oars. W. made a closet table. Mr. Andrews was up to dinner. Allen & Highbarger were also up went to Trimbles & had a dyke meeting in the evening. Allen staid all night with us. Concluded to go and fix the dams as soon as practable. [margin: Rained 8 hours]
  • Sun Mar 15:  Pleasant day. S. went up to Smiths, Guss went down to Siegfrieds. Andrews, Anderson & Matson were here. Matson staid all night.
  • Mon Mar 16:  Clear & pleasant. Finished fencing the orchard.
  • Tue Mar 17:  W. & wife went to the station. T. & S. worked in the garden plowing, covering ditches &c. Hard frost this morning. Saw first mosquitos.
  • Wed Mar 18:  [diary says 17th]Hard frost this morning. Clear day. Worked in garden as yesterday. W. came home to night. Got some plants from Dodge. [margin: Planted early Wyman cabbage]

1874 Mar 19-Apr 3

Image Id: 002-0214
Transcription
  • Thu Mar 19:  [dairy says 18th] Staked off the ground for orchard. Siegfried's folks were up here. T. hauled manure on the berry patch. W. sowed blue grass on the dyke. S. filled ditch & cut wood. [margin: Rain 3 hours]
  • Fri Mar 20:  [diary says 19th] T. hauled manure and dragged the garden. W. & S. moved the scow and set pear trees. Took up the currant bush from the old log house.
  • Sat Mar 21:  [diary says 20th] Very fine day. Set cherry trees, currant bush. Gooseberry, Raspberry, &c. &c. T. hauled rails and tried the breaking plow. S. calked the scow and planted plum stones. [margin: Heard plenty of frogs.]
  • Sun Mar 22:  Very fine day. McNutt, Anderson & D'Arcy were here to dinner.
  • Mon Mar 23:  Vinz went out with the scow and the oxen down to plow D'Arcy's land. Fine spring weather. W. & S. went up to Anderson's to help him put up a log house Got home at 5 oclock. Planted the apple & pear seed.
  • Tue Mar 24:  W. went to the station. got home at 11 oc. S. grafted some of the roots, &c. Afternoon set out grafts. Fixed strawberry ground and planted some peas. Pruned the old trees. Very pleasant and warm.
  • Wed Mar 25:  Made garden some today. set out the plum trees, apricot, quince &c. Planted potatos, onions, beets, turnips, radishes &c. put out currant cutting & set out mangolds & turnips for seed. Fine weather, low tides.
  • Thu Mar 26:  Worked in the house some. Rain a little in the morning. Set out the strawberries and some more apple grafts. Finished setting out the Apple trees. [margin: Rain 2hs]
  • Fri Mar 27:  Very fine day. Made a cold frame for tomatos and split wood. In the afternoon went down and commenced ditching the beet patch.
  • Sat Mar 28:  Tillinghast and Darcy came home with the scow and cattle this morning in time for breakfast. Clear & pleasant. All worked on the marsh ditching. [W. & S. dug 27 rods of ditch in 8 1/2 hours]
  • Sun Mar 29:  Verey pleasant day. S. had a bad cold. Mr. Smith came down to dinner.
  • Mon Mar 30:  W. & T. worked on the ditch in the morning. After dinner on the road down the bank. grading. S. sharpened fence stakes.
  • Tue Mar 31:  W. went to the station. His wife went to Mr. Darcy's visiting. T. hauled wood. S. sharpened stakes. Whitney's Artas(?) came from San Francisco.
  • Wed Apr 01:  S. sharpened stakes. T. hauled wood and W. worked on the road down the bank. Mosquitos quite plenty.
  • Thu Apr 02:  T. hauled wood. W. & S. laid up the rails around the calf pasture and then grubbed in potatos till night. All went to Darcy's to a party at night.
  • Fri Apr 03:  Got home from Darcy's at daylight. "bad omen". T. went and plowed garden for Trimble. W. & S. planted potatos and fixed the road down by the log house.

1874 Apr 4-20

Image Id: 002-0215
Transcription
  • Sat Apr 04:  Rained some in the night. Lettuce & Radishes are coming up.
  • Sun Apr 05:  Very pleasant day. Andrews & Mrs. Jennings were here to dinner.
  • Mon Apr 06:  Had a road meeting, elected T. for road master. W. & S. sided up the house. T, hauled manure. W. & T. went to the station in the evening.
  • Tue Apr 07:  T. hauled manure W. & T. got home from the station at 8 A.M. W. & S. worked on the house putting up cornice. W. dug ditch in the beet ground in the afternoon.
  • Wed Apr 08:  Mrs. Lemer & son came visiting today. S. mixed paint and made moulding. T. dug ditch and hauled stones from the garden. W. dug ditch. Trimble had the oxen to harrow his garden.
  • Thu Apr 09:  W. & T. plowed the garden. S. painted on the inside of the house.
  • Fri Apr 10:  Rained most all last night. S. painted the closet. W. worked around the house in the fore noon. W. & T. dug ditch in the afternoon.
  • Sat Apr 11:  W. & T. dug ditch down towards Trimble's corner. S. went out to saw cedar for the ditches. Went out to the Frenchmen's and saw some bear cubs.
  • Sun Apr 12:  Rained some in the fore noon. All went up to Trimble's to dinner The whole ridge was r???????d.
  • Mon Apr 13:  W. & S. worked in the house papering. T. dug ditch in the afternoon and chored in the fore noon. Trimble had the cattle to haul his things to the slough. Apples & plums appear to be a failure (seedlings). Peas are about two inches high. [margin: Rain two hours]
  • Tue Apr 14:  Whitney went to the station, Trimble & Co. went to the Samish, had out scow. T. dug ditch. S. painted & papered in the fore noon and sawed stuff for ditch in the afternoon. Strong S.E. wind all day.
  • Wed Apr 15:  S. painted sitting room and painted window sash in the afternoon. T. dug ditch and hauled rails also some stuff from Allen's. Whitney helped clear the room and then went to help the Frenchmen work on the bridge, also dug ditch in the afternoon.
  • Thu Apr 16:  S. painted sitting room second coat. T. dug ditch. W. put on wall paper.
  • Fri Apr 17:  t. dug ditch and helped put down carpet &c. W. papered the sitting room. S. finished up the stair way. Mr. Upson the Assessor was here and staid all night. Saw a strawberry in bloom.
  • Sat Apr 18:  W. & T. papered all day. bedroom & hall. S. finished up the hall and painted window sash. High west wind at night.
  • Sun Apr 19:  Very pleasant day. Mr. & Mrs. Smith, Mr McNutt, Mr Anderson and Mr Welcher called on us.
  • Mon Apr 20:  T. finished his sled and harrowed the garden and hauled a load of cedar for ditch. [margin: Trimble came back from the Samish. Had the cattle today.] [From next image] W. dug ditch on road. S. sawed cedar in the fore noon and dug ditch in the afternoon. Potatoes coming up. Transplanted tomatos.

1874 Apr 20-May 4

Image Id: 002-0216
Transcription
  • Tue Apr 21:  Trimble worked for us to day. Hauled ditch timber in the fore noon & plowed on the beet garden at Smith's. Planted the potatos. S. painted in the afternoon. T. cut pole for fence. [margin: Very pleasant weather.]
  • Wed Apr 22:  Trimble & T. worked on the garden plowing & fencing. W. planted potatos & peas. Phila Extra Early next to the road. S. fixed the seed sower & painted on the house.
  • Thu Apr 23:  W. T. & Trimble worked on the marsh garden finished harrowing & fencing. Planted potatos & beets. S. painted on the house. Newton was up after the cattle. Fine day.
  • Fri Apr 24:  S. split up the cedar for the ditches. Trimble & W. put it in. T. planted beets up in the Smith garden. Newton came up for the cattle. S. painted some in the after noon. [margin: Have 65 rods of covered ditch on the marsh.]
  • Sat Apr 25:  Rain a little in the morning. S. painted in the fore noon & made picket fence in the afternoon. Trimble & W. broke marsh all day with two yokes of cattle. T. filled underdrains on the marsh. [margin: The ferns is up thick one feet high.]
  • Sun Apr 26:  Very pleasant day. W. & wife went to Mr Smiths. [margin: Pear & strawberries in bloom.]
  • Mon Apr 27:  S. put lime on the apple trees. W. & T. broke marsh. T. dug ditch on road. S. cut ditch along the bank.
  • Tue Apr 28:  T. went to the station to day. W. & Trimble plowed in the fore noon. s. dug ditch. Split wood in the afternoon. [margin: Rain 5 hours]
  • Wed Apr 29:  Trimble hauled staked for fence. W. & S. drove stakes for 37 rods of fence. T. dug ditch. Rain a little just night.
  • Thu Apr 30:  W. & S. drove stakes in the fore noon. Afternoon W. dug ditch. S. made a pair of gates and sharpened fence stakes. T. dug ditch along the bank. Staked out a field for orchard garden &c. Rain last night.
  • Fri May 01:  W. worked at his again in the fore noon. T. dug ditch. After noon W. & T. hauled hay and worked on the road making bridge &c. S. sharpened stakes. Ballou & payne were here. Windy weather. Rain last night. [margin: Newton & Siegfred had the cattle.]
  • Sat May 02:  S. sharpened stakes in the fore noon. W. hoed in the garden. T. harrowed the beet patch. W. & S. planted beets in the afternoon.
  • Sun May 03:  Staid at home all day. Newton called in the evening. Fine weather.
  • Mon May 04:  W. & S. planted beets all day. T. harrowed till he finished then took the harrow home.

1874 May 5-19

Image Id: 002-0217
Transcription
  • Tue May 05:  S. sharpened stakes. W. & T. planted beets. Trimble hauled stakes. W. & T planted beets. Trimble hauled stakes. Rec'd a lot of seed fro Tillinghast Bros. also some potatos "Comptons surprise" & "Vermont extra early"
  • Wed May 06:  Vinz planted some squash, cucumbers, corn (adams early) & other vegetables. Trimble hauled stakes. W. finished planting the marsh garden. After noon W. & T. worked in the garden grubbing and setting out tomatos. Tomatos about 6 inches high. Potatos look fine. Sisson painted on the house. finished the first and began the sec. Mrs Jennings & Avvie took dinner with us.
  • Thu May 07:  S. painted and finished up the kitchen. T. split wood some. (E. ? S. Corn near home ???? in hill) W. & T. finished putting on stock & paper. Trimble began work but quit for rain. [margin: Rain 8 hours]
  • Fri May 08:  S. painted all day, about finished. Found "line back" & "cherry" with calves. W. & T. dug ditch by the front of the bank. Cool & clowdy. Not good growing weather.
  • Sat May 09:  W. & T. dug ditch down by the old log house. S. bushed the peas, planted "minnesota sweet corn" and howed in the garden. Gnats very thick. Char Matson was here to dinner. Offered him $50. per month (he board himself) to works on dams.
  • Sun May 10:  Warm & pleasant. Three ladies called on us in the evening. S. went to Smith's ranch. Timothy is looking fine also grain. Beets are coming up.
  • Mon May 11:  W. & T. dug ditch. S. sawed logs & drove stakes for fence. Clear & pleasant.
  • Tue May 12:  W. went to the station early. S. drove stakes. Trimble hauled stakes. T. dug ditch.
  • Wed May 13:  Trimble hauled a few stakes and in the afternoon some poles to bridge the lagoon. S. drove stakes. W. & T. dug ditches and worked on bridges.
  • Thu May 14:  Trimble did not work. Went Smith's. S. drove stakes and hung some gates. T. finished hauling stakes. W. hoed in the gardens some. After dinner W. & T. worked on the bridges and picked up lumber, moved the scow &c.
  • Fri May 15:  All got up at 3 A.M. and went to the Samish flats after lumber. Went over and saw the Samish river and Mr. Miller's place. W. was sick. Got the scow as far as Siegfreds and came home at 8 oclock.
  • Sat May 16:  T. worked on the road down by the lagoon. S. worked on fence and made a gate. W. worked in the garden & put cap rail on the fence. Went up to Siegfreds and run the scow of lumber home at night.
  • Sun May 17:  All but Sisson went to Dog Island. He went up to McNutts & Smith's. pleasant day.
  • Mon May 18:  Began work on the dams. Filled the hole in the lagoon and dug the box out of the big slough. The tide was so high we could not make a R.R. got home at 8:30 P.M. Matson began work. [margin: Tide 6 in over the marsh.]
  • Tue May 19:  All worked on the dam. put up a R.R. and began to put in wood. Finished the hole in the lagoon. Mrs. Allen & folks came at night & staid all night. [margin: High Tide. Fine rain in eve.]

1874 May 20-Jun 7

Image Id: 002-0218

  • Mon Nov 23:  W. spent the day writing(?) and S. cut wood. In the afternoon went down and unloaded the scow and took the water out. S. went around the dyke. T. hauled two loads of roots from the garden. [margin:Rainy all day. Tide 2 feet over marsh.]
  • Tue Nov 24:  W. & T. went to the Grange, but wheeled mud a little on the lagoon in the morning. S. wheeled mud topped out dyke all day. [margin: tide 18 in.]
  • Wed Nov 25:  Strong N.W. gale this morning from 6 A.M. till 8. and a hard surf on the dyke. Did not break but was damaged in several places. W. & T. came down from the station in the afternoon and we worked till night on the big dams. Put lumber and wheeling mud. McNutt helped in the afternoon. Ball and McClellan hauled a load of brush.

1874 Nov 27-Dec 17

Transcription
  • Fri Nov 27:  T. & S. started from the station with scow load of oats from Highbarger. Got down about noon. Whitney & Guss came home in boat. Unloaded the oats in the afternoon. [margin: rain 1/2 day]
  • Sat Nov 28:  T. hauled down a load of poles for the scow and took up the apples. In the afternoon hauled a load of parsnips. McNutt & Whitney worked on the dyke. S. worked on dyke in the fore noon and in afternoon fixed steps to the warehouse. [margin: tide on marsh 8 in.]
  • Sun Nov 29:  Very quite sunny Sunday. Nobody here. S. went up to Trimbles.
  • Mon Nov 30:  Worked in the shop cur wood and all worked on the dyke in the afternoon. The marsh is freshening fast now. [margin: Rain 4 hs]
  • Tue Dec 01:  Trimble & Baxter went to the station. W. & T. wheeled mud. S. & McNutt went up to his place and got a load of posts.
  • Wed Dec 02:  Worked on the dam driving posts and wheeling. W. worked on ?????l shelves(?) some. S. on picture frame. T. cut wood. Tides not very high. [margin: Rain 5 hs]
  • Thu Dec 03:  Worked on dams about all day.
  • Fri Dec 04:  W. & S. drove braces in the middle lagoon. T. wheeled on 1st lagoon. S. made batters & picture frame in fore noon. [margin: Rain 1/2 day]
  • Sat Dec 05:  Worked on dams all day making wings, driving braces & mud wheeling. [margin: Tides low]
  • Sun Dec 06:  Very pleasant day. Staid at home. Joke both dinner with us.
  • Mon Dec 07:  Worked on the dyke wheeling driving post &c.
  • Tue Dec 08:  All went to the station to attend Grange. Staid all night. [margin: Rain 3]
  • Wed Dec 09:  Got home at noon from LaConner. Fletcher was here to hire the scow. Worked oon the dyke in the afternoon wheeling & driving braces. Finished the latter. Warm & clowdy.
  • Thu Dec 10:  W. & T. worked on the dyke wheeling mud. S. dug potatos in the fore noon & wheeled mud in the afternoon. Finished digging potatos.
  • Fri Dec 11:  Whitney spent the day writing. T. cleaning seeds. S. worked in the shop making picture frame &c. Cut wood some towards night. [margin: Rain all day]
  • Sat Dec 12:  All topped out dyke all day.
  • Sun Dec 13:  Very quiet Sunday. All staid at home except Guss who went down to Mr. Balls in the afternoon.
  • Mon Dec 14:  Rainy some. W. helped wash in the fore noon. All topped out dyke. Finished up towards Siegfred's and fixed his dyke also. The ground is very wet. [margin: Rain 8 hours]
  • Tue Dec 15:  T. pulled beets. W & S. topped out dyke.
  • Wed Dec 16:  T. pulled beets. W. & S. topped out dyke. [margin: rain 3 hours]
  • Thu Dec 17:  W. did not work today had a lame hip. T. & S. burned the beets and dug ditch in the garden. Mr Ball's folks, McNutt & Allen called on us in the eve. [margin: Very pleasant.]

1874 Dec 18-Jan 6, 1875

Transcription
  • Fri Dec 18:  Mr. Allen staid all night here last night. Very pleasant day. W. staked out some ditches in the afternoon. T. & S. topped out dyke & worked on the garden ditch some. Highbarger came up last night. [margin: Fine weather]
  • Sat Dec 19:  Whitney did not work, was lame yet. T. & S. worked on the garden ditch. [margin: Pleasant day]
  • Sun Dec 20:  All stayed at home all day. Mr Best called on us in the afternoon.
  • Mon Dec 21:  S. dig ditch. The rest of the folks went to the Grange.
  • Tue Dec 22:  W. & Wife went to Whatcom today on the steamer. T brought the hog home from Calhouns. S. dug ditch.
  • Wed Dec 23:  T. & S. ditch down on the Siegfred's line.
  • Thu Dec 24:  T. & S. went to Whatcom. Started at 11 oclock.and arrived at 5 P.M. Rained most all day. Had a fair wind part of the day. All went to Linder's Ball in the evening and stat all night at the Whatcom House.
  • Fri Dec 25:  Christmas. Spent the day pleasantly and took dinner with Mr. Allen's folks at Mr. Jordan's. [margin: very pleasant day.]
  • Sat Dec 26:  All went to Mr. Eldridge's and took dinner & spent the eve. Had a good time.
  • Sun Dec 27:  Spent the day in various places and all spent the eve at McNutts house.
  • Mon Dec 28:  Attended the Whatcom Grange. Wen to Eldridge and got some seedling trees. Took dinner at Mrs. Smiths and wen to a party at Mr. Jordan in the eve.
  • Tue Dec 29:  All went over to Sehome and took dinner with Dr. Barrow's folks. Went to the wharf and saw the Germania land. Spent the eve at the Doctors' and then to births on the steamer Libby.
  • Wed Dec 30:  Reached Fidalgo at daylight and took breakfast with Mr. Best's folks. Then started for home where we arrived a little after noon. S. went to Balls after the mail.
  • Thu Dec 31:  Staid around home all day, and didn't do much of anything. Clowdy day, we're getting ready for New Year's.
  • Fri Jan 01:  All went to the party at LaConner for the benefit of Everet(?). Very unpleasant day rained most all day and it was also very windy.
  • Sat Jan 02:  Came home just after dinner. Had aa nice sail home. rained some. Did not do anything for the rest of the day.
  • Sun Jan 03:  Very sleepy day. Nothing transpired worth recording. Ball's pigs are very troublesome.
  • Mon Jan 04:  Rain most all day did not do much but sit around the house. W. worked at his accounts. [margin: Rain 6 hours]
  • Tue Jan 05:  Went to the Grange at LaConner. Staid all night and went to the Good Templars in the eve. Very pleasant. [margin: Cold & frosty]
  • Wed Jan 06:  Came home at noon. Went up in the woods & cut wood in the afternoon.
