1883 Dec 16-Jan 23, 1884

Image Id: 002-0476
Transcription
  • Sun Dec 16:  Clear frosty & pleasant. Whitneys here calling in the P.M. We were down to Whitneys & Tillinghasts in the evening. Warm tonight.
  • Mon Dec 17:  Whitneys folks & out went to LaConner and attended the Primary. Was elected delegate to the 1st Skagit County Convention. Peter hauling wood with Whitneys wagon. Fine spring-like weather.
  • Tue Dec 18:  Hauling out ditch timber in the A.M. Rainy in the P.M. Peter splitting wood in the P.M.
  • Wed Dec 19:  Hauling out ditch timber in the A.M. I worked for Peter in the P.M.
  • Thu Dec 20:  Killed the pig in the A.M. Milton Leamer came to day from Montana with frozen feet, which are very painful.
  • Fri Dec 21:  Did not do much.
  • Sat Dec 22:  Went to LaConner to tend the Convention. Staid all night.
  • Sun Dec 23:  Staid in LaConner & went to church heard Mr Vanderwater preach. Came home in the P.M.
  • Mon Dec 24:  Stormy s.E. gale the steamer left today with Whitneys oats. Went to the Christmas tree in the eve. Had a fine time.
  • Tue Dec 25:  Not a very pleasant day out of doors, but pleasant in side. The children are happy to day with their presents.
  • Wed Dec 26:  Stormy day not doing much.
  • Thu Dec 27:  Went to LaConner and got my wagon wheels. Had a fair tide both ways.
  • Fri Dec 28:  Began cleaning seed oats. Peter working 8 hours.
  • Sat Dec 29:  Cleaning oats. Peter working 8 hours. Cold.
  • Sun Dec 30:  Thermometer down to 20° this morning. Clear & frosty. Whites boys over this A.M. and last night.
  • Mon Dec 31:  Cleaning seed grain.
  • Tue Jan 01:  Working at cleaning grain in the A.M. Peter also. In the house in the P.M. Peter went to Padilla in eve.
  • Wed Jan 02:  Not working in the A.M. Made a feeding rack in the P.M. out by the hay shed. Peter helping.
  • Thu Jan 03:  Sold Bourdon a lot of damaged grain yesterday & Thomas some today. Cleaning grain. Peter also.
  • Fri Jan 04:  Cleaning grain. Went down to Whitneys in the eve to hear Joe Goodwin play the violin. He was here last night with the other young folks.
  • Sat Jan 05:  Cleaning grain all day. Had the highest ride of the season to day. It has been a very warm & wet week.
  • Sun Jan 06:  Staid at home very quiet Sunday. We are reading "His Sombre Rivals".
  • Mon Jan 07:  Cleaning grain. Peter also.
  • Tue Jan 08:  Went to LaConner to tend election. Rainy day.
  • Wed Jan 09:  Cleaning grain. Bad weather.
  • Thu Jan 10:  Cleaning grain. Whitneys folks up this evening.
  • Fri Jan 11:  Cleaning grain. Reading "The Scarlet Letter".
  • Sat Jan 12:  Cleaning grain. Whitneys & Tillinghasts & S????? were up to eat popcorn & molasses candy. Pleasant.
  • Sun Jan 13:  Pleasant quiet Sunday.
  • Mon Jan 14:  Cleaning grain as usual. Fine weather.
  • Tue Jan 15:  Cleaning grain 1/2 Mr. Burdon here in the eve.
  • Wed Jan 16:  Cleaning grain.
  • Thu Jan 17:  Went to LaConner. Staid at home all night. Peter dyking. Fine winter weather.
  • Fri Jan 18:  Came home from LaConner in the P.M. Filed a saw for Kalso. Went to Callahan's party in the eve.
  • Sat Jan 19:  Very pleasant weather. Cool & clear.
  • Sun Jan 20:  Cleaning oats.
  • Mon Jan 21:  Cleaning oats.
  • Tue Jan 22:  Peter, Ida & Milt went to LaConner. Party in the eve.
  • Wed Jan 23:  Cleaning oats.

1884 Jan 24-Mar 2

Image Id: 002-0477
Transcription
  • Thu Jan 24:  No entry.
  • Fri Jan 25:  Cleaning oats, buy fine weather.
  • Sat Jan 26:  Cleaning grain.
  • Sun Jan 27:  Went calling over to Darcys.
  • Mon Jan 28:  Cleaning grain.
  • Tue Jan 29:  Finished cleaning grain. Sold a load of stuff to Callahan. Oats & carrots.
  • Wed Jan 30:  Fixing a saw and writing up a lease.
  • Thu Jan 31:  Went up to the woods with Peter to make rails.
  • Fri Feb 01:  Went to the woods and then back to load the oats. Finished loading at 4 P.M.
  • Sat Feb 02:  Went to LaConner to weigh the oats.
  • Sun Feb 03:  Came home from LaConner with Callahan. Stormy, NE snow storm. A. Leamer & White boys here.
  • Mon Feb 04:  Did not do much but sit about the house.
  • Tue Feb 05:  Ditto. Monroe(?) here to look at the day.
  • Wed Feb 06:  Cold & snowy. Not doing much.
  • Thu Feb 07:  Went to LaConner. Milt started for Montana. Staid all night at LaConner.
  • Fri Feb 08:  Staid town all day. Snowing again.
  • Sat Feb 09:  Cold freezing weather. Ida sick with tooth ache. She went out to Mary's. I came home on foot.
  • Sun Feb 10:  Mercury down to 13 this morning. Coldest of the season so far.
  • Mon Feb 11:  Cold & clear went to town and brought Ida home in the eve. Mercury at 10.
  • Tue Feb 12:  Staid at home doing nothing the coldest morning of the season. Mercury at 7°.
  • Wed Feb 13:  Crumrine & Stearns down and signed the lease yesterday, I also. Clear & cold.
  • Thu Feb 14:  Went to the Ridge in the P.M. to see Shuster(?). He is to deliver me 5 cord of ditching timber. Ready for the ditch. Sold Mr Cook 2 1/2 tons of hay yesterday.
  • Fri Feb 15:  Cool & pleasant. Ida went to LaConner with Whitneys.
  • Sat Feb 16:  Went up to my timber ranch with Peter to help him split rails. Weather cool, ground covered with snow and frozen.
  • Sun Feb 17:  Cold blustering weather. Whitneys folks and Aunt Lou were up here calling and we went down there in the evening. This has been the coldest Feb since we have lived in this country and the coldest winter since 74 & 5.
  • Mon Feb 18:  Snow again till last night. Ed & Mary was here & the Wallace boys from California. Ida went to town with Ed Stacey to get teeth fixed.
  • Tue Feb 19:  Colder heavy North East snow storm.
  • Wed Feb 20:  Thermometer down to 10 this morning. Snow 10 in deep and drifted some. Snowing again in the afternoon.
  • Thu Feb 21:  Warmer today. But the slough is frozen up. So I could not go to LaConner. Scow melting some. I am reading "Foot Print of Time". Ida came home this eve.
  • Fri Feb 22:  Sold Callahan 20 acres of timber land. Snow went off to day. Not doing much.
  • Sat Feb 23:  Rainy day not doing much. Went to the wood with Peter to make rails
  • Sun Feb 24:  Was at home. Had visitors from Padilla.
  • Mon Feb 25:  Rainy in the A.M. Not doing much.
  • Tue Feb 26:  Loaded up a load of hay for Mr Cook. Worked for Tillinghast in the P.M. in tank(?).
  • Wed Feb 27:  Started for Cooks with hay at 6 A.M. Tillinghast with us got back at 5 P.M.
  • Thu Feb 28:  Went to LaConner and got a trunk.
  • Fri Feb 29:  Working for Tillinghast on tank. Art White here and fixed up my deeds.
  • Sat Mar 01:  Had a lame shoulder did not do much took up carpet. Sold bees to Jennie. It has been fine weather all this week.
  • Sun Mar 02:  Staid at Tillinghasts & Whitneys all day. Pleasant.

1884 Mar 3-Apr 3

Image Id: 002-0478
Transcription
  • Mon Mar 03:  Went to LaConner and took the organ and left it with Martin. Whitney began plowing. Fine weather.
  • Tue Mar 04:  Working in the orchard transplanting trees, &c. Ground in fine rain. Packing up to go to PA.
  • Wed Mar 05:  Sold Callahan & Peck some hay. Took Pecks note for $44.70. Weighed out Seamons(?) hay.
  • Thu Mar 06:  Started for LaConner. Went up as far as Kellys and staid all night. Cold north wind.
  • Fri Mar 07:  Went to LaConner & put up at the McGlinn house.
  • Sat Mar 08:  Spent the day in LaConner sacking trunks, &c.
  • Sun Mar 09:  Went to the dedication of the Baptist Church. Saw Mr. Lyle Wallace captured.
  • Mon Mar 10:  Staid in LaConner. Settled up with Crumrine & Stearns. Got all ready for a start.
  • Tue Mar 11:  Started East at 3 oclock in the morning. Had a rough day. Folks all seasick. Got to Seattle at 3 P.M. & started for Tacoma and around at 7 P.M.
  • Wed Mar 12:  Went to Portland today. arrived at 4 P.M. Went to the Quimby House.
  • Thu Mar 13:  Staid all day in Portland.
  • Fri Mar 14:  Started from Portland at 1 o'clock and travelled all night.
  • Sat Mar 15:  Travelled from Umatilla to Spokane Falls. Our baggage was put on the Express today.
  • Sun Mar 16:  Travelling all day through Montana. Was at Missoula at 2 P.M.
  • Mon Mar 17:  Traveling through Mont. and Dakota.
  • Tue Mar 18:  Still going in Dakota.
  • Wed Mar 19:  Went through Minn. and arrived in St. Paul at 7:30 PM. Went to the Sherman house and staid all night.
  • Thu Mar 20:  Started from St. Paul at 12:45 P.M. Travelled all day & all night.
  • Fri Mar 21:  Arrived in Chicago this morning Early and took Breakfast. Started at 8.50 for Buffalo.
  • Sat Mar 22:  Got in Buffalo at 3.50 and left again at 8.40 and arrived in LaPlume at 7.25 P.M. took supper at Holgates and got home at 8 P.M.
  • Sun Mar 23:  Staid in the house all day. It was a rainy day. Did lots of talking &c.
  • Mon Mar 24:  Saw George this P.M. on his return from Scranton.
  • Tue Mar 25:  Pleasant day went down to LaPlume. Saw the improvements then went to George Baileys. Father went to Scranton.
  • Wed Mar 26:  Staid at home in the A.M. & went down to Factoryville in the P.M. Saw Aunt & Grandma also Grandpa Green & some other folks. Clowdy.
  • Thu Mar 27:  Staid at home all day. Uncle Byron was here and Holgates folks in the P.M.
  • Fri Mar 28:  Went with George to Dalton in the morning. Staid about home in the P.M.
  • Sat Mar 29:  Went to the saw mill in the A.M. Did not do much in the balance of the day.
  • Sun Mar 30:  Mercury down to 15° with a strong wind. Blowing a gale. Went to Church at F.
  • Mon Mar 31:  Cold & windy. Helped Father some tap over trees. Went down to the Post office in the P.M. A little sap run.
  • Tue Apr 01:  Helped Father tap over trees. Sap run pretty good. Went for the mail in the P.M.
  • Wed Apr 02:  Helped gather sap in the A.M. Staid in the house and read East Lyme in the P.M.
  • Thu Apr 03:  Snow storm yesterday eve & P.M. Cold & windy to day. Went to Scranton to get some clothes. Snow 3 inches deep to day. Got home at 7 P.M.

  • Tue May 20:  Working about home.

  • Sat May 31:  Some warmer. Digging plants and hoing strawberries.

  • Fri Jun 13:  Picked berries a little while in the morning.
  • Sat Jun 14:  And hoed corn the balance of the day. Geo went to Scranton & Wilkes-Barre.
  • Sun Jun 15:  Went to Factoryville to church. Heard Rev S. W. Putnam preach a fine sermon.
  • Mon Jun 16:  Having and picking berries. Warm & dry. Mercury standing a 40 in the A.M. and at 80 in the P.M.
  • Tue Jun 17:  Ida started for Scranton to see Nettie. I hoeing & picking berries.
  • Wed Jun 18:  Picking berries. Warm & dry.
  • Thu Jun 19:  Ida came home from Scranton. Work ditto yesterday.
  • Fri Jun 20:  Commencement day at Keystone Academy. Picked berries in the A.M. & went down to Factoryville in the P.M. Took supper with Aunt.
  • Sat Jun 21:  Very warm. Above 90 in the shade for the last 3 days. Picking berries in the A.M. & making wire fence in the P.M. Had peas for dinner.
  • Sun Jun 22:  Ida went to Church. Staid at home & read Uncle Tom's Cabin. Warm & dry.
