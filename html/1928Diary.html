1928 Jan 1-14

Image Id: 003-0035
Transcription
  • Sun Jan 01:  Temp 34. Snow 6 inches deep. Snowing all night and this A.M. At home all day with cold. Heard Revs MaGee & Mathews per radio. The Wrights left for Seattle at 10 A.M.
  • Mon Jan 02:  Temp 35. Some Rain & mist. Snow melting some. Heard of Will Hagadorn's death in Cal.
  • Tue Jan 03:  Light rain. Snow going slowly. Went out to Allen's for groceries this A.M. Temp 48 max Min 43.
  • Wed Jan 04:  Ground still frozen hard, but about all thawed tonight. Temp 48. Downtown & settled bill. Heard Mrs. James Callahan died yesterday morning. Ida about sick with cold. Mine some better.
  • Thu Jan 05:  Clowdy. Temp 50. Went to LaConner & Pleasant Ridge with Bessner to Mrs. Callahans funeral. Ida worse & had Dr. Cook.
  • Fri Jan 06:  Pleasant. Ida some better. Down to Police Court. ??? Mr Knapp, Mr Campbell & Abbey present. Temp 46.
  • Sat Jan 07:  Isaac Tillinghast died in Huntington Park Cal. Clowdy & light rains. Ida worse and had Dr. Cook again. Downtown & got Ford bands renewed.
  • Sun Jan 08:  Clowdy Temp 53. Went to Presby Ch in the A.M. In the PM went to Will Hagadorns funeral at Coug Ch. Ida not much of any better.
  • Mon Jan 09:  Pleasant. Ida had a bad night. I sent for Hilda. Grant here a while. Took Hilda out to Whites & got some eggs. Grant there for Hilda.
  • Tue Jan 10:  Rain last night. Clowdy to day. Ida about the same. In bed. Linwood Davis called this eve. Mrs Bradley out to Mrs. Whitneys tonight.
  • Wed Jan 11:  Rain all day. Ida had a bad night. Dr. Cook here last night. Not much better to day.
  • Thu Jan 12:  Clowdy. Ida no better. Grants folks here a few minutes before going to a Kiwanis meeting.
  • Fri Jan 13:  Cooler part Clowdy. Temp 42. Ida no better. Dr. Cook here this A.M. Went to Mr. Blout's funeral Elks Home. Franks folks came at 6:30.
  • Sat Jan 14:  Temp 38. Pleasant. Wrights here. Ida no better. Grants here in the evening. Ran out of gas and had to ???? my car to get home at 2:30 A.M. Sunday.

1928 Jan 15-29

Transcription
  • Sun Jan 15:  Temp. 30 - 27 at the ranch. The Wrights went home at 3:15 P.M. Pearl, Calvin, Mrs. Stacey & Mrs Leatherwood here this P.M. Mrs. Hermiston came at noon. [margin: Went to Presby Ch this eve.]
  • Mon Jan 16:  Temp 30 Fog. Went with Bessner to Victor Andersons funeral at Conway. Fog all day on the Flats and several accidents.
  • Tue Jan 17:  Temp 30 Fog all day. Ida still in bed. Downtown in the P.M. Called on the Judge & friends.
  • Wed Jan 18:  Temp 32 Foggy. 36 at night. Downtown in the P.M. Ida out on the lounge to day. Wrote to George & Nettie.
  • Thu Jan 19:  Temp 32. 41 in the P.M. Pleasant. Went to the ranch. Ida about the same.
  • Fri Jan 20:  Clowdy & sprinkel of rain. Rev. B.C. Brooks, Frank White here for a call. Also Mrs Williams & Mrs Aygt. Ida about the same. A letter from Nettie.
  • Sat Jan 21:  Clowdy Temp 40. At home A.M. down to Justice Court in the P.M. Helped destroy 185 qts of booze. Ida about the same.
  • Sun Jan 22:  Very(?) pleasant. Went to Presby Ch A.M. & P.M. Hilda & the children called also Frank & Blanche.
  • Mon Jan 23:  Clowdy. Temp 36. Choring & downtown. Ida worse and Dr. Cook here. Fred DeRush died to day.
  • Tue Jan 24:  Clowdy Temp 36. At home most of the day. At home most of the day. Ida slightly better; in bed all day.
  • Wed Jan 25:  Clowdy. At home A.M. down to see the Ford in the P.M. went to the Burns Anniversary lecture in the Eve.
  • Thu Jan 26:  Clowdy. At home A.M. Went to Fred DeRush funeral in the P.M. Ida some better. Mrs. Carroll, Mrs Bessner, Mrs Davis & Linwood & Miss Kash called.
  • Fri Jan 27:  Pleasant. Temp 38. At home almost all day reading. Ida not so well.
  • Sat Jan 28:  Rain most of the day. Light. Went to Mount Vernon to Farm Bureau Luncheon. The Wrights & Mirriam came. Dr. Cook here to day. Mrs Herlison went home.
  • Sun Jan 29:  Showery, Ida & I went to Seattle with the Wrights. Ida stood the trip fairly well. I went to the U.M.E. Church with the Wrights in the eve. Jesper

1928 Jan 30-Feb 14

Image Id: 003-0037
Transcription
  • Mon Jan 30:  In Seattle. Out to the Gongle(?) Grounds with Frank.
  • Tue Jan 31:  Pleasant down town. Visited KOMO stations & went to the Blue Mouse to see the "Jazz Singer". [margin: Saw Basel Pociet(?)]
  • Wed Feb 01:  Pleasant. Went to the Metropolitan to see the "King of Kings". A wonderful picture. Ida not much better.
  • Thu Feb 02:  Pleasant. Downtown and over to the university campus and called on Donald's folks.
  • Fri Feb 03:  Pleasant. Down town with Frank & called on the McGinnis's folks.
  • Sat Feb 04:  Downtown A.M. at Wrights in P.M. The Wrights went to see "King of Kings",
  • Sun Feb 05:  Showery. At Wrights & heard Rev Mathews in the A.M. by radio. Went to university M.E. Ch Vesper service at 5 PM. Ida a little better.
  • Mon Feb 06:  Pleasant. Came to Anacortes with Frank on the Retriever. Arrived at 8:15. Frank here all night.
  • Tue Feb 07:  Rain A.M. & last night. Downtown and paid bills & played horseshoes with Abbey.
  • Wed Feb 08:  Pleasant. Temp 40. Went to the ranch. Grant has three men ditching. Fifty tons of hay & that oats gone. Hay at $15.00 per ton. Frank started back with the Retriever.
  • Thu Feb 09:  Pleasant. Temp 35. Choring about home and downtown in the P.M. Had a ride in the new Ford Coupe.
  • Fri Feb 10:  Pleasant but rain at night. Went to the farm. Made a sluice box for the lane ditch & fixed windows in the old house. I rec'd a litter from Ida & answered. [margin: Got a load of hopwood.]
  • Sat Feb 11:  Temp 35. Hard rain last night & this A.M. Pleasant P.M. At home & downtown to lunch.
  • Sun Feb 12:  Pleasant. Went to church AM & Pres PM. Was down to Mrs Williams for dinner. Lincoln Day.
  • Mon Feb 13:  Clowdy. Went to Seattle with Frank. He came with Mr. Campbell to figure on building an additions to this house.
  • Tue Feb 14:  [margin: W.A. Hawkins died.] Pleasant. In Seattle & went to the big auto show. If is fine. Ida had Dr. Palmer to day & is not much of any better. In bed about all the time.

Transcription

  • Fri Feb 17:  Frost 34. Pleasant. went to the ranch and had an interview with County & Drainage Coms. Nothing definitely settled. Frank in Mr Campbell here from Seattle this eve about new house. He reports Ida & Nettie better.
  • Sat Feb 18:  Foggy. Went to Mount Vernon with Bessner to a tax meeting. Stopped at the ranch on way home.
  • Sun Feb 19:  Foggy all day. Went to Church A.M. & P.M. Pres. Wrote to Geo & Ida.
  • Mon Feb 20:  Clowdy & cool. Went to the farm for lunch & in P.M. down to Wilsons. Mrs. McCullough's sale took place to day.
  • Tue Feb 21:  Clowdy. At home in the A.M. Cleaned the chimney & range. Transplanted some tulips. Down town in P.M. A letter from Ida.
  • Wed Feb 22:  Temp 32. 28 at the ranch. Went to the farm and in the PM. went to LaC. to see the ruins of the Baptist Church. The Ford busted and had to leave it at Whitney. Staid at Grants all night.
  • Thu Feb 23:  Temp 30 at the farm. Came to Anacortes with Harry Sanderson and worked around the place moving plants &c. Campbell began moving dirt for the new addition to the house.
  • Fri Feb 24:  Temp 30. Pleasant. Working around the place till noon. Went to the Ranch for the Ford in the P.M. Letter from George. Heard of G.B. Mathewson's death.
  • Sat Feb 25:  Cool S. wind clowdy. Working about the garden. Planted sweet peas, garden peas & carrots. Frank and Campbell here with new house plans.
  • Sun Feb 26:  Pleasant. Went to P church A.M. Went & got Mr & Mrs Williams who spent the evening with me listening to Radio.
  • Mon Feb 27:  Pleasant. Men hauling dirt working here A.M. Went to C.C. Luncheon, then to Mt Vernon and the ranch. Paid taxes.
  • Tue Feb 28:  Temp 32 Frost. Planted more carrotts & peas. Took out the old fireplace. Went to Presby supper for organ fund.
  • Wed Feb 29:  Frost 32. Pleasant. Working about the place. Washed. Planted sweet peas & a row of garden peas. The men still hailing out gravel.

1928 Mar 1-15

Image Id: 003-0039
Transcription
  • Thu Mar 01:  Frost 30. Clear & pleasant. Men still digging basement. Self tinkering at various jobs. Mr Campbell, Contractor, here today.
  • Fri Mar 02:  Frost 30. At home. Franks folks came last night & the Goods came this AM. Ida came also. Frank went back to Seattle to night.
  • Sat Mar 03:  Temp 35 Frost. Men still all at the basement. I went to Farm Bureau meeting with Bessner.
  • Sun Mar 04:  Pleasant. Went to Ch & SS & to Ch in the eve. Bunyon Pictures.
  • Mon Mar 05:  Pleasant. Six men hauling dirt. I went to the Ranch & got a jack screw. Grant plowing since Sat. Ground in good order.
  • Tue Mar 06:  Pleasant. Campbell here and they began putting in forms for concrete.
  • Wed Mar 07:  Clowdy but no rain. Lovelace & Son putting in forms. Ida sick with neuritis. Ed Handy died yesterday.
  • Thu Mar 08:  Clowdy with south wind & hard rain at night. Frank here to day. Carpenters putting in forms. Signed a deed to Wrights for a half interest in Lot 8-9-10 Bk 142.
  • Fri Mar 09:  Rain last night & showery all day. Mr Campbell here to day. Pouring concrete this PM. Ida downstairs but complaining of not feeling well. Grant sick this week.
  • Sat Mar 10:  Rainy AM. Pleasant PM. Pouring cement. Finished the walls. Donald & wife came this evening.
  • Sun Mar 11:  Pleasant. Frank came with the "Retriever" at 9:30 & went to Wilsons this PM. Nettie & Francis also. Went to Presby Ch this AM. Don & wife went back at 10:30 today.
  • Mon Mar 12:  Strong wind & rain at night. Made a bird bath. Went downtown with Frank. He sailed for Alaska this P.M. at 4 oclock. May he have good luck.
  • Tue Mar 13:  Pleasant. Campbell here and took down the forms. All ready to make the floor to the basement.
  • Wed Mar 14:  Pleasant. Frost 34. All hand working on house improvement. Pouring floor of the basement, front door steps & excavating for fireplace. Ida downtown for a Collins bath. James O'Laughlin died.
  • Thu Mar 15:  Clowdy. Rain last night & some to day. All hands working. Concreting nearly done. Floor laid, rough.

  • Tue Mar 27:  Clowdy & strong S.E. wind & rain at night. Went to the ranch. Land very wet. Grant has the Jorgenson boys papering upstairs. Men at the sewer. C. Campbell here this P.M. Got eggs at Whites 28 cts per Doz. [margin: Harris Peck buried today 27th.]

  • Thu Apr 05:  Pleasant. All hands working. Enlarged the north bedroom and finished shingling the high part and stripped the porch of shingles.

  • Sat Apr 07:  Fine day, Men working 1/2 Self choring all day.

1928 Jun 26-Jul 12
