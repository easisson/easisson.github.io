1878 Dec 29-Jan 22,1879

Image Id: 002-0407
Transcription
  • Sun Dec 29:  Staid at home in the fore noon and went down to Whitneys in the P.M. Cold & frosty. Ed came home from Fidalgo.
  • Mon Dec 30:  Worked on my secretary. Was making doors, &c.
  • Tue Dec 31:  Worked on Secretary doors some and part of the time on brackets. Had company in the evening to watch with old year out. Had supper at ten o'clock.
  • Wed Jan 01:  Got up at 8 A.M. rather late to begin this New Year. Did not do much except take a horse back ride. Went to the Ridge & called on McK???? folks.
  • Thu Jan 02:  Cold as usual. Made picture frames most all day, Ed and I went up to the woods in the morning and got a load of bark. Mr. Gilkie was here in the eve and staid all night.
  • Fri Jan 03:  Filed a saw for Whitney in the morning and worked on my secretacy the balance of the day. Whitney got home LaConer this eve. [margin: Cold.]
  • Sat Jan 04:  Worked on Secretary most of the day. Clean & cold. Went to Literary in the evening. Had a good turn out.
  • Sun Jan 05:  Staid at home all day. Reading & writing some. Gave Ed Stacey note of $30.00 for cow interest 12 pcnt.
  • Mon Jan 06:  Worked on secretary most of the day. Cold as usual
  • Tue Jan 07:  Went to LaConner and tried to settle up with Gaches but he had no money. Got home at 4 P.M.
  • Wed Jan 08:  Worked in the ????? on secretary ????ing it. making & varnishing picture frames &c. Ed hunting killed 10 ducks & 2 geese. [margin: Snow this morning. Rainy.]
  • Thu Jan 09:  In the A.M. helped Whitney load barley. In the P.M. worked on my aquarium. Mandy Upson sick.
  • Fri Jan 10:  Worked on aquarium in the A.M. and in the P.M. split posts for here yard. Very hard wind. Ed went to LaConner.
  • Sat Jan 11:  Little snow this morning. Worked in the hen yard all day. Began putting on the pickets. McKown start after his hay.
  • Sun Jan 12:  Staid at home in the fore noon and in the P.M. went down to Whitneys. Whitney up here in the morning. Warmer.
  • Mon Jan 13:  Stormy some. Put a few pickets on the chicken yard. [margin: Snow.]
  • Tue Jan 14:  Finished the chicken yard. Cold and frosty this morning. [margin: Snow.]
  • Wed Jan 15:  Mr Clark eat dinner here today. In the A.M. staid around home and hung an ax and got ready to go to the woods. In the P.M. went up to the woods & cut down a tree for rails. McKown came with the beef just dark. [margin: Snow.]
  • Thu Jan 16:  Cut up and took care of the beef in the A.M. and helped Jones fix up Trimbles old boat after dinner. Cold & stormy.
  • Fri Jan 17:  Snow last night as usual. Went up to the woods. Made a hundred rails and cut another cedar. Lots of snow in the woods. Went to Literary in the evening.
  • Sat Jan 18:  Satid in the house all day did not do anything. Cold, Windy & Rainy.
  • Sun Jan 19:  Staid at home all day. Went down to Whitneys just night. Lisle Wallace was here all day.
  • Mon Jan 20:  Went up to the timber and made rails & pickets.
  • Tue Jan 21:  Making rails as usual. Al & Martha came down this P.M. [margin: Rain last night.]
  • Wed Jan 22:  Al went home this morning. Making rails as usual. Rain in the P.M. Have 815 12 ft rails to date.

1879 Jan 23-Feb 14

Image Id: 002-0408
Transcription
  • Thu Jan 23:  Rainy most all day. Ida & Martha went over to D'Arcy's. Went up to the woods and made rails some. Mrs. Klingenmaier got hurt. [margin: Rails 350 Pods? 30]
  • Fri Jan 24:  Staid around house all day and went up to the school house to the children's party. Got home at 1.30 A.M.
  • Sat Jan 25:  Worked on my secretary all day varnishing(?) & staining(?).
  • Sun Jan 26:  Went down to Mr. Klingenmaier's. Mrs K. was some better. Came home to dinner and went up to Joneses with Harry. Pleasant day.
  • Mon Jan 27:  Went up to the woods and made rails until 2 P.M. Went down and staid all night with Klingenmaier. Pleasant.
  • Tue Jan 28:  Staid in bed until 10 o'clock. After dinner went up and chopped down two trees for rails.
  • Wed Jan 29:  Made rails as usual. Ida & Ed went down to Klingenmaier's.
  • Thu Jan 30:  Made rails. Pleasant weather. Cold frosty night.
  • Fri Jan 31:  Finished making rails & cut wood some up by McNutt ranch. Maple. Went to the last Literary in the evening. Wallace was down and stay with us(?).
  • Sat Feb 01:  Cut wood up on Smiths ranch in the A.M. and covered ditch in the afternoon. Fine weather.
  • Sun Feb 02:  Went down to Whitneys in the morning. Staid in the house the balance of the day.
  • Mon Feb 03:  Went up to the wood and sharpened posts. Ed began hauling rails for cow pasture.
  • Tue Feb 04:  Ed hauling rails. I was sharpening posts and cutting roads in the woods. [margin: Nice weather.]
  • Wed Feb 05:  Went up on Smiths land and cut fire wood. Alder. Ed hauling rails.
  • Thu Feb 06:  Dito yesterday. Rain last night.
  • Fri Feb 07:  2 inches of snow on the ground this morning. Ed finished hauling rails, hauled them out to the slough. The maple wood also. Got done at noon. Worked on my secretary in the P.M. putting on doors & knobs. There was a man due selling wheat at 25&cent; per lb. Got 8 lbs.
  • Sat Feb 08:  Very stormy day staid in the house all day. Ed went to LaConner. Cemented my aquarium.
  • Sun Feb 09:  Spent the day reading &c. Wrote a letter to the Rural Publishing Co. for seeds. Stormy day.
  • Mon Feb 10:  Cut wood and done chores about the house. Tried my aquarium. It was a failure. Snow in A.M.
  • Tue Feb 11:  Snow this morning. Very cold & windy. With N. East snow storm. Conn(?) here in the evening. Shot 2 ducks on the grain.
  • Wed Feb 12:  Cold & stormy snow. Clark's folks were here to dinner. They were moving to day. Sended their stuff in my granary.
  • Thu Feb 13:  Filed a saw for Ck Soderburg in the A.M. in the P.M. Did not do anything most of the time. Fixed the sluice box in the 45 ft slough. Went down to Mrs Upsons to supper and staid all night at Whitneys.
  • Fri Feb 14:  Snow gone this morning. Went up to Balls in the P.M. & got my pig. Got 9 eggs today. Helped Clark tow his things up the slough.

1879 Feb 15-Mar 8Duplicate of next

Image Id: 002-0409
Transcription
  • Sat Feb 15:  Went up to Smiths Granary and helped Clark unload his goods then went and put my rails and wood into his scow and came home with it. Unloaded the scow & done the chores. [margin: Showers]
  • Sun Feb 16:  Staid at home in the A.M. in the afternoon went up to Callahan's and then down to Whitneys got the washing and came home. Changable weather.
  • Mon Feb 17:  Went down to get a load of wood. Done the chores and then went up to the White house to get some trees & bushes &c. Planted ome potatos & cabbage in the house(?). Got a letter from Geo & Nettie.
  • Tue Feb 18:  Rainy day. Then down to Whitneys & paid off his note and got it Cow calved back. Made fence the balance of the day for Cow pasture.
  • Wed Feb 19:  Rain hard in the morning 3 hours. Worked on the fence some. Very hard south wind in the evening. Ida went down to Mr Upsons of the P.M.
  • Thu Feb 20:  Very heavy wind last night and this evening. Blew my shed into the slough and the privy over also. Work on the cow yard fence, finished it. Harry & Jule staid here tonight. Wall flowers in bloom.
  • Fri Feb 21:  Fixed up the fence where the shed was and then went and drove up the cow and calf. Made gate for the cow pen. and filed the rip saw. Cut wood some. Rainy P.M.
  • Sat Feb 22:  Very windy & rainy. worked for harry to day and done the chores. Made window & door frames.
  • Sun Feb 23:  Very stormy day. Done the chores and went down to Whitneys just night.
  • Mon Feb 24:  Worked at home in the A.M. making a walk for the cow across the slough out by the shed or where it was. In the PM worked for Harry 4 hours making doors for his house. High wind this morning.
  • Tue Feb 25:  Worked for Harry to day. took up a scow load of stuff and worked on the house. Harry staid here all night. Jule also.
  • Wed Feb 26:  Worked for Harry to day. Fixing house hanging doors &c. Rained all the P.M. Harry & Jule staid all night.
  • Thu Feb 27:  Harry & Jule here yet. Worked on Harry's house cold & stormy.
  • Fri Feb 28:  Worked for Harry in the forenoon. Pleasant. He & Jule went home in the P.M. I made fence in the PM.
  • Sat Mar 01:  Pleasant. Worked at my picket fence. went down to Whitneys to sing in the evening.
  • Sun Mar 02:  Set a hen on 13 eggs. Very hard wind in the night. Rainy & clowdy to day. Went up to the ridge in the afternoon. Got some school books at Jakes to set up school(?).
  • Mon Mar 03:  Had a team to haul rails in the A.M. Made fence in the P.M. very windy.
  • Tue Mar 04:  Had a very bad cold. Did not do much in the fore noon. Made fence in the P.M. Allen was here assessing. Cold disagreeable weather. Got 16 eggs.
  • Wed Mar 05:  Grafted some trees and made fence, finish drawing posts.
  • Thu Mar 06:  Went down to Whitneys in the morning with eggs. Made a pair of oars. Very hard wind. the hardest of the season.
  • Fri Mar 07:  Down to W's in the evening. Began my picket fence putting on pickets. Ida went over to Jules in the P.M.
  • Sat Mar 08:  Cold stormy day. Did not do much. Fixed my violin and cut wood. All down to Whitneys in the evening to hear Clark play.

1879 Mar 9-Apr 2

Image Id: 002-0411
Transcription
  • Sun Mar 09:  Stormy staid at home all day in the house. Had bad cold.
  • Mon Mar 10:  Snowing & rainy all day. Very fine rain. Worked outdoors some putting on pickets.
  • Tue Mar 11:  Worked around house on picket fence. Ida getting read to go to Oregon. High tide. [margin: Rainy some.]
  • Wed Mar 12:  Pleasant. worked on picket fence all day. Lettie Upson was up to help Ida some. Ed Stacey was here.
  • Thu Mar 13:  Finished putting on pickets on the fence. Pleasant with north wind went out and burned straw and made a gate also a hanger(?) by gate.
  • Fri Mar 14:  Went to LaConner to take Ida on her way to Oregon. Jule went also. Staid all night at LaConner. Rain tonight.
  • Sat Mar 15:  Staid in LaConner all day. Stormy day.
  • Sun Mar 16:  Came home to day. Ida started from LaConner on the Fanny Lake. Pleasant. Clark & Upson call on my in the P.M.
  • Mon Mar 17:  In the A.M. ditching, on the Allen 80. Draining wet places. In the P.M. did not do much. Fine weather.
  • Tue Mar 18:  Spent the day making & fixing gates. Finished my picket fence gates & all. Got the mail. A letter from Vinz & one from Mr. Hyde to Ida. Fine weather.
  • Wed Mar 19:  Rainy. R.E. Whitney and Kate Bradley married this morning. Grand party this eve at Padilla Hall. Clark Schultz, Libby & Gisham bros. played. 140 people present. The LaConner folks came on the Fanny Lake.
  • Thu Mar 20:  Slept in the fore noon. In the P.M. went up to the Whitehouse with Whitney's folks.
  • Fri Mar 21:  Pleasant day in the AM finished splitting my wood up on Smiths place. In the P.M. made garden a little and dug parsnips. Planted some peas, lettuce, Radishes & buts(?).
  • Sat Mar 22:  Did not do much of anything. Rain again last night. Went down to Whitneys in the evening and heard some music.
  • Sun Mar 23:  Got up done the chores, then went over to Harry's and from there went to Al Leamers. Got dinner & staid all night.
  • Mon Mar 24:  Came from Al's this morning. Got home at noon and worked on the Roller in the P.M. Pretty hard rain last night. No plowing done yet in this neighborhood. Leamer's ground in good order. Calhoun working 5 teams today.
  • Tue Mar 25:  Worked on Jakes Roller all day.
  • Wed Mar 26:  Worked on the Roller to day. Rainy & windy.
  • Thu Mar 27:  Worked on Roller 2 hours. Did not do much the balance of the day. There was a magic lantern show in Padilla Hall. which did not amt to much.
  • Fri Mar 28:  Rained all day steady. Staid in the house and cooked & fiddled & fixed violin bow. Harry was here in the A.M.
  • Sat Mar 29:  Did not do much but cut wood. Rain a good part of the day. Skagit River up very high.
  • Sun Mar 30:  Was up on the ridge most of the day. Took dinner with Allen's. Pleasant.
  • Mon Mar 31:  Tinkered around the house & garden in the A.M. and dug ditch in the P.M.
  • Tue Apr 01:  Dug ditch in the Lake on the "Allen 80" in the A.M. and spaded garden and set out some onions in the P.M. Made some flower beds.
  • Wed Apr 02:  Ditching all day on the "Allen 80". Went to Allens and got some eggs and set 2 hens on 15 eggs each. Pleasant weather. [margin: Pleasant.]

1879 Apr 3-23

Image Id: 002-0412
Transcription
  • Thu Apr 03:  Ditching in the A.M. on the "Allen 80". Cleaned out the old ditch on Smiths(?) line. In the P.M. made garden some planted some peas & onions. Pleasant.
  • Fri Apr 04:  Ditching in the A.M. & eve(?). Made garden in the P.M. Planted some turnips & carrots & set out some strawberries. John Ball here in the eve with scow load of grain. Pleasant with N. wind.
  • Sat Apr 05:  Rainy part of the day. Did not do much of anything. Planted some Early Rose potatoes.
  • Sun Apr 06:  Staid at home all day. Read in the Hunchback of Notre Dame. Ed Stacey was here to dinner.
  • Mon Apr 07:  Rainy day. Did not do much. Worked on my boat some.
  • Tue Apr 08:  Still rain and disagreeable. Finished fixing my boat skeg. Dug ditch some out in the peat bed. Took some planks up to the 45 ft slough in the PM. Windy.
  • Wed Apr 09:  Windy & rainy in the morning. Worked on the 45 slough box in the P.M. Powell & Harry helped.
  • Thu Apr 10:  In the AM read Fowler's Book. In the PM worked at the 45 ft slough as yesterday. Powell & Harry also Otto. Put the box in.
  • Fri Apr 11:  In the AM worked on Jakes Roller in the AM 3 hours. In the PM finished putting in the box. Otto, Harry & Powell also. Otto up here in the evening.
  • Sat Apr 12:  Rainy. In the fore noon went down to Whitneys & settled up. In the PM it rained and did not do much of anything.
  • Sun Apr 13:  Rainy day. Staid at home all day writing letters & reading &c.
  • Mon Apr 14:  Rainy in the morning. Worked on the Roller 3 hours. Travelled over the ranchs with Whitney in the PM. Was up to the White house & got some strawberry &c. to set out. Windy night & cold.
  • Tue Apr 15:  Worked at home in the A.M. setting out berries &c. Worked 5 hours on Jakes Roller. Whitney tried to plow but the land was all too wet. Rain again this evening.
  • Wed Apr 16:  Worked on Highbargers Roller 9 hours. Rain again last night. Went up to the Ridge & sawed out some washers for the Roller.
  • Thu Apr 17:  Rain again this. Land too wet to plow. Worked at home making a hog pen. Pleasant in the P.M.
  • Fri Apr 18:  Have worked 42 hours on the Roller to this date. In the AM finished up the hog pen. Went up and cleaned out the ditch to the sluice box. In the PM worked on the Roller 3 hours. Clowdy with north wind. Got 14 eggs to day & 6 hens wanted to set.
  • Sat Apr 19:  Trimmed the fruit trees, dug carrots, fixed the flower beds in the A.M. In the P.M. sawed stuff for a plank walk in front of the house. Pleasant.
  • Sun Apr 20:  Rainy in the morning. Windy in the P.M. Went up to Clarks in the A.M. Staid at home the balance of the day. Writing, reading &c.
  • Mon Apr 21:  Pleasant drying day. Whitney began plowing with two teams. I worked on the walk most of the day. Got it done.
  • Tue Apr 22:  Planted my "bounty of Hebron" potatos. Made a small cold frame and set out the cabbage plants. was down to Whitneys inn the P.M. Plowed a little in the garden. Fine weather.
  • Wed Apr 23:  Worked for Whitney plowing with old Selina & Billy. Fine team. Upson, D??? & Harry all plowing in the same field. Fine weather. Mrs McCowan sick.

1879 Apr 24-May 16

Image Id: 002-0413
Transcription
  • Thu Apr 24:  Plowing for Whitney. Dry weather with cold north wind. Chickens hatched to day.
  • Fri Apr 25:  Plowing as usual. Rainy in the P.M. but did not stop plowing. Clark has 33 acres sowed.
  • Sat Apr 26:  Rainy some but not enough to stop plowing. Whitney began sowing wheat & began plowing on my ranch. Plowing for Whitney.
  • Sun Apr 27:  Pleasant. Went down to Whitneys to dinner & in the P.M. went over his winter grain. It looks pretty well but is full of chaff. Got two letters from Ida.
  • Mon Apr 28:  Plowing with Harry on the "Allen 80". No rain yet, but threatening(?).
  • Tue Apr 29:  Plowing on the Allen 80. Pretty soft in place and hard plowing for the driver. Very pleasant & drying day. Whitney finish sowing wheat. Had 30 acres.
  • Wed Apr 30:  Plowing as usual but had to quit at 3 o'clock. It rained from that till 5 P.M. Pretty soft plowing on the Allen 80 up along the dyke.
  • Thu May 01:  The ground pretty wet but kept plowing on the Allen 80. Showery day. Poor day for picknics &c. Whitney finished harrowing in wheat.
  • Fri May 02:  Hard rain last night. Went up to Kalso's and got my boat fixed in the morning. Spaded a little in the garden. In the P.M. moved across the slough & plowed a little, but it was too wet & soft. Whitney went to LaConner.
  • Sat May 03:  Rain most all last night. Did not plow any today but moved lock across the slough. Duer plowed. Helped move & fixed plow in the P.M.
  • Sun May 04:  Wrote a letter to Ida & sent her money to come home. Ed Stacey was here. Went down to Klingenmaiers in the P.M. with Whitneys folks. Pleasant.
  • Mon May 05:  Plowing for Whitney on the "allen 80". Pleasant day. He sowed 5 2/3 acres of barley on my place to day.
  • Tue May 06:  PLowed for Whitney in the A.M. Rainy. Rained all the afternoon & evening 3/4 inches of water fell. Carlson was here in the afternoon. Went to Whitneys and got some paint and began painting the kitchen floor.
  • Wed May 07:  Finished painting the floor & painted the sitting room. Worked in the garden some. Had a good drying wind to day.
  • Thu May 08:  (Plowed 10 days up to date inclusive) Plowing again. Fine north wind weather. Ground drying fast but is pretty soft to plow today. Mr. Moore from Skagit was here to supper.
  • Fri May 09:  Plowing in Whitneys Peat bed with five teams. "Reckon we plowed pretty near two acres" Very fine day. Northwind.
  • Sat May 10:  Plowed all day for Whitney in his Peat bed. Pretty muddy. Light rain in the evening. Got a letter from Geo. and one from Ida.
  • Sun May 11:  Went over to Browns & Soderburgs took dinner with Kamies folks. Clowdy day. Soderburg & Ball have oats & barley just coming up.
  • Mon May 12:  Plowing for Whitney. The winter grain is a failure and full of chaff. "Such is life. A destiny" Harry & Jule were over here this evening. Fine weather.
  • Tue May 13:  Plowing in the A.M. Rained after dinner 1/2 inch. Finished painting.
  • Wed May 14:  Plowing as usual. Otto began sowing oats on my ranch. Cold.
  • Thu May 15:  Cold & windy plowing began on my ranch after dinner finished Whitneys preemption claim.
  • Fri May 16:  Pleasant drying weather. Plowing all day for Whitney. There is 18 acres of oats & 5 1/2 of barley sowed on my ranch to date.

1879 May 17-Jun 9

Image Id: 002-0414
Transcription
  • Sat May 17:  Plowing for Whitney on my ranch finished for this season. Left 10 acres for Summer fallow to kill the grass. Plowed my garden just night. Expected Ida & Pearl but they did not come.
  • Sun May 18:  Strawberries in full bloom. Staid at home in the AM. was down to Upsons to dinner. Helped cross the horses over in the P.M. & went up to Davies(?) to supper.
  • Mon May 19:  Plowing for Whitney in the A.M. Rained in the P.M. Something has caught all but 6 chickens. Otto up here in the evening.
  • Tue May 20:  Plowing for Whitney all day. Pleasant day. Aunt Lou, Eva & Miss Bradley came from LaConner & Mr Alexander.
  • Wed May 21:  Rec'd a telegram from Ida last night stating she was not coming this week. Plowed all day for Whitney as usual. Fine weather. Plowing across the slough.
  • Thu May 22:  Plowing - 5 teams going across the slough. Fine Weather.
  • Fri May 23:  Plowing as usual. Over next to Telegraph slough. Fine weather.
  • Sat May 24:  Plowing for Whitney. Pleasant & warm.
  • Sun May 25:  Took a boat ride with Barney & Otto to Guemes Island. Got back at 6 o'clock. Warm & dry.
  • Mon May 26:  Pleasant & warm growing weather. Sowed 2 acres of grain on my ranch which is the last. Went to LaConner & also to Dodge's place & A. Leamers.
  • Tue May 27:  Heavy rain last night. Worked at home to day in the garden. Set out cabbage. Planted onions beets, beans & potatos & flower beds.
  • Wed May 28:  Worked in the garden hoing plants &c. Planted some sugar beets.
  • Thu May 29:  Sowed 8 acres of oats for Highbarger this morning. Went up to the Ridge in the P.M. to cut timber for a beam. Whitney's are heading out on the W. house ranch.
  • Fri May 30:  Had a shower of rain this morning. Worked in the woods hewing timbers for a barn. Fine growing weather. Ida came home this eve about 10 oclock.
  • Sat May 31:  Did not do much to day. Mr White's folks were here to day & Don Meggs(?). In the P.M. took a walk with Mr. White & Whitney across the slough. Planted some peas.
  • Sun Jun 01:  Very warm pleasant day. Staid at home. Mr. Powell took the calf away. Went down to Whitneys in the evening. Lile Wallace was here and staid all night.
  • Mon Jun 02:  No entry.
  • Tue Jun 03:  Drove team for Whitney harrowing. Fine growing weather.
  • Wed Jun 04:  Drove a team as yesterday. Upson & Whitney at Court at LaConner.
  • Thu Jun 05:  Rainy in the P.M. Worked in the house helping Ida in the A.M. Hoed in the garden in the P.M.
  • Fri Jun 06:  Hard rain last night. Started for LaConner in the AM at 5 oclock. Met George in Telegraph slough & came back. Spent the visiting. Went up to the White house in the P.M. with Whitneys folks.
  • Sat Jun 07:  Strawberries ripening some. Staid in the house talking in the AM. Rainy some. Went with George salmon berrying in the P.M. got 5 quarts.
  • Sun Jun 08:  Staid at home most of the day. Went over & took supper with Harry's folks. Cool & clowdy.
  • Mon Jun 09:  Worked in the garden in the AM & went salmon berrying in the PM with Whitneys folks.

1879 Jun 10-Jul 4Duplicate of next

Image Id: 002-0415
Transcription
  • Tue Jun 10:  Worked for Road Tax. Up in the woods cutting sleepers for Canal bridge. Mosquitos quite bad. [margin: Trimble finished seeding.]
  • Wed Jun 11:  George went to the picknic at March's with Whitneys folks. Very pleasant day. Worked in the garden some. Plowing around trees.
  • Thu Jun 12:  Worked for Harry making a water tank. Very warm. Mosquitos bad.
  • Fri Jun 13:  Worked for Harry making wood shed. Warm & dry.
  • Sat Jun 14:  George & I hauled the sills & sleepers for my barn. Rain some last night. Warm & pleasant to day. Mosquitos bad.
  • Sun Jun 15:  Fine weather. Staid at home in the AM went to preaching in the eve by Mr Tade.
  • Mon Jun 16:  Laid the foundation to my barn. Fine growing weather.
  • Tue Jun 17:  worked at the scow ditch up at the barn.
  • Wed Jun 18:  Dito yesterday in the A.M. Went down & got lumber for barn in the PM.
  • Thu Jun 19:  Hoed in the garden a little. Bob Whites folks were here in the P.M. We are getting ready for the picknick. Found an oat head with 580 oats.
  • Fri Jun 20:  Started on an excursion to Fidalgo Island & mt erie at 6 oclock. All of Whitneys folks along. Folks seasick. Camped at Chris Hanson's place.
  • Sat Jun 21:  Went to Bests and ascended the mountain had a good time & got back to the campground at 6 oclock. Mr Whites folks went along.
  • Sun Jun 22:  Went down through Deception Pass and took dinner on the island. Mr Whites folks were along. Mr Thorp & wife also. Hard a hard pul to LaConner. Got home at 9 oclock.
  • Mon Jun 23:  Had a fine rain last night. Plenty of strawberries now. Done the chores & hoed in the garden.
  • Tue Jun 24:  Did not do much. Wrote a piece for the paper. Fine growing weather. John Ball here in the P.M.
  • Wed Jun 25:  Went up to Callahans. Then got some lumber and made a pair of scow oars.
  • Thu Jun 26:  George & I went down to Hansons. Staid most all day. (took dinner with Hanson.)
  • Fri Jun 27:  Geo went to LaConner with Whitney. Fixed my boat. Hauled a tank of water. Jake hauled a load of wood. [margin: Light rain.]
  • Sat Jun 28:  Went up and painted Allen's sitting room. Fine growing weather. Grain looking well.
  • Sun Jun 29:  Staid at home all day. Dave McCormick and Al here to dinner. Went down to Whitneys in the evening. [margin: Sallie died.]
  • Mon Jun 30:  Got ready to go to the saw mill and started after dinner. Got as far as Steam Boat pass and camped on the beach.
  • Tue Jul 01:  Got to the saw mill at 2 oclock and loaded the scow. Slept in the saw mill and went to bed at 10 P.M.
  • Wed Jul 02:  Started for home at 5 oclock. Got home at 6 oclock. Took breakfast with the sock sower Johnson.
  • Thu Jul 03:  Did not feel very well this morning. Got up at 9 oclock. Unloaded the lumber & sawed up the s??? lumber. Took the scow home and gave Callahan the oars I made.
  • Fri Jul 04:  Rained all day. Staid at home. Mr Upson here to dinner. Whitneys folks went to Anacortes.

1879 Jul 5-Aug 2

Image Id: 002-0417
Transcription
  • Sat Jul 05:  Rain most all day. Did not do much of anything.
  • Sun Jul 06:  Pleasant. Staid at home. Darcy was here.
  • Mon Jul 07:  George & I worked on the barn. Mrs Powell here washing. Pleasant.
