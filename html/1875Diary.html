1874 Dec 18-Jan 6, 1875

Image Id: 002-0230
Transcription
  • Fri Dec 18:  Mr. Allen staid all night here last night. Very pleasant day. W. staked out some ditches in the afternoon. T. & S. topped out dyke & worked on the garden ditch some. Highbarger came up last night. [margin: Fine weather]
  • Sat Dec 19:  Whitney did not work, was lame yet. T. & S. worked on the garden ditch. [margin: Pleasant day]
  • Sun Dec 20:  All stayed at home all day. Mr Best called on us in the afternoon.
  • Mon Dec 21:  S. dig ditch. The rest of the folks went to the Grange.
  • Tue Dec 22:  W. & Wife went to Whatcom today on the steamer. T brought the hog home from Calhouns. S. dug ditch.
  • Wed Dec 23:  T. & S. ditch down on the Siegfred's line.
  • Thu Dec 24:  T. & S. went to Whatcom. Started at 11 oclock.and arrived at 5 P.M. Rained most all day. Had a fair wind part of the day. All went to Linder's Ball in the evening and stat all night at the Whatcom House.
  • Fri Dec 25:  Christmas. Spent the day pleasantly and took dinner with Mr. Allen's folks at Mr. Jordan's. [margin: very pleasant day.]
  • Sat Dec 26:  All went to Mr. Eldridge's and took dinner & spent the eve. Had a good time.
  • Sun Dec 27:  Spent the day in various places and all spent the eve at McNutts house.
  • Mon Dec 28:  Attended the Whatcom Grange. Wen to Eldridge and got some seedling trees. Took dinner at Mrs. Smiths and wen to a party at Mr. Jordan in the eve.
  • Tue Dec 29:  All went over to Sehome and took dinner with Dr. Barrow's folks. Went to the wharf and saw the Germania land. Spent the eve at the Doctors' and then to births on the steamer Libby.
  • Wed Dec 30:  Reached Fidalgo at daylight and took breakfast with Mr. Best's folks. Then started for home where we arrived a little after noon. S. went to Balls after the mail.
  • Thu Dec 31:  Staid around home all day, and didn't do much of anything. Clowdy day, we're getting ready for New Year's.
  • Fri Jan 01:  All went to the party at LaConner for the benefit of Everet(?). Very unpleasant day rained most all day and it was also very windy.
  • Sat Jan 02:  Came home just after dinner. Had aa nice sail home. rained some. Did not do anything for the rest of the day.
  • Sun Jan 03:  Very sleepy day. Nothing transpired worth recording. Ball's pigs are very troublesome.
  • Mon Jan 04:  Rain most all day did not do much but sit around the house. W. worked at his accounts. [margin: Rain 6 hours]
  • Tue Jan 05:  Went to the Grange at LaConner. Staid all night and went to the Good Templars in the eve. Very pleasant. [margin: Cold & frosty]
  • Wed Jan 06:  Came home at noon. Went up in the woods & cut wood in the afternoon.

1875 Jan 7-27

Image Id: 002-0231
Transcription
  • Thu Jan 07:  Staid around the house and did not do much. S. filed saw some. [margin: 6 in of snow this morning. Mercury down to 16°]
  • Fri Jan 08:  Dito yesterday.
  • Sat Jan 09:  T. Yoked up the oxen and hauled up some oats and some drags. S. went up to Trimbles and got his hair cut. then went out to the Frenchmen's and took dinner.
  • Sun Jan 10:  Snowed went all night. and all day today. Staid in the house all day.
  • Mon Jan 11:  Snow 11 inches deep this morning, and pretty cole. Staid in house as usual. S. not very well. T. went up to Ball's drove some of his cattle home.
  • Tue Jan 12:  Very cold this morning. The Frenchmen went to the station to day. T & McNutt went to look for the cattle. Did not find them. [margin: Mercury 11°]
  • Wed Jan 13:  The Frenchmen came back to day and brought the mail. Bob found the cattle and drove them home. s. went up to Trimbles & staid most all day went down to the slough at night and saw the ice in great granularitys(?).
  • Thu Jan 14:  Staid in the house as usual. McClellan & Ball called in also Edward. No sign of warm weather. [margin: Mercury at 8°]
  • Fri Jan 15:  All quiet. Non one doing anything except trying to keep warm. [margin: Mercury down to 14°.]
  • Sat Jan 16:  Clear & frosty. S. went down to Ball's got home 10 P.M. [margin: Mercury at 10°]
  • Sun Jan 17:  No sign of warmer weather. Mrs. Ball called in in the afternoon. Got a sing & read one of Beecher's sermons in the evening. [margin: Mer at 12°]
  • Mon Jan 18:  W. & S. sawed wood with Trimble's saw. T. chopped wood out by the shed. McClellan came down after the mail. [margin:Mer at 10°]
  • Tue Jan 19:  Snowed last night and most all day today.
  • Wed Jan 20:  Warmer today. The snow melting fast. Killed the Hog. He weighed 300 lbs.
  • Thu Jan 21:  Cut up the pork and carved a piece to Trimble and gave some to the Frenchmen. [margin: Rain most all day.]
  • Fri Jan 22:  Plenty of snow on the ground yet. Spent the day cutting pole for wood.
  • Sat Jan 23:  T. hauled wood. W. filed the saw. W. & S. sawed wood in the afternoon in the afternoon. warm & pleasant.
  • Sun Jan 24:  All staid at home all day. Edward called on us and gave us a piece of elk. [margin: Snowed a good part of the day.]
  • Mon Jan 25:  All worked in the wood shed in the fore noon, afternoon T. cut wood & W. & S. began making rails. [margin: 150 S.]
  • Tue Jan 26:  Mr. Potter brought up the Mail today. W. & S. made rails, T. hauled wood. J.S. Conner called in the afternoon. [margin: 300 S. 30 D.]
  • Wed Jan 27:  Made stakes all day. T. hauled wood all day. Began to snow just night. [margin: 400 S.]

1875 Jan 28-Feb 20

Image Id: 002-0232
Transcription
  • Thu Jan 28:  W & S made rails out on the McCollum claim. T. cut wood.
  • Fri Jan 29:  Same as yesterday. The snow is very bad in the woods. Weather good.
  • Sat Jan 30:  T. hauled wood drags &c. to day W & S made rails as usual.
  • Sun Jan 31:  All staid at home. McNutt & Siegfred's folks called in the afternoon.
  • Mon Feb 01:  W. & S made rails. T. began hauling out to the bank by the McCollum house. got along first rate.
  • Tue Feb 02:  W. & S. went to LaConner attended the Grange & Lodge. T. hauled rails. Had to haul boat over the ice. McClellan went with us.
  • Wed Feb 03:  T. hauled rails. W. & S. got home at noon. S. sharpened stakes in the afternoon. W. filed saw. [margin: The Frenchmen found their house burned to night.]
  • Thu Feb 04:  W. & S. made rails. T. hauled rails out on the marsh.
  • Fri Feb 05:  Worked in the shop making pins for fence. [margin: Rainy all day.]
  • Sat Feb 06:  Very pleasant day. W. & S. made rails. T hauled rails out of the afternoon.
  • Sun Feb 07:  Very pleasant. McNutt call in the afternoon. All staid at home all day. Read a sermon in the eve.
  • Mon Feb 08:  Worked in the shop and in the wood shed. W. began making a harrow, cut wood and made pins. [margin: Rained most of the day.]
  • Tue Feb 09:  T. hauled rails. W. & S. split the same.
  • Wed Feb 10:  Highbarger arrived this morning with the scow & his goods. T. helped him haul up his things and mened the road some. W. & S. split rails. Fine Weather, snow gone.
  • Thu Feb 11:  W. & S. made rails, T. hauled rails and delivered them on the ground ready for fence. All but S. went to Ball's in the evening. Jenkins folks were here in the afternoon.
  • Fri Feb 12:  W. & S. made rails. T. hauled them out.
  • Sat Feb 13:  Very pleasant weather. W. & S. made rails. S. made some shakes. T. hauled rails as usual. McCormick staid all night. [margin: Mrs. Canfield died.]
  • Sun Feb 14:  W. & wife went to the station to go to the funeral. Pleasant day. S. went around the dyke in the eve and found a gate washed away. Shot a duck.
  • Mon Feb 15:  S. made rails & went down to fix the box. T made ???? down the bank.
  • Tue Feb 16:  S. went to the Grange. T hauled rails.
  • Wed Feb 17:  Came home from the station at noon. S. filed saw in the afternoon and W. made stake. T. hauled rails.
  • Thu Feb 18:  T. handled rails. W. & A. split & sawed rails. Bob White's folks and Mrs. Ball called in the afternoon. [margin: Pleasant weather. Found Nona & her calf.]
  • Fri Feb 19:  Work the same as yesterday.
  • Sat Feb 20:  Made & handled rails until 3 P.M. worked on harrow the rest of the day. [margin: Rainy in afternoon.]

1875 Feb 21-Mar 17

Image Id: 002-0233
Transcription
  • Sun Feb 21:  Very pleasant day. Mr. McClelland called in the afternoon. Mr. Conner & wife & Mrs. O'Laughlin cause just night and staid all night.
  • Mon Feb 22:  Conners folks went home this morning. made & hauled rails as usual.
  • Tue Feb 23:  Made & hauled rails as usual.
  • Wed Feb 24:  Made rails in the fore noon. Worked on harrow & cut & split wood. [margin: Rain in afternoon.]
  • Thu Feb 25:  Made & hauled out rails. Siegfred had the scow. [margin: Rainy till 9 A.M.]
  • Fri Feb 26:  Work the same as yesterday. [margin: Found 6 small pigs.]
  • Sat Feb 27:  Rainy all night. High wind from N.W.
  • Sun Feb 28:  McNutt was here in the afternoon. W. & S. took a walk on the ranch. Found it wet.
  • Mon Mar 01:  S. sawed rail cuts and sharpened stakes. W. dug ditch. Scowed up the lumber for roller. T. hauled brush down in the road. [margin: Cold.]
  • Tue Mar 02:  W. & S. went to Grange. Staid all night & went to the lodge also. T. hauled rails. [margin: Wet]
  • Wed Mar 03:  Got home from station at noon. Had rough water and got wet. dug ditch and sawed rail cut in afternoon. T. hauled rails as usual. [margin: Windy weather]
  • Thu Mar 04:  W. & S. finished opening the ditch on Siegfred line. T. hauled rails. Set oout some ??te flowering carrots.
  • Fri Mar 05:  S. sawed rail cuts in the fore noon and worked in the roller in the afternoon. T. hauled rails & cut wood. W. dug ditch and floated the roller lumber up to Allen's house.
  • Sat Mar 06:  S. sawed rail cuts in the fore noon, dug ditch in the afternoon. W. dig ditch all day. T. hauled lumber & rails.
  • Sun Mar 07:  Quite pleasant. All staid at home to day. McClelland came down in the afternoon.
  • Mon Mar 08:  W. & S. made rails. S hauled rails.
  • Tue Mar 09:  W. & S. made rails in the A.M. and worked on the roller in the P.M. T. hauled rails & sawed wood. [margin: T. planted some tomatos. Rain 4 hs.]
  • Wed Mar 10:  W. & S. made rails till 4 P.M. Rain. T. hauled rails. Matson staid all night with us.
  • Thu Mar 11:  W. & S. made rails in fore noon and worked on roller in P.M. T. hauled rails and cut wood. N.W. wind all night. [margin: Rain 4 hours]
  • Fri Mar 12:  W. & S. made rails and T. hauled rails. Windy.
  • Sat Mar 13:  Dito yesterday. S. made shakes in the P.M. Pleasant.
  • Sun Mar 14:  Cool & windy. S went down on the marsh and found some plank. Edward called in the ebe and got the necul(??).
  • Mon Mar 15:  W. & S. made rails and shakes. T. dug ditch. [margin: rain in the eve.]
  • Tue Mar 16:  W. & S. made rails. Finished out in the woods. T. dug ditch.
  • Wed Mar 17:  S. made rails down towrds the barn. W. & T. dug ditch. Mrs. Ball came down in the P.M. [margin: Pleasant. Cold frosty mornings(?)]

1875 Mar 18-Apr 8

Image Id: 002-0234
Transcription
  • Thu Mar 18:  S. put the harrow together & painted it. Made rails in the P.M. W. & T. dug ditch. Rainy at night.
  • Fri Mar 19:  W. & T. ug ditch. S. made rails. Sheriff Allen staid all night with us. Land near barn is very wet. [margin: Cold & Windy.]
  • Sat Mar 20:  S. made rail. W. & T. dug ditch.
  • Sun Mar 21:  McClelland, D'Arcy, Williamson, Mrs. Ball & Mrs. D'Arcy called on us. Williamson staid all night. [r. margin: W. & S. ????d of some month(?)] [l. margin: Hard N.W. wind.]
  • Mon Mar 22:  All dug dithc. S. went down to sow seed but it was too windy. D'Arcy got wind bound and had to wait till afternoon.
  • Tue Mar 23:  W. & S. went to the Grange & Guss also. T. staid at home and hauled wood & beets. [margin: Cold]
  • Wed Mar 24:  W. & S. could not get home heavy blow from S.E. S. went and staid with Williamson. T. hauled wood & split wood.
  • Thu Mar 25:  W. & S. went to D'Arcy's and at night went home. Wind blew pretty hard all day. Very cold and disagreeable.
  • Fri Mar 26:  S. worked on roller and pruned the trees. also put kerosene on them. Found lots of bours and blight. W. & T. dug ditch.
  • Sat Mar 27:  Dug ditch and worked on the rollers. T. hauled up a load of stuff from the slough and a plank for the roller tongue.
  • Sun Mar 28:  Cold N.W. squalls all day. Had no callers. T. went up to Ball's in afternoon.
  • Mon Mar 29:  Snow all the fore noon. W. & T. split wood. S. worked on harrow & roller. In the A.M. sowed grass seed, a & W. & T. dug ditch. [margin: The marsh is full of water.]
  • Tue Mar 30:  Worked on the ditch to day. Quite pleasant. Jake had the cattle 5 mins.
  • Wed Mar 31:  Worked on the ditch till 4 P.M. when it rained. W. went to Balls' for the mail. [margin: Jake had the cattle.]
  • Thu Apr 01:  Rain in the fore noon. T. hauled beets in the afternoon. W. & S. dug ditch.
  • Fri Apr 02:  All went to LaConner to Dance. S. made a pair of oars. & W fixed boat in fore noon. [margin: Clowdy & rainy.]
  • Sat Apr 03:  Did not get home from LaConner until 5 P.M. Work -- N.W. squalls in afternoon. Andrews came home with us.
  • Sun Apr 04:  All staid at home. Andrews went home in the afternoon. Clear & windy. [margin: Very hard frost this morning.]
  • Mon Apr 05:  Dug ditch and burned the grass off the marsh. Had a road meeting at Trimble and elected Highbarger. Read minutes. S. went to the station at 3 oclock.
  • Tue Apr 06:  T. hauled up manure on the garden. S. got home from station at 9 oclock. W. & S. dug ditch. T marsh is drying off fast. [margin: Cold & clear with north wind.]
  • Wed Apr 07:  T. hauled manure all day. and a load of beets at night. W. & S. finished the roller and harrow. Rainy at night.
  • Thu Apr 08:  W. & S. dug ditch in the fore noon. Afternoon parted potatoes. T. hauled manure and bgan to plow the garden. Clear & pleasant and little warmer.

1875 Apr 9-Apr 27

Image Id: 002-0235
Transcription
  • Fri Apr 09:  All worked in the garden plowing, harrowing & planting. Planted potatos, onion, beets, turnips and set out seed roots. Made a cold frame. [margin: Frost]
  • Sat Apr 10:  Very warm & pleasant. Worked in garden all day, plowing & planting. Sowed the Bennett Rygrass on the E. side of orchard.
  • Sun Apr 11:  T. & S. went to the station and also to church. in the afternoon went up to Calhoun's to church. the rest staid at home. [margin: Very pleasant weather.]
  • Mon Apr 12:  Began sowing oats on the marsh. Set out some carrots and strawberries &c. T. harrowed on the marsh.
  • Tue Apr 13:  Ball & Trimble brought the mail. Worked in the garden and in the marsh. Set out some plant on the marsh, currants & B berries also. Matson was here all night. [margin: planted mangolds.]
  • Wed Apr 14:  S. grafted some root grafts. T. harrowed in oats. and set out apple seedlings. Planted some beans &c.
  • Thu Apr 15:  T. harrowed in oars & W. & S. dug ditch. S. went down & staid on his claim and took some oats up along the dyke to sow. [margin: very pleasant weather.]
  • Fri Apr 16:  S. dug ditch in the forenoon and graded slough banks in the afternoon. W. sowed grain and dug ditch. T. harrowed in the grain. [margin: nice weather.]
  • Sat Apr 17:  Dug ditch, graded sloughs and harrowed in grain. Had 13 acres sowed.
  • Sun Apr 18:  Little rainy in the morning All staid at home. S. went up to Balls in the evening. Had company from Fidalgo. Bests, Whites & Howards. [margin: Peas, beets, onions, lettuce, turnips &c coming up. Planted sweet corn.]
  • Mon Apr 19:  Whitney sowed oats. S. graded sloughs and T. harrowed as usual.
  • Tue Apr 20:  W. & S. went to the Grange. T. harrowed in oats. S. went home with D'Arcy and then to Wilchers after the scow. [margin: oats coming up.]
  • Wed Apr 21:  W. came home from LaConner this morning. T. harrowed in oats. W. sowed oats. S. & D'Arcy got home at night. Too much wind to get the scow of wood.
  • Thu Apr 22:  D'Arcy & S. grade slough banks. T. harrowed in oats. W. sowed oats and worked around the house some. Rain in the evening. [margin: Rain a considerable last night.]
  • Fri Apr 23:  Graded sloughs, sowed oats & harrowed, Planted potatos some and in the afternoon went with D'Arcy after the scow; got to his place at 12 oclock at night. Had a hard time.
  • Sat Apr 24:  Came home from D'Arcy's before breakfast. Sharpened stakes, sowed oats, harrowed in oats, planted potatoes, sweet corn and hoed strawberries.
  • Sun Apr 25:  S. & T. went over to Fidalgo to White's and Best's. Came home at 10 o'clock and took some oats up along the dyke to sow. The cougars are going for Ball's cattle. [margin: very pleasant.]
  • Mon Apr 26:  S. sharpened stakes. W. sowed grain. Harrowed in oats as usual. fine weather.
  • Tue Apr 27:  S. sharpened posts and made a gate. W & wife went to the station. T. harrowed in oats. [margin: Light frost.]

1875 Apr 28-May 17

Image Id: 002-0236
Transcription
  • Wed Apr 28:  W. & S. began making fence. Made 25 rods. after ten oclock. T. harrowed oats. The first sowed oats are up and looking fine. Dr. Meggs was here fixing teeth for Gus.
  • Thu Apr 29:  Made fence, sowed grain and harrowed. Dr. Meggs went to Fidalgo. Gnats & mosquitos quite thick at night. About 20 acres of oats are up. Potatos are begging to come up.
  • Fri Apr 30:  W. & S. went down on the Parrish claim dyking. T. harrowing grain as usual. Fine growing weather.
  • Sat May 01:  Rain some last night. Finished sowing grain down by the barn. T. harrowed all day. W. & S. graded slough banks. [margin: Winter wheat 6 to 18 in high good color.]
  • Sun May 02:  Clowdy with some rain. Mr. Balls folks came down to dinner.
  • Mon May 03:  Rain last night. Ground to wet to harrow. T. hauled rails. W. & S. graded sloughs. S. sharpened posts in afternoon,
  • Tue May 04:  Rainy most all night & day. Sharpened posts, made fence pins, cut wood, sprouted potatos cleaned out the shop &c &c.
  • Wed May 05:  Sharpened posts. graded sloughs & hauled rails. Rain some.
  • Thu May 06:  Grading sloughs and hauling rails. [margin: Rainy.]
  • Fri May 07:  W. & S. grade sloughs in the forenoon and made fence in the afternoon. T. hauled rails. Rainy in morning. Ground very wet. Grain growing slowly.
  • Sat May 08:  Very pleasant day. Hauled rails & made fence. D'Arcy came up and helped make fence. Mr. Whites folks came over in the afternoon. Cool nights. Wilson strawberries in full bloom. [margin: Guss not well. Thinks she has the ague]
  • Sun May 09:  Pleasant day. T., Clarence(?) & Sisson went up to Balls and took dinner. Mr Whites folks staid all night. Rainy in the night.
  • Mon May 10:  Company went home this morning. Strong west wind, and the weather is cold. W. & S. made fence. T. hauled rails.
  • Tue May 11:  W. went to the station with a load of potatos. S. made fence and T. hauled rails. Cold & clowdy.
  • Wed May 12:  W. & S. worked on fence. S. hauled rails down on Trimble's line.
  • Thu May 13:  W. & S. staked out the fence from Trimbles corner to Allen slough. S. made some bridges over the sloughs & sawed & split 70 posts 8 ft long. W. made fence and T. hauled rails. [margin: Began to rain at 4 P.M.]
  • Fri May 14:  In the fore noon W. & S. transplanted some tomatos, cabbages & pear seedlings. In P.M. made fence. T. hauled rails as usual. [margin: Rainy all day.]
  • Sat May 15:  T. hauled rails as usual. W. & S. made fence. Finished on the North end. Worked on Trimble's line in the afternoon. Planted some parsnips to transplant. Ground is very wet.
  • Sun May 16:  Very pleasant day. Had no company. S. & T. went down to Welchers in P.M.
  • Mon May 17:  T. hauled rails. W. & S. made fence till noon then went to LaConner attended the Good Templars. [margin: Cold and Windy S.E.]

1875 May 18-Jun 3

Image Id: 002-0237
Transcription
  • Tue May 18:  T. hauled rails out of the timber. S. staid with McCormick last night and in fore noon went up to Pleasant Ridge. The grain looking fine excepts spring wheat which is poor. Went to the Grange in afternoon then home.
  • Wed May 19:  S. went to Balls in the morning after butter. T. hauled rails. W. & S. made fence. T. & W. began to plow the garden. [margin: Cold mornings. Frost enough to cut oats and beans some.]
  • Thu May 20:  S. began to make some gates and they began to plow. It was too wet. S. made an axhandle. Sorted & sprouted the potatos in the warehouse. and made fence some towards night. Whitney not very well. [margin: Rain last night and part of the day.]
  • Fri May 21:  W. & S. ran the fence in to Allens slough and quit. S. made a double gate by Trimblescorner. Hoed in the garden in the afternoon. T. continues to haul rails. [margin: Rain most all night last night.]
  • Sat May 22:  W. & S. hoed in the garden. Cool and clowdy. Siegfreds folks were here to dinner. T. hauled rails. [margin: Planted some Snow White Macon peas E. end of row.]
  • Sun May 23:  Very pleasant day. All staid at home. March's folks were here in the afternoon. and staid all night. [margin: Whitney had the ague.]
  • Mon May 24:  Rain most all the fore noon. T. harrowed the marsh garden. Marchs folks started to go home and had to come back. S. killed a muskrat. S. & T. planted garden stuff in the afternoon on the marsh. Onions, turnips, beets, cabbage, potatos, corn, &c.
  • Tue May 25:  T. planted cabbage & parsnips in the A.M. and hauled rails in P.M. S. cut timber for barn. W. sick not working. [margin: very pleasant weather.]
  • Wed May 26:  Same work as yesterday. Dana is unwell.
  • Thu May 27:  S. helped W. about cleaning house some and hoed in the garden in the P.M. Hewed barn timber. T. hauled rails & timber. [margin: pleasant]
  • Fri May 28:  In the A.M. W. & S. worked down to the dam. Put a box in the dyke ditch and began to make a bridge over the dyke. Fixed a place for the boat. Made some bridges for the scow for stock loading. S. hewed timber in the P.M. T. hauled timber and finished hauling rails. [margin: Pleasant weather.]
  • Sat May 29:  W. worked on the dyke bridge. T. hauled manure for Tomatos and timbers for barn. S. hewed timber for barn.
  • Sun May 30:  Cold & rainy unpleasant day. Winter wheat - stands 25" to 2 1/2 ft high. Oats 25 in to ?? - Stretched out the longest are 24 inches. Saw wild rose nearly ready to blossom. Also red clover beginning to blossom. Peas in bloom.
  • Mon May 31:  W. finished the dyke bridge and began working on the one over Allen Slough. S. hewed timber, plowed the tomato ground. and began plowing on the marsh. [margin: Trimble worked in the afternoon. ]
  • Tue Jun 01:  W. went to the station. S. finished hewing timber and began getting out bridge covering. [margin: Trimble helped plow.]
  • Wed Jun 02:  Worked on the marsh grading sloughs. Wheeling mud on bridge. Splitting bridge timber &c. Set out the tomatos just night. [margin: Trimble helped Vinz plow.]
  • Thu Jun 03:  T. finished plowing the marsh garden and hauled some bridge timber. W. & S. went down and dyked on the Parrish claim. Came home by Balls. [margin: Very cold. Rain most all last night.]

1875 Jun 4-24

Image Id: 002-0238
Transcription
  • Fri Jun 04:  W. & S. worked on the bridge across Allen Slough. T. sowed and harrowed in oats. Sowed some corn also. Very pleasant day. [margin: Had the first ripe strawberries.]
  • Sat Jun 05:  S. set out 350 pear & apple seedling on the marsh. Trimble thinned beets. T. finished harrowing oats and hauled bridge timber in P.M. W. & S. worked on bridge also. Mosquitos the thickest of the season so far. [margin: S. platned some Champion of England peas & K of suedes Turnips.]
  • Sun Jun 06:  Warm & clowdy. First rate day for mosquitos. Had no company. The grain is turning yellow and dying in spots.
  • Mon Jun 07:  W. & S. worked on the bridge. T. hauled timber.
  • Tue Jun 08:  W. went to the station. T. hauled timbers for bridges. S. worked on the big bridge and grade some sloughs in the road.
  • Wed Jun 09:  W. worked around the house in the fore noon and on bridge with S. in P.M. T. worked on bridge in forenoon and hauled timber in P.M. [margin: fine weather.]
  • Thu Jun 10:  S. went to Pickards(?). Set out some cabbages and hauled timber for bridge and ditch by Trimbles corner. Whitney no well. [margin: Trimble worked.]
  • Fri Jun 11:  Trimble hoed garden W. & S. also T. worked on the fill for "Box Slough" T. went to Fidalgo in P.M.
  • Sat Jun 12:  S. worked about the house and garden in A.M. and cleaning out spring in P.M. W. & T. worked on the fill in the Box Slough in forenoon and on the spring in the P.M. Set out some tomatoes & cabbage. [margin: Had company in the eve Mr. Balls folks, Miss Eldridge, Mrs Smith Miss White.]

  • Mon Dec 13:  W. & S. were at LaConner. T at home. Pleasant day.
  • Tue Dec 14:  W. & S. came home from LaConner at noon. [margin: Hard rain last night.]
  • Wed Dec 15:  All graded slough on the Allen "80". [margin: Pleasant day.]
  • Thu Dec 16:  Graded sloughs as usual. Frost.
  • Fri Dec 17:  Graded sloughs on the Allen "eighty".
  • Sat Dec 18:  W. had a boil. Did not work. S. & T. worked around the shop and split wood. [margin: Rainy in the fore noon.]
  • Sun Dec 19:  Rainy & Clowdy. Spent the day reading & writing.
  • Mon Dec 20:  Rainy. Ball came down and we kill the old Cow. "Allens Cow" Dressed 684. Cut wood in the fore noon.
  • Tue Dec 21:  W. went to the station. S. & T. packed meat. cut wood, graded sloughs, &c. [margin: Rainy in A.M.]
  • Wed Dec 22:  W. came home from the station in the P.M. S. & T. graded sloughs on the Allen "80".
  • Thu Dec 23:  All graded slough in forenoon. S. drove stakes near Balls a land - of the time. A little after noon the Skagit being very high broke through over Balls dyke in two or three places and begun to flood us. We piled up all our rails on a mile of fence that was to be so the water would not sealthe(?) them. Indian slough was not bank full at the preemption though the water must have been nearly three feet deep at McNutts old place.
  • Fri Dec 24:  The Skagit is going down quite rapidly - Water on the ranch from 1 ft to 18 inches. T. & W. helped Ball kill hogs - 11 hd in about 4 1/2 hours w 5 of us- Sisson started for station.
  • Sat Dec 25:  T. & W. wife & boys went to Balls for Christmas dinner, went horse back - Skagit clear down and water running out of holes in dyke. [margin: Albert Leamer & Martha Hancock married.]
  • Sun Dec 26:  Water still lowering inside dyke. [margin: Rain.]
  • Mon Dec 27:  Rainy. W. & T. choring around. S. at the station.
  • Tue Dec 28:  S. at the station, W. & T. moving blacksmith shop.
  • Wed Dec 29:  Cut wood and worked in the shop some. [margin: High tide. ]

1875 Dec 30-Jan 21, 1876

  • Thu Dec 30:  T. hauled wood and bark. S. sowed & split wood. W. piled bark and graded sloughs.
  • Fri Dec 31:  Very hard wind all last night & rain. W. & T. dug ditch on the Allen "80". N.W. gale & rain in the afternoon. S. sawed wood and pin timber. [margin: Heavy rain last night.]
  • Sat Jan 01:  T. oiled harness. W. & S. made pin, sawed wood, & worked on the shop.
  • Sun Jan 02:  All staid at home. Mr D'Arcys folks, Mrs. Ball, Arthur & Clarence White were here to dinner.
  • Mon Jan 03:  All hands went to the Grand Ball at LaConner. Rained part of the way there and we got pretty wet. Had a good time. [margin: Rainy.]
  • Tue Jan 04:  Arrived home from Ball at noon. Feeling pretty sleepy.
  • Wed Jan 05:  All went down on Allen's line ditching, it is pretty wet. Went down to fix the box by the warehouse at 12 midnight. Too much tide.
  • Thu Jan 06:  Ditching the same as yesterday. Went & fixed the box at 9 oclock. [margin: Cold & Rainy]
  • Fri Jan 07:  Vinz packed the meat over. W. & S. ditching on South "80". T. working on the road. Ditching. Nice day.
  • Sat Jan 08:  Ditching the same as yesterday. [margin: Pleasant day.]
  • Sun Jan 09:  All staid at home. S. went around the dyke and found the "50 ft" slough box open. Went down and fixed it at night. Ball call in the P.M. Am reading "We and our Night on's" [margin: Clear & Frosty night]
  • Mon Jan 10:  S. worked on the road 1/2 day, cutting tree our of the road and sawed word the balance of the day. T. hauled wood. W. worked about the house in the A.M. and went to LaConner in the P.M. [margin: clear & cold]
  • Tue Jan 11:  S. sawed wood and made shakes in the P.M. T. Hauled mangolds. W. at LaConner. [margin: Mercury at 29°]
  • Wed Jan 12:  S. made shakes. T. hauled rails & wood. W. at LaConner.
  • Thu Jan 13:  S. made shakes. Have 800 made. W. & T. dug ditch. T on the road. S. & T. went to Fidalgo Island staid all night at Mr. Bests house. [margin: Clear & pleasant.]
  • Fri Jan 14:  S. & T. went to Whites and then over to Graham's deer hunting. W. done chores, was writing &c. [margin: Rain.]
  • Sat Jan 15:  S. & T. at Graham's. W. dug ditch.
  • Sun Jan 16:  S. & T. came over to Sully's and staid all night at White's.
  • Mon Jan 17:  T. & S. got home at noon. W. dug ditch in the AM. T. worked on road ditching in P.M. and W. & S. were on the Parish claim dyking.
  • Tue Jan 18:  W. & S. worked ditching. In the P.M. worked down on the Parish chain dyking. T. worked on the road. [margin: Mrs. & Miss Leamer came down with Highbarger. Snowed all day.]
  • Wed Jan 19:  S. made pin for fence. W. worked in shop making pins &c. T. oiled harness. Keany(?) was here; his boat was wrecked last night or rather the cargo lost.
  • Thu Jan 20:  W. & S. worked on the Parrish claim. T. worked on the road.
  • Fri Jan 21:  All worked on the Parrish claim dyking. Very cold & disagreeable weather for dyking.
