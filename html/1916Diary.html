1916 Jan 1-9

Image Id: 001-0509
Transcription
  • Sat Jan 01:  This has been a very quiet uneventful day. Ida down stairs a while. Mr & Mrs William, Mr Cloyd, & Dr. Frost called. The latter to give Ida a medical treatment. Frank & Grant not back from Seattle. I wonder what all this year will bring forth besides election.
  • Sun Jan 02:  Ida poorly as usual. Temp 20° Clear & pleasant. Went to S.S. & church in the A.M. Dr. Burdon here this eve. Ida went over to Wright's to stay to night and while I am gone to Mt. Vernon. Coldest for two years.
  • Mon Jan 03:  Temp 30 Snow & north wind most of the day. Went to Mount Vernon to Coms Meeting.
  • Tue Jan 04:  Temp 20° In Mt. Vernon. Home on the noon Train. Clear & cold all day. Ida came home feeling no better. Dr. Frost here this eve. 22° at 9 P.M.
  • Wed Jan 05:  Temp 26° Snow showers to day. Down town & settled bill & home the rest of the time working.
  • Thu Jan 06:  Ida had a bad night. Very blue this A.M. Temp 35° & more snow last night & this A.M.
  • Fri Jan 07:  At home about all day. Dr. Caprow here also Mrs. Hudson and Dr. Frost. I about the same.
  • Sat Jan 08:  Rain some last night & this A.M. Choring & fussing about the house. Temp 37° Went for eggs.
  • Sun Jan 09:  Ida's cold worse. Went to S.S. & church in the morning. Rain & snow. Disagreeable day. [margin: Heard that W. F. Robinson died this A.M. in Pasadena.]

1916 Jan 10-28

Image Id: 001-0510
Transcription
  • Mon Jan 10:  Temp 20 at 7 A.M. & 17 at 9 P.M. Fierce north wind all day. Ida's cold pretty bad. Mercury at 17 in some places. Grew cold very fast last night. Got up at 3 this A.M. to let the water out of the car.
  • Tue Jan 11:  Dr. Frost here. Temp 13° this A.M. 8° at the farm. Coldest since Jan 13-1910(?) Pipes frozen both here and at Wrights.
  • Wed Jan 12:  Temp 26°& four inches more snow. Ida worse. Mr. Robinson's body & folks came to day from California.
  • Thu Jan 13:  Temp 26° at A.M. & 23° at 9 P.M. Ida had a bad night. Self went to Mr. Robinson's funeral as one of the honorary Pall bearers. Funeral at 7:30 P.M. Cold N.E. wind. Reading "Coniston". Mr. McPhail present this eve.
  • Fri Jan 14:  Temp 20° Clear & cold with steady north wind. Frank White & Margurite here. Went down town & got the water pipe fixed. M. staid all night.
  • Sat Jan 15:  Temp 22° Mrs. White here fixing dresses. Self choring & downtown. [margin: Hilda sick.]
  • Sun Jan 16:  Pleasant Temp. 26 ° 36 at 2 P.M. Above freezing for the first time since last Sunday. Went to S.S. & church. Mrs White at Abby's & here in the eve.
  • Mon Jan 17:  Temp. 32 here & 19 at the ranch. Clear & pleasant. Went to the Ranch & also La C. and Bay View. Roads hard but rough. Bot the P???? Tire at Blades. Oats worth $25.00 now. Grant loading a car with straw.
  • Tue Jan 18:  Temp 24° Clear & pleasant At home.
  • Wed Jan 19:  Clear till 3 P.M. then clowdy with S.E. wind. Rev Wilson over looking after paupers. I took him out to 29 St. Went to P. Meeting and too the Williams.
  • Thu Jan 20:  Temp 38° with S Wind. Rain some. At home - Reading "The Grey Dawn" snow at night.
  • Fri Jan 21:  Temp 36. showers & snow & rain - Cleaned out the tank & went downtown in the P.M.
  • Sat Jan 22:  Strong S.E. wind all day. Temp 50. Choring around & downtown. Winter broken
  • Sun Jan 23:  Rain last night. Temp 33° night 30. Fierce N.W. wind all day. Went to chruch & S.S. in the A.M. The Whitneys & Earnie Alexander to Wrights for dinner. Ida & self also. Heavy west wind all day.
  • Mon Jan 24:  Temp 24° Took some laundry to Mrs. Linbeck at home the bal. of the day. Reading &c.
  • Tue Jan 25:  Temp 32° Four inches more snow. At home & down town & reading. Dr. Frost here.
  • Wed Jan 26:  28° at 9 P.M. Temp 29° and 3 1/2 inches more snow. This is the longest spell of winter since 1907.
  • Thu Jan 27:  Temp 24° Clear & cold with N. wind. Ida complaining again yesterday & to day. Home all day reading & choring.
  • Fri Jan 28:  Temp 24 - 18 at the Ranch Self & Frank went over to Grant's in the Ford. Fine sleighing for those that have a way to use it. [margin: Rough & difficult autoing]
