1888 Dec 28-Jan 7, 1889

Image Id: 002-0601
Transcription
  • Fri Dec 28:  Lovely weather. Dyking in the A.M. and went up to Ball's in the P.M.
  • Sat Dec 29:  Dyking. Bob Woodburn helping me. Still fine weather with light frosts.
  • Sun Dec 30:  Still frosty. Mercury at 25. Clowdy & signs of snow.
  • Mon Dec 31:  Another beautiful day. Mercury at 24 this P.M. Dyking some & in the P.M. went up and got Maud, Tillinghast went with me. The cross dyke is no account water up in the orchard to day. Sent a big letter off to George this A.M. 12 pages.
  • Tue Jan 01:  Fine weather doing chores. Shot 2 duck. Very quiet New Year day.
  • Wed Jan 02:  Began work on a small scow and some ??? chores. Frosty weather & higher tides. Water coming through the dyke pretty bad.
  • Thu Jan 03:  Finished the scow. The carrots are spoiling with salt water. Mrs Ball & Globe here to dinner. Whitney went to Fidalgo Island this eve. Kate also.
  • Fri Jan 04:  Not doing much by chores. Highest tide for 2 weeks. Pretty well filled up inside. Dave Fulks cross dyke broke in several places and flooded him again. Got a bill from Martin & Gaches this P.M. Dug the parsnips this P.M. Bebber has worked 27 days besides the dam & Whitneys cross dyke.
  • Sat Jan 05:  Pretty high tide to day & some rain. Not doing much but chores.
  • Sun Jan 06:  Reading "Mask Torn Off". High tide again today. Warm & pleasant. The inside dyke still islands.
  • Mon Jan 07:  Pleasant. The Hulls came to day. Was down to the dam this P.M. with Whitney. Grandma started for W.I. Dug carrots & put down carpet. Light frost this A.M.

1889 Jan 8-19

Image Id: 002-0602
Transcription
  • Tue Jan 08:  Dug carrots & put down the carpet & spanded(?) room. Whitney had 2 teams plowing today.
  • Wed Jan 09:  Not doing much but choring and hunting ducks. Shot 5.
  • Thu Jan 10:  Was down to Whitneys most of the day running engine to grind! Pretty hard rain today.
  • Fri Jan 11:  Choring. Hauled some straw & wood. Rainy P.M. Tides not high this week.
  • Sat Jan 12:  Went to town. Paid bills at P. Polsons, butcher's bill and Jorgensons also Goldsmiths.
  • Sun Jan 13:  At home all day. Finished my letter to Father and read some. Pleasant but not very cold.
  • Mon Jan 14:  Was at Whitneys running engine to grind feed. Peter came down this P.M. to hunt.
  • Tue Jan 15:  Mercury at 26 this A.M. & last night. Grinding feed again at Whitneys. Peter hunting. Clear & cool. Was down to Tillinghasts this eve.
  • Wed Jan 16:  Went hunting this A.M. with Peter and done chores in the P.M. Peter went home this P.M. with a doz. ducks. I shot 5 today. John Peth got hurt.
  • Thu Jan 17:  Done chores. Dug carrots & went up to Balls. Warmer with south wind all day. Water up into the orchard to day again. Tides not overly but in all day from daylight till dark.
  • Fri Jan 18:  Done chores and went up to Bob Woodburns in the P.M. and out to look at timber claim with John Ball.
  • Sat Jan 19:  Went to Mt Vernon to witness for Bob Woodburn. Walked all the way and got home at 5 P.M. Paid taxes and got Miller's deed recorded.

1889 Jan 20-31

Image Id: 002-0603
Transcription
  • Sun Jan 20:  At home all day. Rainy some. Tillinghast and Mr. Hull up and spent the eve.
  • Mon Jan 21:  Doing chores. Rainy part of the day. Put down moulding(?) in the parlor in the P.M. Had a letter from D.M. Leamer.
  • Tue Jan 22:  Was up to Ball's in the A.M. and made him a proposition to swap land. Clowdy and warm like spring. Grass starting.
  • Wed Jan 23:  Got up to go to town but did not on act. of wind. Rainy part of the day. Choring about and dug carrots.
  • Thu Jan 24:  Working in the shop some making eave troughs for the back porch and painting bracket &c. Rainy A.M.
  • Fri Jan 25:  Went to town and got home at 12.30. Lovely weather. Putting down oil cloth and putting up picture rails in P.M. Whitney came home from Seattle to day. Wrote to Geo. Sisson and sent for L H C for Mrs Miller.
  • Sat Jan 26:  Done the chores. Put up an eave trough and conductor on kitchen porch. Sold timber land to John Ball for $300. Henderson & Shields here this Eve. Still lovely weather. Dug some more carrots.
  • Sun Jan 27:  Another lovely day. Frost this A.M. 27 °. Shields & Henderson started home after dinner. McKennas folks here this A.M. to get the wardrobe.
  • Mon Jan 28:  Still pleasant. Frost this A.M. 28°. Made a foot rest and put up pictures, split wood, &c.
  • Tue Jan 29:  Painted porch posts and put up the brackets, done chores. Water up into the orchard again. Alf Stacey here last night & this A.M. Pleasant and no rain.
  • Wed Jan 30:  Dug a scow load of carrots. Another fine day. Working about the house & shop.
  • Thu Jan 31:  Rainy some. Working in the shop making front gate posts.

1889 Feb 1-11

Image Id: 002-0604
Transcription
  • Fri Feb 01:  Warm with strong south wind. Working in shop painting & ???ting pickets for porch. Canted the gate posts also.
  • Sat Feb 02:  Painting and digging carrots. Otto here to see about supervision(?) business. Warm & pleasant. Grass getting quite green.
  • Sun Feb 03:  Another beautiful day and warm. Mercury at 46 this A.M. Started to go to church at Bay View but could not get there. Tide was out.
  • Mon Feb 04:  Putting moulding around the porch posts and painting them. Whitneys men began on the dyke. Two men here from Skagit to buy horses. Fine day.
  • Tue Feb 05:  Sold Rock, Dol. and Kate and the old harness for $140. Worked on front gate some in the P.M. Fine weather.
  • Wed Feb 06:  Made honey and painted front gate. Pleasant. Whitneys team plowing the past 3 days.
  • Thu Feb 07:  Went up to Balls and got Star and brought him home, Found Ball's men plowing with 2 teams. Painted on the balesters for Porch.
  • Fri Feb 08:  Finished painting the porch, fixing and painted the gate - second coat.
  • Sat Feb 09:  There has been no water over my ranch for the past 2 days. Lovely weather. Grass growing lovely. also in the AM took the scow down to the school house after noon set out a spruce tree and done roses. Began making a horse of Star.
  • Sun Feb 10:  Another pleasant day. Warmer than last 60°. Had bad cold. Mr Hull & T's folks here this P.M.
  • Mon Feb 11:  Doing chores & cutting wood. Was fixing fence ome and down to the dam. Gordon here in the evening show his collection of Australian curiosities. W. & T's folks here also in the eve.

1889 Feb 12-23

Image Id: 002-0605
Transcription
  • Tue Feb 12:  Choring & cutting some wood. Wrote to Geo in the eve. Tide over the ranch again.
  • Wed Feb 13:  Not doing much but chores. Raining some and a cold wind. Made Grants wind man and fixed a cupboard for the girls.
  • Thu Feb 14:  Frost about 28. Setting fence posts and tearing down the old wood shed. Signing for 0 shares of the Farmers Insurance Co. of Seattle, also fo the Sunday P.I.
  • Fri Feb 15:  Set aw 2 letters to George and ?? oats. Mercury at 28. Clear & pleasant. Doing chores and working on the hen fence.
  • Sat Feb 16:  Frost again, about the same as yesterday. Went to the saw mill in the A.M. Making fence in the P.M. Good plow weather.
  • Sun Feb 17:  Cool and a little snow this A.M. less than 1 in. Pleasant in the P.M. T. & Mr Hull up in the P.M.
  • Mon Feb 18:  Bold raw disagreeable day with some snow, but it melted as fast as it came. Worked on the fence some and filed 2 saws.
  • Tue Feb 19:  Warmer to day. Worked at the fence some and was down to the dyke and school house in the P.M. 9 men working at the big dam.
  • Wed Feb 20:  Making a gate for the chicken fence. Began making an apron up by the hay barn. Yesterday tide not low enough.
  • Thu Feb 21:  Sent for the Voice and "sweet Cicely". Hung the gate on the chicken fence and finished the apron up by the hay barn. Rain some this A.M.
  • Fri Feb 22:  Clowdy not very pleasant weather. Made a gate latch. Done the chores and in the P.M. helped on the Allen Dam. Higher tides than we had 2 weeks ago.
  • Sat Feb 23:  The dam all night at 10 oclock this A.M. North wind and a cold mist. Worked on the dyke this P.M. Got the water nearly shut off this side.

1889 Feb 24-Mar 6

Image Id: 002-0606
Transcription
  • Sun Feb 24:  Clear & pleasant with north wind. Frost, temp 32°. Spent the day mostly reading. Hull came up and went home across the ranch. He thinks there will be some cabbage seed yet. I don't see much chance for any.
  • Mon Feb 25:  Went up to Mr Egbers to make a tank for him. C. Bowden here in the eve. South E wind all day, but good plowing weather. Nearly all the farmer are at it. Whitney & a good gang at the dyke.
  • Tue Feb 26:  working at the tank again to day, finished it. Clowdy with a S. easter but no rain.
  • Wed Feb 27:  The dyke broke again yesterday A.M. Did not do much today. Strong S.E. wind.
  • Thu Feb 28:  Went to town. Took Grandma & Mrs W. went also. Had a good trip & got home at 4 P.M.
  • Fri Mar 01:  Rain a good part of the day. Made a piece of fence by the privy and tinkered in the shop some.
  • Sat Mar 02:  Working on the dyke to day. Got the water shut off again and are in hopes for got this time. The grass in my meadow seems to be alive yet.
  • Sun Mar 03:  Nearly sick with cold. Wrote to mother. Fine weather. Was down to Whitneys a while in the P.M.
  • Mon Mar 04:  Went down to fix the sluice on the road, then threw rails, &c out of the road. Worked on picket fence in the P.M.
  • Tue Mar 05:  Went down to fix bridge again but there was too much water. Not doing much today.
  • Wed Mar 06:  Was down to the dam a little while, then came home and filed saws for W. Fine dry weather. Every body plowing.

1889 Mar 7-18

Image Id: 002-0607
Transcription
  • Thu Mar 07:  Was down on the dyke to day. Whitney not well.
  • Fri Mar 08:  Helping Whitney grind feed.
  • Sat Mar 09:  Went down to the dam and got a raft of lumber in the A.M. In the P.M. worked on the dyke again. Whitney suffering with a carbuncle. Rain some this A.M.
  • Sun Mar 10:  Was at Bay View to church. Pulliam preached. At home in the P.M. and down to Tilling. in the evening and to supper.
  • Mon Mar 11:  Was up at saw Kalso and was down on the dyke a while, and fixed engine house door in the P.M. for Whitney. Read a letter from George this P.M. Had a light rain this A.M.
  • Tue Mar 12:  Rainy again this A.M. Fixed the platform by kitchen porch. In the P.M. went over to Whitneys Island and worked on the platform at the barn.
  • Wed Mar 13:  Quite a hard rain last night. Made a tongue for the Whitney wagon & in the P.M. worked on the platform over to the Island. Rainy part of the day.
  • Thu Mar 14:  Rainy a good part of the day. Was up to see the colts in the A.M. & up helping them with the sow mill engine in the P.M.
  • Fri Mar 15:  Working on the chicken house. Laid the foundation and put the floor on.
  • Sat Mar 16:  Went to Jenne's to calk a tank. Was there all day. Pleasant day but the ground very wet. Otto at Jenne's also.
  • Sun Mar 17:  Was at home in the A.M. Mr & Mrs Sterns here in the P.M. & I went up to see the saw mill ending.e Strong S.E. wind.
  • Mon Mar 18:  Not doing much in the A.M. In the P.M. washing and oiling harness. Sent off the school census and a letter to Geo. Sisson.

  • Sun Apr 07:  Wrote to George and went to church & Sunday school at B.V.

  • Sat Apr 13:  Working at Whitneys filing saws and fixing harrows &c. Art White here to dinner. Had a shower in the eve.

1889 Apr 16-27

Image Id: 002-0610
Transcription
  • Tue Apr 16:  Working on the road 1/2 and for Whitney in the P.M. making kitchen shelfs.
  • Wed Apr 17:  Working on the Island making fence for W. wire fence.
  • Thu Apr 18:  Went to town with Whitney. Pleasant & dry. Farmers all along with seeding.
  • Fri Apr 19:  Working on the Island making fence.
  • Sat Apr 20:  Plant garden for Whitney in the A.M. some and make fence 3 hours in the P.M. Threatening rain some. Have the colts all halter broken now.
  • Sun Apr 21:  Strong S.E. wind all day and rain in the evening. At home all day.
  • Mon Apr 22:  Had a heavy rain all night & some this A.M. Working at Whitneys grinding feed and sowing timothy seed over by the Telegraph slough bridge. Whitney done seeding on this side saturday last.
  • Tue Apr 23:  Went to the Island and took Star colt over to break him in to work. He worked in all right. Came home and made a harrow tongue for Whitney. Pulliams folks here all night. Was down to Whitneys this evening to sing and visit.
  • Wed Apr 24:  Clowdy with a little rain this A.M. Pulliams folks went home this A.M. also self not feeling well with a bad cold. Went to Seaman's in the P.M. and to the saw mill.
  • Thu Apr 25:  Went to the Island and tinkered up harness for Whitney. He started for Seattle to day.
  • Fri Apr 26:  Tinkering about home and in the eve. Went up to Seaman's and got the team. Grain along the road looking fine. John Ball about done seeding. Jean colt cut her should to day some way, but uncertain how.
  • Sat Apr 27:  Started for Stanwood. Tillinghast with us. Arrived at Hancock's at 3 P.M. Pleasant trip.

  • Thu Jun 06:  Began work on Whitney's house. Bonar plowing. Working Star.
