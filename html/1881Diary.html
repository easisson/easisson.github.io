1880 Dec 31-Jan 26,1881

Image Id: 002-0439
Transcription
  • Fri Dec 31:  Rainy part of the day. The snow going off very fast. Had to go down to do Whitney's chores in the evening.
  • Sat Jan 01:  Whitney came from LaConner this morning sick. I did his chores to day. Ground very full of water but warm. Turned Whitneys horses out again across the slough.
  • Sun Jan 02:  Raining again this morning. Ida not gaining strength much. Mrs. Dick Ball came over to day.
  • Mon Jan 03:  Went down to Whitneys & got a grindstone & fixed it up in the granary.
  • Tue Jan 04:  Went to LaConner. Had a hard time. Head tide both ways.
  • Wed Jan 05:  Done chores in the fore noon & fixed an apron to the sluice box by the 45 ft slough in the P.M.
  • Thu Jan 06:  Rainy part of the time. Dug ditch in the orchard for under drain. Allen brought the boat home.
  • Fri Jan 07:  Ida worse than common to day. Dug ditch in the A.M. I fixed for killing hogs in the P.M. Fine weather.
  • Sat Jan 08:  Worked on the bridge up by Callahans road work. Pleasant frosty morning.
  • Sun Jan 09:  Staid at home all day. Rainy.
  • Mon Jan 10:  In the A.M. went up & got Otto & butchered the hogs. Ditching in the P.M.
  • Tue Jan 11:  Cut up the pork. Done the chores & went to LaConner. Staid all night at McGlinns. Stormy.
  • Wed Jan 12:  Came home from LaConner. Done the chores, Whitneys also, finished the ditch in the orchard.
  • Thu Jan 13:  Done chores then ent down to Klingenmaiers. Got some drags for wood. Made an extension to the wood shed in the P.M. Mr Gilkie here to dinner.
  • Fri Jan 14:  Doing chores & working on the wood shed. Went up to Geo. granary & got some lumber. Traded a hog to him for 400 sq ft lumber. Very pleasant day. Freezing a little at night.
  • Sat Jan 15:  Clean & cold this morning. Mercury at 24°. Worked on smoke house & done chores.
  • Sun Jan 16:  Pleasant day. Darcys folks & Al Leamer here.
  • Mon Jan 17:  Raining. Signed note to H B Stewart for $500. Wrote to Smith and sent the money to him. $597. Made settled bal.
  • Tue Jan 18:  Worked in the granary making bedstead, &c. Worked on smoke house in the P.M. Done chores &c. Rainy.
  • Wed Jan 19:  Very warm & pleasant. Whitney came home to day. Went up to the timber & sowed ditching timber ground. Very wet & full of water.
  • Thu Jan 20:  Worked on the bridge up beyond Callahans place. Road work. Very pleasant. Frosty weather.
  • Fri Jan 21:  Worked on the bridge part of the A.M. Finished putting on the stringers. Began filling ditches in the P.M.
  • Sat Jan 22:  Went to the timber & sowed ditching timber. Pleasant.
  • Sun Jan 23:  Very pleasant day. Was down at Whitneys. Spent the day reading & writing to George.
  • Mon Jan 24:  Went to the timber & made posts & shake timber.
  • Tue Jan 25:  Making posts. George Brown had my team to ball out his ditching stuff. Hauled a load for me.
  • Wed Jan 26:  Finished making posts & hauled out the balance of my cedar. Also some wood for smoking meat.

1881 Jan 27-Feb 18

Image Id: 002-0440
Transcription
  • Thu Jan 27:  Clean frosty weather. Made a gate and hung it out by the road. Began making fence on the south line. "West side of Allen 80". Rec'd the mortgage from Smith.
  • Fri Jan 28:  Very hard snow storm all day. Cut wood some and fix my wagon. Helped Whitney scow his horses across the slough.
  • Sat Jan 29:  Pleasant day. Snow about 8 inches deep. Got Whitney to help me and scowed my timber home from up the slough.
  • Sun Jan 30:  Staid at home all day. Clear & cold. John Ball was here a little while. Went up & put the scow in the slough by the dyke.
  • Mon Jan 31:  Sharpened posts & unloaded the scow. Weather getting warmer & snow going off fast.
  • Tue Feb 01:  The highest tide of the season this morning. Finished sharpening posts & finished the smoke house in the P.M.
  • Wed Feb 02:  Went out & made fence a little while. Had to quit for rain. Cut wood outside of the dyke in the P.M.
  • Thu Feb 03:  Made fence most all day, but it is a very nasty day to work on account of rain.
  • Fri Feb 04:  hauled some posts & punchions in the morning. Rainy in the A.M.
  • Sat Feb 05:  Building fence on the line between the Allen 80 & lot 8. Warm nice weather for winter. Wilson came back to Whitneys. Got a litter from George.
  • Sun Feb 06:  Warm & rainy. Wrote a letter to Aunt Lou. Heard a frog.
  • Mon Feb 07:  Fixed the road out by the barn. Made a fence out by the 45 ft slough and hauled wood for smoke house and 200 fence posts &c.
  • Tue Feb 08:  Began the east & west ditch through the plot bed. Rec'd a letter from Father. Warm & pleasant.
  • Wed Feb 09:  Pleasant day. Mrs Allen here visiting. Ditching & driving posts. Took the meat our of the barrel & hung it up ready for smoking.
  • Thu Feb 10:  Ditching all day. Began smoking meat. Mrs Cornelius here this evening. Willie Stewart & Miss Upson here at Whitneys. Fine drying weather.
  • Fri Feb 11:  Pleasant. Willie Stewart & Lettie Upson here in the A.M. Did not sleep much last night - Pearl, cause. Ditching & cleaning out an old ditch. Gave Wilson a note & mortgage for $400. [margin: with interest from this date at 15% note payment of the taxes.]
  • Sat Feb 12:  Digging ditch & driving posts. Snowing in the P.M. & evening. Ordered my oats shipped by this mail.
  • Sun Feb 13:  Staid at home all day. Mrs Cornelius went to Darcy's. Clear & cold with some snow.
  • Mon Feb 14:  Cool this morning. Digging ditch all day.
  • Tue Feb 15:  Dito yesterday.
  • Wed Feb 16:  Dug ditch all day. Finished driving posts & the string of ditch with them.
  • Thu Feb 17:  Ed stacey & Lyle Wallace here. I went to the woods. Cut down a fir tree for wood & cut some maple, alder & birch.
  • Fri Feb 18:  Filed my saw & dug ditch in the P.M. Wallace & Ed went home to day. Storm last night.

1881 Feb 19-Mar 17

Image Id: 002-0441
Transcription
  • Sat Feb 19:  Warm with south wind. Fine weather for working. Spent the day hauling rails posts & ditch stuff.
  • Sun Feb 20:  Very quiet Sunday. Rainy & windy most of the day. Allen's folks returned & he brought the mail but no letters for us. The rain warm & spring-like. Reading papers most of the day.
  • Mon Feb 21:  Rainy day. Did not do much, but cut wood & do chores.
  • Tue Feb 22:  Staid in the house and mended socks most of the day. Rainy.
  • Wed Feb 23:  Rainy most all day. Did not do much. The old black sow had 10 pigs yesterday. 1 died.
  • Thu Feb 24:  Another pig died to day. Staid in the house most of the day. Windy & rainy. Whitney killed his hogs. Began reading Ivanhoe.
  • Fri Feb 25:  Rather pleasant day but very windy. Had rheumatism in my back & legs. Did not do much but chores.
  • Sat Feb 26:  Sowed the balance of my timothy seed. Still had a lame back & legs, also sore throat. Clowdy.
  • Sun Feb 27:  Nona calved today, killed the calf. Rainy all day. Still under the weather. Heard of Annie Armstrong death yesterday. Very warm and the grasses growing finely.
  • Mon Feb 28:  Not rainy to day. Allen down here to fix his sail. I helped him make it.
  • Tue Mar 01:  Ditching & filling ditch. Took down the bacon & packed it 240 lbs fell short 16 lbs in curing. [margin: Gave J.P. Stewart note & mortgage for $500 dated Jan 15th with interest at 12 1/2 percent.]
  • Wed Mar 02:  Went to LaConner. Took the meat and sold it to Andrews. Mrs Wallace & Alfred came home with me.
  • Thu Mar 03:  Went down to the beach & cut wood all day & brought a boat load home.
  • Fri Mar 04:  Worked in the garden, filling ditch, digging parsnips, carrots &c.
  • Sat Mar 05:  Went to town to take Mrs Wallace & Grandma. Did not get home till 9 P.M.
  • Sun Mar 06:  Clowdy & rainy. Emona(?) Stroble stopping here now.
  • Mon Mar 07:  Went down to the beach and got a load of wood with Whitney's boat. In the P.M. filled & covered ditch.
  • Tue Mar 08:  Helped Whitney clean grain to day. Heavy rain last night.
  • Wed Mar 09:  Helped Whitney in the A.M. drove posts in the P.M.
  • Thu Mar 10:  Filling ditch most all day. Rain again last night. Windy today.
  • Fri Mar 11:  Filling ditch in the A.M. & working for Whitney in the P.M. Cleaning grain. Rainy in the P.M. & last night.
  • Sat Mar 12:  No rain to day. Working for Whitney cleaning grain.
  • Sun Mar 13:  Very pleasant & drying day. With strong north wind. Went up to Jone's. He was not at home. Jenkins was here today.
  • Mon Mar 14:  Started for Utsalady with Whitney. Arrived at 3 P.M. Fine day.
  • Tue Mar 15:  Staid in Utsalady all day looking over grain for Whitney. Rainy. Found some grain spoiled.
  • Wed Mar 16:  In Utsalady getting out reffuse lumber all day. Idaho in town this morning.
  • Thu Mar 17:  Started or home at 2 P.M. got home at 6. Sailed all the way.

1881 Mar 18-Apr 12

Image Id: 002-0442
Transcription
  • Fri Mar 18:  Did not do much in the A.M. but fill ditch out in the meadow. Helped Whitney clean grain in the P.M. Warm & spring-like. [margin: The little white sow had 6 pigs.]
  • Sat Mar 19:  Making fence all day out by the meadow. Fine weather no rain.
  • Sun Mar 20:  Staid at home as usual. Pleasant day.
  • Mon Mar 21:  Helped Whitney clean grain in the A.M. & cleaned my seed grain after dinner.
  • Tue Mar 22:  Made fence & drove posts in the A.M. Worked in the garden in the P.M.
  • Wed Mar 23:  Went up to the timber & cut stove wood. Rainy some. Came home & filed the saw before night.
  • Thu Mar 24:  Went to the timber & cut wood. Ground drying off some.
  • Fri Mar 25:  Not doing much but chores. Rainy day. Light rains.
  • Sat Mar 26:  Showery & windy. Done the chores & made a crib for baby. Not much prospect of plowing in April.
  • Sun Mar 27:  Clowdy with south wind. Staid at home all day.
  • Mon Mar 28:  Rain, a considerable last night. Helped Whitney kill his cow and made garden in the P.M. Set out strawberries, planted potatos, peas, onions, radishes, lettuce, &c.
  • Tue Mar 29:  Pleasant day. Began plowing for the first time this spring. Harry came home to work for Whitney.
  • Wed Mar 30:  Warm & pleasant. Good growing weather. Plowing.
  • Thu Mar 31:  Plowing. Ground drying fast.
  • Fri Apr 01:  Pleasant. Plowing. Finished the wehat lot & began on the 16 acre piece.
  • Sat Apr 02:  Pleasant. North wind. Plowing. Pears in blossom.
  • Sun Apr 03:  Staid at home in the A.M. was down at Whitneys in the P.M. Mrs. Jones was here to spend the day. Pleasant.
  • Mon Apr 04:  Plowing. The steamer Susie in with our lumber. Went and helped to load her with grain in the evening.
  • Tue Apr 05:  Helped to finish load grain in the morning. Plowed until 4 P.M. & had to quit for rain.
  • Wed Apr 06:  Too wet to plow to day. Showery. Divide the lumber and hauled up two loads. Fixed fence & finished covering ditch in the garden.
  • Thu Apr 07:  Showery in the A.M. Made an addition to the hen house and plowed a little in the garden. Pretty wet to plow. Whitneys teams running this P.M.
  • Fri Apr 08:  Plowing all day in the 16 acre piece. Drying day.
  • Sat Apr 09:  Plowing finished the 16 acre lot & began on the S.E. part of the Allen 80. Have 16 acres plowed. Had the first pie plant to day. Peas, radishes, &c. up. Heard from my oats. They sold for 142 1/2.
  • Sun Apr 10:  Very drying day but cold. Staid at home in the A.M. & went up to the Ridge in the P.M. to take Mrs. Crumrine home.
  • Mon Apr 11:  Plowing on the Allen 80. Fine weather.
  • Tue Apr 12:  In the A.M. went to the burial of M.D. Smith and in the P.M. plowing. Darcy took his pig. Done 20 acres plowed now.

  • Fri Apr 22:  Clowdy but no rain. Rough harrowing on the Allen 80 south side.

  • Sun Apr 24:  Clowdy & blowing strong from the S.E.
  • Mon Apr 25:  Heavy rain last night. Did not do much to day but make a doubletree for the seeder.
  • Tue Apr 26:  Heavy shower last evening. Ground very wet. Drying up some to day. Cutting wood up by Smith's.
  • Wed Apr 27:  Plowing in the A.M. & seeding some in the P.M. Have 11 1/2 acres sowed now. Drying day.
  • Thu Apr 28:  Plowing in the A.M. and harrowing in the P.M. Fine day. Ground drying very fast. Planted some mangolds.
  • Fri Apr 29:  Harrowing after the seeder in the A.M. & plowing in the P.M.
  • Sat Apr 30:  Sowing grain most all day. An acre of barley, sack of wheat & some oats. 6 1/2 - 18 acres to date.
  • Sun May 01:  Pleasant drying day. Staid at home most of the day. Callahan using the seeder. Dolly quite lame.
  • Mon May 02:  Harrowing this piece by the barn in the A.M. Plowing in the P.M. 3 hours.
  • Tue May 03:  Rough harrowing on the east end of the Allen 80.
  • Wed May 04:  Finished harrowing & plowed some in the A.M. Rain in the P.M. plowed till 3 P.M. Whitney sick. Rain last night.
  • Thu May 05:  Plowing most of the day. The Stacey Heifer calved today. Brought up up & milked her. Rain last night.
  • Fri May 06:  Sowing oats all day on the Allen 80. E field. 8 acres.
  • Sat May 07:  Sowed 3 acres. 29 acres to date. 40 sacks oats. Harrowing in the P.M. Fine weather north wind.
  • Sun May 08:  Pleasant drying day. Jones & his wife here to supper.
  • Mon May 09:  Kalsow plowing 1/2 day. Harrowing in the A.M. went to P. Ridge & hired Al Leamer's team in the P.M. Got home at 8 P.M.

1881 May 10-Jun 2

Image Id: 002-0445
Transcription
  • Tue May 10:  Harrowing & plowing. Kalsow plowing for me. Callahan plowing on the E. end of Allen 80. Ed Stacey came with the team this evening.
  • Wed May 11:  Kalsow plowing 1/2 day. Edi plowing with Sulkey plow. I with my team on 3 acre lot by timothy patch.
  • Thu May 12:  Plowing with 3 teams. Clowdy but no rain. Kalsow plowing. 1 day.
  • Fri May 13:  Plowing with 2 teams on north side of the "80". Clowdy but no rain.
  • Sat May 14:  Cold & clowdy. Rain some in the P.M. did not stop work. Ed harrowing with 3 horses. Sowed 6 1/4 acres. 9 1/2 sacks. Plowed 1/4 day with 2 horses.
  • Sun May 15:  Cold with north wind. Drying wind. Whitneys folks here calling.
  • Mon May 16:  Plowing, finished on the north side of the "80".
  • Tue May 17:  Ed plowing all day with 2 horses. I plowed 1/2 day & harrowed 1/2 day with 3.
  • Wed May 18:  Ed harrowing with 3 horses. Seeding with my team. 8 1/2 acres. 44 acres to date. Whitney killed my black cow.
  • Thu May 19:  Whitney killed the black cow yesterday. Went to town with beef to day. Edi seeding in the A.M. 5 acres. Harrowing in P.M. with 3 horses.
  • Fri May 20:  Ed harrowing with 3 horses. Sowed 4 acres by hand. Plowed the balance of the day 3/4.
  • Sat May 21:  Seeding 5 acres. Write out rough harrowing. Plowed 1/2 day. Harrowed 1 day with 2 horses. Clear & pleasant. Ground very dry in the some places. 60 acres sowed.
  • Sun May 22:  Staid at home all day. Mrs. Stroble & Mrs Crumrine were here to dinner. Clear weather & drying wind.
  • Mon May 23:  Plowing with one team and harrowing with one team. Finished harrowing 4 acre piece by gate.
  • Tue May 24:  Trimble plowing for oats & sowing also. 64 acres oats sowed. Clowdy to night looks some like rain. Got a letter from G. Brown Esq.
  • Wed May 25:  Kalsow harrowing for me to day. Oats. Began plowing for barley with my team. Ed went home with Al's team to day.
  • Thu May 26:  Kalsow working 1/2 day. Plowing all day.
  • Fri May 27:  Finished plowing about 10 oclock and rough harrowed the balance of the day. Grandma went to P. Ridge.
  • Sat May 28:  Rain some last night about 1/4 inche and some today. Sowed 5 1/4 acres barley to day.
  • Sun May 29:  Clowdy day. Went up to Trimbles in the P.M. and got my hair cut.
  • Mon May 30:  Began harrowing. Then went to Allen's with the seeder & sowed for him 3/4 day. Willie harrowing for me in the P.M.
  • Tue May 31:  Shut up my pigs & went after a load of wood. Took the black sow down to Whitneys. Rain again last night. Sowed 5 acres for Allen in the P.M. Willie harrowing for me.
  • Wed Jun 01:  Capt Bowden staid here last night. Plowed on the E end of the Allen 80. Willie harrowing. Finished the barley field.
  • Thu Jun 02:  Plowing in the A.M. In the P.M. rough harrowed & began sowing. Whitney got home from Utsalady.

1881 Jun 3-Jul 1

Image Id: 002-0446
Transcription
  • Fri Jun 03:  Finished sowing my late oats. 2 3/4 acres & began harrowing. Rain in the P.M. So I did not go out. Sowed the clover seed & hoed the garden.
  • Sat Jun 04:  Some more rain to day. Finished harrowing in the A.M. & worked around the house in the P.M. P up up some grain to take to LaConner. Have 17 sacks left for feed.
  • Sun Jun 05:  Staid at home all day. Except being down at Whitneys in the P.M. Whitney still laid up with sore legs.
  • Mon Jun 06:  Worked in the garden in the A.M. hoeing &c. Harrowed in the clover seed and hauled a load of wood in the P.M.
  • Tue Jun 07:  Went to Allen's in the morning. Filed a saw for him. In the P.M. plowing in the orchard. Quit for rain about 4 P.M.
  • Wed Jun 08:  Working in the orchard all day & plowing & hoeing. Went to help Harry load the engine on the scow.
  • Thu Jun 09:  Harrowed the orchard and took Whitney out to the grain on the sled. Hauled out manure on the orchard in the P.M.
  • Fri Jun 10:  Rainy in the A.M. Working in the granary in the A.M. Fixing grindstone filing saws, &c. Hauling manure in the P.M.
  • Sat Jun 11:  Went to the timber & cut threshing wood, Allen grain all looking well except where the wheat was. It is yellow & looks bad.
  • Sun Jun 12:  Staid at home all day reading & writing letter to George. Darcy's folks here in the evening. Jenny & Otto also. Rainy in the P.M.
  • Mon Jun 13:  Went to LaConner with a load of oats. 24 sacks. Rain some this morning.
  • Tue Jun 14:  Worked in the garden in the A.M. Fine growing weather. Went to the timber & cut engine wood in the P.M. 35 acres of grain looks splendid. Late grain nothing extra. Cows got in the gard and eat off my oats.
  • Wed Jun 15:  Working in the timber all day. Fine growing weather.
  • Thu Jun 16:  Cutting fir wood on the Smith Ranch. Warm & dry.
  • Fri Jun 17:  Made cant hooks for Whitney and files his saws. M???? ??? & worked in the garden for myself. [margin: Bridge 1/2 day]
  • Sat Jun 18:  Went to LaConner to get pictures taken. Did not get home until after 9 P.M.
  • Sun Jun 19:  Staid at home. Mrs Siegfred staid all night here last night. Began raining at 5 P.M.
  • Mon Jun 20:  Nice rain last night & this A.M. Working around the house in the A.M. & working on the Skagit road in the P.M.
  • Tue Jun 21:  Working on the Skagit road all day.
  • Wed Jun 22:  Working Skagit road 1/2 day. Chopped a tree for rails in the P.M. The heifer went visiting today.
  • Thu Jun 23:  Splitting rail up on John Balls ranch. Fine weather.
  • Fri Jun 24:  Hoeing in the garden in the A.M. Worked for Whitney 1/4 day taking timber out of the slough. Not feeling well.
  • Sat Jun 25:  Went down to Whitneys to cut brush but it rained. Took the scow home. !/2 for bridge. Rainy day.
  • Sun Jun 26:  Heavy rain last night. Clowdy & windy to day.
  • Mon Jun 27:  Worked on the bridge cutting brush.
  • Tue Jun 28:  Working for Whitney to apply on bridges.
  • Wed Jun 29:  Working on bridge. Started a crib but it came up again. Fine growing weather.
  • Thu Jun 30:  Working for myself making rails. Warm.
  • Fri Jul 01:  Making rails. Mosquitos very thick. Susie in to day with the bridge lumber. Barley heading out. Grain looking well.

1881 Jul 2-Aug 8

Image Id: 002-0447
Transcription
  • Sat Jul 02:  did not do much of anything. Hauled a load of wood. It looks aas though I would have to buy another horse. Dolly very lame.
  • Sun Jul 03:  Ida & the children started for LaConner to celebrate the 4th. Eat dinner down to Whitneys. Frank Darcy here in the P.M.
  • Mon Jul 04:  Went to LaConner to spend the fourth. Came back in the eve at 10 P.M.
  • Tue Jul 05:  Went to the timber & cut wood. Came home at 3 P.M. to see the Buckeye Man.
  • Wed Jul 06:  Worked on the bridge to day. Mosquitos bad.
  • Thu Jul 07:  Hewing stringers for the bridge.
  • Fri Jul 08:  Working on Bridge. Warm & dry, grain doing well.
  • Sat Jul 09:  Working on bridge. Put in the first bent.
  • Sun Jul 10:  Had the first green peas. Staid at home all day.
  • Mon Jul 11:  Worked on the bridge 1/2 day. Fixed up the water scow and started with it for water.
  • Tue Jul 12:  Went after water and to LaConner, also bought a horse of Engle for $120. - Borrowed $30- of Tom Barnet(?) for which I gave note & mortgage for the amt. due in 60 mos with interest of 1 1/2 per ct per month.
  • Wed Jul 13:  Went to LaConner and brought the horse home.
  • Thu Jul 14:  working on the bridge 1 day. Put in bent and put on stringers. Poor hay weather.
  • Fri Jul 15:  Working on bridge.
  • Sat Jul 16:  Working on the bridge hewing stringers &c.
  • Sun Jul 17:  Went up to Allen's in the P.M. horseback.
  • Mon Jul 18:  Hauled a load of wood & worked on the bridge in the P.M. 1/2 day.
  • Tue Jul 19:  Mowing some, broke the mower & did not do very much.
  • Wed Jul 20:  Mowing some in the A.M. & went to Darcy's after my tank lumber in the P.M.
  • Thu Jul 21:  Raked & put up hay.
  • Fri Jul 22:  Putting up hay in the A.M. went to LaConner in the P.M. Paid for my lumber & bought some harness. Came home in the eve.
  • Sat Jul 23:  Finished putting up hay & chored around some.
  • Sun Jul 24:  Carrie & Frank White here. Went up to see Jones in the eve. Nelson Kelly here for currants.
  • Mon Jul 25:  Mowing.
  • Tue Jul 26:  Mowing until 11 A.M. Raking & putting up hay in the P.M. Kalso helped me 1/2 day to apply against Jones road work.
  • Wed Jul 27:  Worked on bridge 1/2 day. Raking & cocking hay in the P.M.
  • Thu Jul 28:  Rain a little last night. Finished cocking hay and mowed 3 acres in the P.M.
  • Fri Jul 29:  Rain again last night. Did nothing with the hay today. Heavy shower in the evening. Did not do much.
  • Sat Jul 30:  Rainy most of the day. Worked on the scow, plowed in the A.M. Chored around in the P.M.
  • Sun Jul 31:  Pleasant day. Al Leamer here to dinner.
  • Mon Aug 01:  Mowing in the A.M. & raking in the P.M. with John Balls rake about 2 hours.
  • Tue Aug 02:  Putting up hay all day. Kalso helped my about 4 hours.
  • Wed Aug 03:  Started for LaConner to tend court as Grand Jury man. Put up at the Maryland Hotel.
  • Thu Aug 04:  Working for Uncle Sam for 3.00 a day & doing nothing and boarding myself.
  • Fri Aug 05:  Found a true bill in the case of U.S. vs Wm Goolson for selling whiskey to Indians. Came home at 12 P.M.
  • Sat Aug 06:  Moved the hay press. Hot. Otto & team to help.
  • Sun Aug 07:  Staid at home most of the day. Was at Whitneys a little while in the P.M.
  • Mon Aug 08:  Went after water at 3 A.M. & down to Hansons. Got home at 5 P.M. on the scow 1/2 full of water.

1881 Aug 9-Sep 9

Image Id: 002-0448
Transcription
