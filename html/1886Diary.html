1885 Dec 12-Jan 1, 1886

Image Id: 002-0513
Transcription
  • Sat Dec 12:  Ditching in the orchard. Sterns plowing.
  • Sun Dec 13:  Home all day. Wrote to Geo.
  • Mon Dec 14:  Ditching in the A.M. went up to John Balls in the P.M. & graded sloughs some.
  • Tue Dec 15:  Grading sloughs out in the Allen 80. Sterns plowing. Bargained for Crumrine's team & wagon, colt, plow, harrow &c at $100.
  • Wed Dec 16:  Filling ditches and setting out trees. Sterns plowing.
  • Thu Dec 17:  Fine day. Sterns plowing. Setting out trees in the A.M. & P.M. also.
  • Fri Dec 18:  Sterns finished plowing the 11 acre piece. My self building fence. Fine weather to work.
  • Sat Dec 19:  WEnt down to Whitneys & then down to the mill to see McKown. Fixed a saw in the P.M. and a water bb. Fine weather to work.
  • Sun Dec 20:  Went to LaConner & staid all night. Went to Church and heard Banks preach. Staid at McGlinn's.
  • Mon Dec 21:  Came home from LaConner at 4 P.M.
  • Tue Dec 22:  Went up to the Smith ranch with Sterns to make rails for line fence.
  • Wed Dec 23:  Went up to the Smith ranch with Sterns to make rails for line fence. Windy & cold, rainy some.
  • Thu Dec 24:  Higher tide this A.M. but not so high as the Nov tide. Not working today. Laying off for Christmas tree this A.M.
  • Fri Dec 25:  Christmas. Had a fine time last night at the tree, but the weather was bad & rainy. Had the highest tide this A.M. we have had for 10 or more years. John Ball flooded again. Our dyke all right. Except what ran over and leaked through. Very pleasant A.M. but clowdy towards noon. Got a chicken of Aunt Lou for Christmas.
  • Sat Dec 26:  Went up to the Smith Ranch and made rails and at 10 P.M. went up and helped John Ball put it a dam. Sterns helping make rails.
  • Sun Dec 27:  Helping Ball until 7 A.M. Was pretty sleepy. Rainy day.
  • Mon Dec 28:  Helped Sterns fix the shed for his colts. Bought some oats of Crumrine. 90 dollars worth at 18 per ton. Fixed dyke some in the P.M.
  • Tue Dec 29:  Worked for Whitney making a sluice box.
  • Wed Dec 30:  Worked on the house ceiling, the large bedroom & done the chores.
  • Thu Dec 31:  Went to Crumrines to get the team, but he was not at home. Help Sterns haul straw & hay. Got 910 lbs baled hay of Whitney. Crumrine brought the team down in the P.M.
  • Fri Jan 01:  Spent the day in the house visiting W.J.W., Mr & Mrs Richards, Aunt Lou, A.G.T. & wife were here to dinner. Not a very pleasant day out of doors, but passable in the house by the stove. about 1/2 inch of snow.

1886 Jan 2-23

Image Id: 002-0514
Transcription
  • Sat Jan 02:  Working on the house 3/4 day. Putting up clothes rooms partitions. Sterns fixing fence in the P.M. out side of the pasture.
  • Sun Jan 03:  Went to church at the Hall. Rev Brooks preached. Wrote a letter to Geo.
  • Mon Jan 04:  Worked on the house 3/4 day. Sterns hauling rails for me up on the Smith ranch. Ida sick. Aunt Lou & Will up here in the eve.
  • Tue Jan 05:  Rainy more or less. Sterns finished hauling rails. I went up to the timber and made some to finish out.
  • Wed Jan 06:  Working on the house 3/4 day. Sterns dyking.
  • Thu Jan 07:  Working on house 1/2 day and putting down carpet in the P.M.
  • Fri Jan 08:  Working on the house 3/4 of a day. Done the chores &c.
  • Sat Jan 09:  Rainy day. Working on the house 3/4 day. Finishing up stairs & casing tower windows.
  • Sun Jan 10:  Staid at home all day. Reading most of the time in Adam Bede. Quite pleasant and warm for winter.
  • Mon Jan 11:  Went up to Crumrines in the A.M. then came back and worked on the house casing tower windows.
  • Tue Jan 12:  Working on house finished tower windows. Rainy good part of the day.
  • Wed Jan 13:  Working on the house, made a trap door for tower and put up moulding &c. Heavy rain all last night and a good part of the day.
  • Thu Jan 14:  Snow this A.M. about 4 inches. Worked on the house. Finished moulding, the ceiling & began putting in pantry shelves.
  • Fri Jan 15:  This is the coldest A.M. of the winter so far. Done the chores, harnessed the team, hauled a load of hay & a load of wood in the woodhouse. Worked on pantry shelves 1/2 days. [margin: Uncle Will here in the eve.]
  • Sat Jan 16:  Clear & cold. Done the chores & some odd jobs in the house. Put up some more shelves in the pantry. Made a picture frame & some knife boxes. [margin: Mercury at 19° at 9 P.M.]
  • Sun Jan 17:  Reading most of the day. Cold but pleasant. Mrs A. & May B came up in the P.M.
  • Mon Jan 18:  Went up to the Ridge in the A.M. & settled with Crumrine and got the harrow teeth. did not do much the balance of the day but chores. Aunt Lou, Will & Sterns folks here in the evening.
  • Tue Jan 19:  Went to the timber with Will and cut down a cedar tree for ditch timber. This has been the coldest day of the season. Average 18°
  • Wed Jan 20:  Went to LaConner with Sterns & Tillinghast. Had a very hard trip. Very heavy north wind. All day & night.
  • Thu Jan 21:  Working on the trap door to tower and choring around. Filed the big saw. Went down to Whitney's in the P.M. a while. W.J. here in the eve. Reading Fool's Errand.
  • Fri Jan 22:  Went to the woods to saw shake & ditch timber. Sterns also. Snowing some all day but not enough to prevent work.
  • Sat Jan 23:  3120 lbs of hay of Whitney. Hauled a load of hay & a load of wood & split wood some. Snow going off fast with chinook wind.

1886 Jan 24-Feb 7Duplicate of next

Image Id: 002-0515
Transcription
  • Sun Jan 24:  Had the highest tide this A.M. we ever know of since living here. Nearly every body flooded but our dyke has no breaks, except down at the Sigfried place, but it ran over nearly all around. Water 3 or 4 inches deep about the house. Whitney's ranch badly flooded across the slough. He has a break and lots of hay & grain wt. Had a heavy S. Easter last night and a Norwester to day. Went down and helped Whitney fix his rink dyke in the P.M.
  • Mon Jan 25:  Tide 10 inches less than yesterday A.M. Helped Whitney this morning on dyke & sluice box since 10 A.M. till 7.30 Tues morn A.M. Went to Jones to get saws out of the woods. Went over to help put in Whitneys box but could not on acct of water inside of dyke. [margin: Mrs. Whitney had a boy baby (Ilo) today.]
  • Tue Jan 26:  Done the chores & went to bed in the A.M. In the P.M. began fixing dyke down by the point. Tillinghast, Otto & VanValkenburg helping.
  • Wed Jan 27:  Not doing much in the A.M. Working on dyke in the P.M. Co. dyke.
  • Thu Jan 28:  Done the chores & made a pair of single trees. Bad day to work out of doors. Wanted to go to town but did not for wind & rain was too bad. [margin: W.J. here to ???d black lice(?)]
  • Fri Jan 29:  Went to LaConner. Blanche started for Fidalgo. Had a good trip and tide both ways. The Schooner Perry here for 1000 sacks of oats of Otto's. price per ton $20.
  • Sat Jan 30:  Helped Otto & Sumes(?) load oats and done the chores.
  • Sun Jan 31:  The schooner Perry went out of the slough this A.M. Went calling on Jones & Callahan's folks. Clear with north wind. Got a 9 pound butter crock of Whitney to day.
  • Mon Feb 01:  Helped Sterns weight out oats in the A.M. 24716 lbs in the P.M. Sawed up fence post and made some double trees. Done the chores, &c. Cold rain in the eve.
  • Tue Feb 02:  Rainy in the A.M. & misty in P.M. In the P.M. went down to Whitneys hay barn & got 16 bales of hay on scow. Mrs W. not well.
  • Wed Feb 03:  Went to town with Whitney in a boat, got some pipes for kitchen chimney. Had an easy trip.
  • Thu Feb 04:  Unloaded the hay. Mr Sterns helped. Rained some. In the P.M. fixed chimney for the kitchen. Cemented it some. [margin: 1/2 on house.]
  • Fri Feb 05:  Went down to the sawmill in the A.M. and pinned truss in the P.M. Sterns went to the Skagit. The grass is starting nicely and it look very much like spring.
  • Sat Feb 06:  Done the chores & began on the door yard fence, painted the posts & dug the holes and set five of the posts. Very windy day. [margin: On fence 3/4 day.]
  • Sun Feb 07:  Went to church in the Hall. Rev Brooks preached. Sterns folks here to dinner to day.

1886 Feb 8-23

Image Id: 002-0517
Transcription
  • Mon Feb 08:  Heaviest west wind last night of the winter and it blew all the A.M. Some rain in the night. Worked on door, yard fence. Set the posts. Grandma went to town to hear Miss Varissa White(?). Sent for circulars to Cole & Flemming & J. Means. [margin: On fence 1.50]
  • Tue Feb 09:  Working on the fence most of the day. Windy & some rain. A surprise party over to Sterns. We went & staid till midnight. [margin: On fence 1.50]
  • Wed Feb 10:  Weighed grain for Otto. Rainy day & lots of wind. The Schooner is to take another load to Victoria at $20/ton.
  • Thu Feb 11:  Helped Sterns load up his traps(?) & worked on the fence. [margin: F 3/4]
  • Fri Feb 12:  In the A.M. went down to the saw mill & got some lumber to make a hanow off(?). Settled up with Sterns and took old Kate as bal due me. Worked on fence some in the P.M. Got a letter from George.
  • Sat Feb 13:  Stormy day. Worked up stairs most of the day making a hanner(?). Aunt Lou & Uncle Will up in the eve.
  • Sun Feb 14:  Home all day and down to Whitneys in the eve. wrote to Geo.
  • Mon Feb 15:  Fixed up the chicken yard and house. Mary & Alf down to night.
  • Tue Feb 16:  Put a fence along the dyke from the granary to Whitneys Corner.
  • Wed Feb 17:  Rainy last night, but good day to work. Made some flower beds. Cleaned out the chicken house, mended fence & gate. and cleaned up around the yard. &c, &c, &c
  • Thu Feb 18:  Went down to Whitneys & got a pair of pigs, pruned raspberries some and some other chores. Mrs Darcy here this P.M. and Sterns up after hay but did not get any.
  • Fri Feb 19:  Spent the day grading the yard. Mrs Darcy went down to Aunt Lous. North wind. Good working weather.
  • Sat Feb 20:  Hauled out some lumber for gates and 2 load of wood into the wood house. Worked the bal of the day in the garden pruning trees & currant bush. Set out a spruce & a juniper in the front yard.
  • Sun Feb 21:  Sterns here after hay, etc. Pleasant weather. Staid about home all day. The Schooner in again from Victoria. Grant is pretty well weaned now, he has had no titty since Wednesday.
  • Mon Feb 22:  Transplanted some evergreens in the yard. Took away the fence in front of the house and hung a gate to the pasture north of the house. Otto here and paid me on his note 396.60. Went to Whitneys and paid him the amt. due him, $25. & $30 towards the seeder am to pay bal. when I sell the grain. Fine drying weather and drying up fast.
  • Tue Feb 23:  Went to LaConner & paid bills to J. Gaches, Poole & Saloon, Davis, Graham & Maher &c. Had a windy day. Whitney went also. Whitney had to mow lawn yesterday.

1886 Feb 24-Mar 10

Image Id: 002-0518
Transcription
  • Wed Feb 24:  Sterns here this A.M. for his chickens. Bowden left this A.M. for Victoria. White working trees. Went down to Aunt Lous and got some chickens in the eve.
  • Thu Feb 25:  Did not do much in the A.M. except make a round chair for Grant. In P.M. went down and put dirt in T's hotbed and worked on my harrow till night. Rain a little in P.M. Went down to Tillinghasts in the eve.
  • Fri Feb 26:  Worked on the harrow some and in the garden some. Went dow to Ws & got some gooseberry cuttings.
  • Sat Feb 27:  Finished painting the harrow and commenced cleaning out the chamber. Grafted some trees & transplanted some. This is the coldest night of the month except on. Bill Woodburn to be here next week to work.
  • Sun Feb 28:  Fine day. At home. Will Woodburn came to work tomorrow.
  • Mon Mar 01:  Hauling in dirt in the yard. Woodburn helping. Went and got Peter's stove and put in the old house. Hauled a load of straw for bedding. Sharp frost this A.M.
  • Tue Mar 02:  Hauling dirt all day. 2 men & team. Sterns brought up the bal. of the seed oats and the scow.
  • Wed Mar 03:  Hauling dirt & working on the yard leveling &c. The Dr. down to see Whitney last night. W began plowing.
  • Thu Mar 04:  Woodburn working on the road with Otto. I working in the front yard. Made a hand roller and rolled it.
  • Fri Mar 05:  W & self clearing the old fence away and getting ready to build new one on the east end of the ranch. Most everyone plowing in the neighborhood except myself. Sent to James Means for shoes to day. Looks like rain to night. Very fine weather for the last week.
  • Sat Mar 06:  Working on the Smith line fence. Setting posts. Got them all set. Fine weather but looks like rain to night.
  • Sun Mar 07:  Went to church and heard Mr Brooks preach. Was in to see Whitney for a while. Peter was here to dinner & staid all night. Frank Darcy & Mr Jorgenson here also. Beautiful day but rained a little in the A.M.
  • Mon Mar 08:  Began plowing with 3 horses. Got old Prince home again, seeded the lawn & set a gate, post and helped Otto 2 hours load grain. Very windy & a little rain.
  • Tue Mar 09:  Windy but a drying day. Will plowing. Helped Bowden load 308 sks oats in the A.M. and he started for Victoria. Made a gate & fixed fence some. Worked on the Smith line fence in the P.M. Eva had her boy today.
  • Wed Mar 10:  Will plowing. I working on the Smith line fence & gate. Got it all up. Fine plowing weather. Ground in fine order. Bowden started out of the bay for Victoria this A.M.

1886 Mar 11-27

Image Id: 002-0519
Transcription
  • Thu Mar 11:  Rainy A.M. Went to town with Mr. Armstrong. Woodburn plowing in the P.M. not working in the A.M.
  • Fri Mar 12:  Rained hard all day. Will went out to plow but had to quit. I fixed the harrow teeth & mended sacks. Will washing the harness in the P.M. He here 1st 3/4 of all day, up to date.
  • Sat Mar 13:  Good working day. We cleaned out the ditch along the Smith line fence.
  • Sun Mar 14:  Rainy, unpleasant day. Staid at home & read Two Milestones in Cat W.(?).
  • Mon Mar 15:  Ditching on east end of ranch. Sill. Went to town and bought me a harness.
  • Tue Mar 16:  Ditching, grading sloughs, fixing a gate, &c. Snow storm this A.M.
  • Wed Mar 17:  Went to LaConner with Ida. Gertie Wallace came home with us. Strong north wind this P.M. Sent for Will a knife. Got some garden seeds of Till & 2 grape vines & some raspberries.
  • Thu Mar 18:  Oiled harness in the A.M. & went to Darcys for grain & fanning mill in P.M. Will also. Drying day. Brought some wheat & oats.
  • Fri Mar 19:  Spent the day cleaning grain for seed. 133 sacks all told. 2 wheat & 5 of barley.
  • Sat Mar 20:  Finished cleaning grain and Will dyked the balance of the day. I cleaned out bedroom up over the dining room & cased a window. Ditch the barnyards & dyked some in the P.M.
  • Sun Mar 21:  Went down to see Eva's baby and called on Whitney. Got a ???? weight - 20 lbs. Strong wind all day. Ground dry enough to plow again now but it looks like rain.
  • Mon Mar 22:  Plwoing in a slough & ditches hauling old fence posts. Went to Darcy's to take the mill home in P.M. Windy drying day.
  • Tue Mar 23:  Went to town to take Gertrude & got money for oats. Paid Gaches not of 207 dollars. Strong west wind all day. Got home at 8 P.M. Will plowing.
  • Wed Mar 24:  Raining in the A.M. Not doing much. Went out to plow but quit. In the P.M. Will & self out by the SW corner of the "Allen 80" grading a slough.
  • Thu Mar 25:  Finished the harrow. Fixed up the carpenter shop some. Set out a grape vine. Put up the kitchen chimney. Made a railing around the porch upstairs, &c.
  • Fri Mar 26:  Plowing. Will out in the field & I in the orchard. Fine drying day. My shoes came from Barton.
  • Sat Mar 27:  Will breaking sod on the Allen 80. I not doing much but sticking out lumber & choring about some. Hoed strawberries some.

1886 Mar 28-Apr 15Duplicate of next

Image Id: 002-0520
Transcription
  • Sun Mar 28:  Pleasant but cold. Uncle Will here to dinner. Was down to see Whitney in the P.M.
  • Mon Mar 29:  Began plowing with 2 teams in the P.M. Will plowing sod along Ottos fence in the A.M.
  • Tue Mar 30:  Plowing with 2 teams. Fine day.
  • Wed Mar 31:  Warmest day of the season. Plowing with 2 teams.
  • Thu Apr 01:  A.M. rainy, but warm. Not doing much. Filed a saw & split wood.
  • Fri Apr 02:  W. & self hauling out manure in the orchard. Ground quite wet.
  • Sat Apr 03:  Drying day. Plowing with 2 teams. Ground in fairly good order. Rev. Brooks here stay all night.
  • Sun Apr 04:  Pleasant morning. This will be a drying day. Went to church and down to see Whitney in the P.M. Heard Joe Goodwins service.
  • Mon Apr 05:  Plowing all day. Fine weather & drying day.
  • Tue Apr 06:  Plowing. Strong north all day & last night. Went up to Kalso's & got a cow this A.M. for which I am to pay 45 dollars, as soon as I get the oat money.
  • Wed Apr 07:  Plowing with 2 teams as usual. Finished all but one land(?). Clowdy day but no rain.
  • Thu Apr 08:  Will plowing with 3 horses. I working in the garden. Setting out strawberries, raspberries, gooseberries, currants, &c.
  • Fri Apr 09:  Planting potatos & peas. Windy & blowing like rain. The schooner Perry in at Whitneys with lumber. Sent some peas to Geo. Made a flower bed and worked on the harrow some. Will finished plowing & began harrowing sod. I was over to Jenne's in the P.M. Rain some just night. The schooner is laying(?) at the granary. [margin: Finished plowing.]
  • Sat Apr 10:  Will harrowing some but had to quit for rain both in the A.M. & P.M. Myself helping Bowden load the oats. Put on 520 sacks. Finished & painted the other harrow. Strong N. wind part of the day.
  • Sun Apr 11:  Bowden sailed for Victoria this A.M. Whitney & Kate came up this A.M. & staid till 4 P.M. Pleasant day.
  • Mon Apr 12:  Harrowing with 3 horses. Planted some onions & potatos. Went down to McKenna'a to get a horse. Whitney began seeding to day.
  • Tue Apr 13:  Misty in the A.M. and quite pleasant in the P.M. Went down to fix up the seeder in the A.M. & plowed for Tillinghast in the P.M. Will harrowing all day.
  • Wed Apr 14:  Will harrowing all day. Finished the sod and went out in the "80". I went to Jenne's & bought a colt for 200. dollars. Went down & got the seeder & seeded 3 acres of the Darcy oats in the P.M. [margin: Will rough harrowing.]
  • Thu Apr 15:  Seeded the bal of the Darcy oats & the barley. Took the McKenna horse to the pasture. Hauled a load of wind and hay.

1886 Apr 16-May 4

Image Id: 002-0522
Transcription
  • Fri Apr 16:  Will harrowing. I plowing for Tillinghast. Very windy day. Rain some just night.
  • Sat Apr 17:  Plowing for Till. Finished his ranch. Will rough harrowing. This has been a cold clowdy week but the land is in fair order. Most every one seeding. Borrowed $100 of Tillinghast and paid it to Jenne on colt.
  • Sun Apr 18:  Fine day but cold. Drying weather. Vinz & Emma up to call on us & Aunt Lou & Uncle Will.
  • Mon Apr 19:  Will harrowing. Went to town with Whitney & Tillinghast. Got oat money & paid up all my bills in town. Send for Binding Time and wrote about wagons. Paid note of insurance.
  • Tue Apr 20:  Heard that Armstrongs oats over up looking well. Seeding, sowed the wheat and began in oats. Will finished rough harrowing. Nearly.
  • Wed Apr 21:  Seeding finished this side of the road. Will finished rough harrowing and harrowed in the field in the P.M.
  • Thu Apr 22:  Will began seeding with 3 horses in "the 80". I rolling the sod & harrowing in P.M.
  • Fri Apr 23:  Will seeding 16 acres. I harrowing & sowing timothy seed.
  • Sat Apr 24:  Finished sowing seed & rolled it. Harrowed some. Will seeding 17 acres. Frost this A.M. Very drying weather but cold.
  • Sun Apr 25:  Very quiet Sunday. Wrote to George & read some. Good growing weather.
  • Mon Apr 26:  Will seeding & I grading a slough out in "the 80" in the A.M. & harrowing sod in the P.M. Pleasant day.
  • Tue Apr 27:  Finished seeding and finished rolling this side of the road. The barley & one piece of oats are up. I roll in the A.M. & hauled out a load of ditch timber and a load of wood in the P.M. Will harrowing in the P.M. The land is getting pretty dry.
  • Wed Apr 28:  Harrowing with 2 teams. Clowdy & threatening rain.
  • Thu Apr 29:  Harrowing 6 hours with 2 teams. Had to quit in the A.M. for sleet & hail. Cold disagreeable weather.
  • Fri Apr 30:  Rainy all the A.M. did not harrow any to day. Mending sacks and hauled up 2 bales of hay from Whitney.
  • Sat May 01:  Clowdy & poor weather for harrowing but we are at it - with 2 teams. Have been all over once and it will have to do if it keeps wet.
  • Sun May 02:  Rainy A.M. but pleasant P.M. Went down to the school house to preaching by the Rev. Brooks.
  • Mon May 03:  Harrowing with 2 teams finished. I harrowed the orchard in the P.M. with an as me harrowing. Very drying and pleasant.
  • Tue May 04:  Ida went to town with Whitney. Will plowing for John Harrold. I planting carrots, beets, peas, wheat and other garden truck.

1886 May 5-24

Image Id: 002-0523
Transcription
  • Wed May 05:  Will working for John Harrold plowing & seeding. I set out some asparagus, planted cord & cur weeds out of the grain field. Went up to the woods in the P.M. Fine growing weather.
  • Thu May 06:  In the A.M. went to the timber & hauled out 2 loads of ditch timber to the east end of ranch. In the P.M. began splitting & getting ready to put it in. 2 hands.
  • Fri May 07:  Will harrowing & harrowing in seed(?) for Tillinghast 3/4 day. I filling ditches.
  • Sat May 08:  Fine gorwing weather. The last sowed oats are beginning to come up. Finished filling and covering the ditches in east end of the 80 in the A.M. In the P.M. moved the privy &c. Tillinghast had a horse today. Had a little thunder but no rain.
  • Sun May 09:  Hitched up the team & went to Richards ranch, Whitneys & Tillinghasts folks went along. I got home at 6 P.M. Had a rough trip.
  • Mon May 10:  Fine growing weather. Will & myself began dyking in the pasture lot. [margin: 2 hrs]
  • Tue May 11:  Worked in the garden some & made a couple of sluice boxes. Had a fine rain this eve. Will seeding for John Harrold.
  • Wed May 12:  Will dyking all day. I 1/2 & went to town in the P.M. Fine growing weather.
  • Thu May 13:  Dyking on the pasture dyke. Moved the cook stove into the kitchen to day. [margin: 1 1/2]
  • Fri May 14:  Dyking part of the day. Had a fine rain for grain in the P.M. [margin: 1 day]
  • Sat May 15:  Dyking 1/2 day. hoeing in the garden 1/2 day. Planted some corn & beans & squashes. Fine growing weather. Sent an order to Geo. for a wagon yesterday. [margin: 1 day.]
  • Sun May 16:  Wrote to Father to day. The new cow had a calf. Mr Jone's folks & Uncle Will here to dinner. Pleasant day.
  • Mon May 17:  Fine growing weather. Dyking on pasture dyke. Clear & warm. Grain looking well. [margin: 2 days]
  • Tue May 18:  Planted some more corn & summer squashes. Ida went to Mr Darcys. Dyking 1 3/4.
  • Wed May 19:  Dyking 2 days. Corn coming up.
  • Thu May 20:  Dyking 2 days.
  • Fri May 21:  Ordered a wagon one week ago to day from York State. Will & self working on the road.
  • Sat May 22:  Will & self 1/2 day on the road. In P.M. W. & team working for Till. Self fixing milk safe & working in the garden.
  • Sun May 23:  All went to Ed's and I went to church. Fine growing weather. Roads getting quite dusty.
  • Mon May 24:  Working on the road. Finished my tax. Will also working. Finished. Whitney lost another cow today. The warmest day of the season.

1886 May 25-Jun 13

Image Id: 002-0524
Transcription
  • Tue May 25:  Kate had a calf this A.M. Will working on the road all day. & I 1/2 for himself. Hoeing in the garden in the P.M.
  • Wed May 26:  Dyking again out by the stable. Warm & dry. Had a slight shower in the eve. Set out 24 tomato & some cauliflower plants.
  • Thu May 27:  Dyking all day. Strong south wind. Frank Darcy here in the eve and said the Skagit is rising fast.
  • Fri May 28:  Dyking warm & pleasant.
  • Sat May 29:  Hoeing carrots in the A.M. & went to town in the P.M. Will working in the orchard in the P.M. digging grass away from the trees. The Skagit River is high this week. Bought a bb of flour.
  • Sun May 30:  Home in the A.M. & down to Whitneys in the P.M. Fine warm weather.
  • Mon May 31:  Will dyking. I dyking 1/2 & worked for W. 1/2 on milk house. Have dyked 22 days so far 1 man to date. [margin: 1 1/2]
  • Tue Jun 01:  Will dyking. Self working for Whitney on milk house. Grain very weedy and needs rain. Was out looking at it this eve.
  • Wed Jun 02:  Working at Whitneys. Will dyking. Warm & dry. Grain not doing well.
  • Thu Jun 03:  Working on milk house. Will dyking as usual. Whitney started for Seattle last night.
  • Fri Jun 04:  Will dyking. I working for Whitney on milk house. Al Leamer here to nite.
