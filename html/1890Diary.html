1889 Dec 19-Jan 1, 1890

Image Id: 002-0632
Transcription
  • Thu Dec 19:  Rainy part of the day. Making pickets.
  • Fri Dec 20:  Grinding feed in the A.M. and making & nailing on pickets in the P.M. Showery. Capt Bowden here this eve.
  • Sat Dec 21:  Pleasant day. Went to town, saw lots plowing. Got some juniper trees up in Sullivans grove.
  • Sun Dec 22:  New moon to day. Went to B.V. to church. Mrs Crumrine was baptized to day. Pleasant.
  • Mon Dec 23:  Went to post notices of R. Meeting. Pleasant. People still plowing.
  • Tue Dec 24:  Cold again. Mercury at 25° Set out some cedars in the yard and in the P.M. & eve went to B.V. to the C. tree. Cold & frosty. Got home at 10 P.M.
  • Wed Dec 25:  Mercury at 22° Clear & pleasant. Mrs & Mr Randall here to dinner also Arthur & Bebber.
  • Thu Dec 26:  Cutting wood, doing chores & working on the fence. Cool. Mercury at 22°.
  • Fri Dec 27:  Warmer & raining. Cleaned out the house tank and in the afternoon help Whitney grind feed.
  • Sat Dec 28:  Pleasant day. Tem 26°. Cutting wood and working at the fence. The prospect is poor for the timothy.
  • Sun Dec 29:  Temp. 26°. Clowdy looks like snow. Did not go B.V. to day. Nettie & Mama have bad colds.
  • Mon Dec 30:  Hauling hay in the A.M. Al Risbal helping. Making pickets in the P.M. Snowing some.
  • Tue Dec 31:  Making pickets & a picket gate. Alf Stacey here. Cold 29.
  • Wed Jan 01:  Cutting wood some. Was down to Whitney's in the P.M. & eve. Eating warm sugar.
    [from next image]Alf. Stacey here last night went to LaConner today. Coldest day of the winter so far. Temp 15° this A.M. and at 18 all day.

1890 Jan 2-13

Image Id: 002-0633
Transcription
  • Thu Jan 02:  Temp 18°. Snowing some. Shod a bob sled & done the chores. Helped Whitney grind this P.M.
  • Fri Jan 03:  Colder. Temp 12° this A.M. Clear. Reading the Royal Gentlemen. Working on sled. Went to town with Whitney. Pretty cold traveling.
  • Sat Jan 04:  Working on sled & cutting wood. Clear & pleasant.
  • Sun Jan 05:  Temp 13°. Went to S.S. on church. Pearl & I. the bal. staid at home. Signs of a breakup at night.
  • Mon Jan 06:  Temp 25°. Fixed the box on the sled in the A.M. & went to the road meeting in the P.M. Snow again last night. It is about 4 inches & up now.
  • Tue Jan 07:  Temp 22°. Pleasant day. Mended stove grate & a gum boot. Then went sled riding up to Callahan's & Ball's.
  • Wed Jan 08:  Temp 32. Cutting wood some & working at pickets. Warmer. Clowdy & looks like rain.
  • Thu Jan 09:  Temp 34. Showery day. Rain very cold and frost still in the ground. Cut wood some & worked in the shop some.
  • Fri Jan 10:  Temp 32. Some showers of snow this A.M. Cutting wood some & putting pickets on the fence.
  • Sat Jan 11:  Cool windy disagreeaable day. East wind. Cutting & splitting wood. Snow some in the evening.
  • Sun Jan 12:  Warmer. Went to church in the sled but it was pretty rough. Gray preached.
  • Mon Jan 13:  Rain again last night. Warmer some to day. Making fence and tearing away fence in the front of the old house.

1890 Jan 14-26

Image Id: 002-0634
Transcription
  • Tue Jan 14:  Temp 32. Working on fence most of the day. Snowing most of the day but it melted as it came. Very disagreeable weather.
  • Wed Jan 15:  Still a disagreeable east wind, not doing much but chores & cutting wood. !/2 inch snow last night.
  • Thu Jan 16:  Hung the big gate and done chores and made a little gate by the root house. Still cool & frosty.
  • Fri Jan 17:  Frost again this A.M. Whitney went to Anacortes. I cleaning up out front of the yard, burning brush, &c. Cut wood in the P.M.
  • Sat Jan 18:  Temp 30°. Fine weather, but not wet enough to suit us. Frost again. Set & made two hitching posts by the front gate.
  • Sun Jan 19:  Temp 25°. Snow 1/2 in. last night. Went to S.S. Pretty cold day.
  • Mon Jan 20:  Temp 20°. Cutting wood & reading "Hairworth's".
  • Tue Jan 21:  Temp 19. Fine winter weather. Put 13 sks oats in feed bin. Went to Bay View in the P.M.
  • Wed Jan 22:  Not doing much but chores & cutting wood. Still quite cold.
  • Thu Jan 23:  Choring. Put a fence around the juniper trees.
  • Fri Jan 24:  Whitney home from Anacortes. I went to town with him today. Anacortes booming in great shape.
  • Sat Jan 25:  Temp 38° Strong S. wind but cold. Tide hight for the season. Rain & snow a good part of the day. Frost about out of plowed ground. Soderburg's dyke broke.
  • Sun Jan 26:  Clowdy dismal day with strong S. wind. Did not go to B.V. to day. Grant quite sick last night with grip, but better today.

1890 Jan 27-Feb 6

Image Id: 002-0635
Transcription
  • Mon Jan 27:  Still blowing strong from the south but not getting much rain. Alf & his mother came down to day with a load of goods. The Mary Pearly here after Callahans grain.
  • Tue Jan 28:  Still blowing. Alf brought another load. Mary here yet.
  • Wed Jan 29:  Still blowing. Alf brought another load. Helped weigh grain some the A.M. for Gaches. Mary & Alf left this P.M. No rain so speak of. Art White here to night on his way from Mount Vernon. Hauled a small load of hay this eve.
  • Thu Jan 30:  Working in the yard some and set out some currant bushes. Still blowing. South wind, but no rain.
  • Fri Jan 31:  Not doing much but chores. Alf came down again. Still blowing some.
  • Sat Feb 01:  had the heaviest rain of the season last night. Helped Alf set up stoves this A.M. Hauled 2 load of hay this P.M.
  • Sun Feb 02:  Went to SS & church. Mr Baldwin preached. Roads very bad.
  • Mon Feb 03:  Went to Kalso's to make out road report. Considerable more rain today. Went around to look at the dyke this eve. Had the highest tide of the season to day.
  • Tue Feb 04:  Done the chores & finished sawing wood. Clowdy & a little rain.
  • Wed Feb 05:  Went to McKowns and got some pie plant roots, also to Geff's & got some strawberries. Worked in the yard in the P.M. Set out trees, &c.
  • Thu Feb 06:  Rain again this A.M. Washing and oiling harness to day. C. Wilson here to pay his threshing bill.

1890 Feb 7-16

Image Id: 002-0636
Transcription
  • Fri Feb 07:  Went to the Ridge(Kalso's) & got a lot of raspberries plants. Set them out & worked about the yard some. Strong S. wind but little rain. Anacortes still booming. O. Kalso over to day.
  • Sat Feb 08:  Working in the front yard making a flower bed & grading some in the P.M. Went to the Ridge and out to see the timothy patch. It is generally alive but very small. Got some shrubs at the Ridge. Whitney home from Anacortes wild.
  • Sun Feb 09:  Clowdy & showery. did not go to SS. Reading and writing.
  • Mon Feb 10:  Went to Anacortes with Whitney and saw the city. It is immense. Staid all night with White's folks. Fine weather.
  • Tue Feb 11:  Came home from the city at noon. Another fine day. Alf was here last night. did the chores and talked Anacortes the balance of the day.
  • Wed Feb 12:  The last two days. Pleasant days. Whitney men plowing. Heavy rain last night. Charley W upset this A.M. going to Bay View with cabbage. Self went to town with boat and staid all night.
  • Thu Feb 13:  Came from LaC. this A.M. stormy S.E. wind. Sowed some grass seed in the P.M. and done the chores.
  • Fri Feb 14:  Not doing much but chores. Sowed some grass seed this A.M. Clover & timothy. Reading and &c. in the P.M.
  • Sat Feb 15:  Grinding feed or running engine for Whitney. He is not home yet. Snowing some and colder.
  • Sun Feb 16:  At home all day. Snowing. Snow 3 or 4 inches deep. Mercury at 23° this A.M. Bro Paulson & Dave Fulk here calling. This is more winter than we care for at this season.

1890 Feb 17-Mar 1

Image Id: 002-0637
Transcription
  • Mon Feb 17:  Mercury at 20 this A.M. and the snow still with us. Not doing much but chores.
  • Tue Feb 18:  Mercury at 20 again clear. Had a fracas with the horses. Hauling hay and straw. Randall helping 3 hours or so. Whitney came home this eve. Was down there 3 hours talking real estate & partnership business.
  • Wed Feb 19:  Colder. temp 14. Clear and cold all day. Decided to go into business with W.
  • Thu Feb 20:  18 Tem. E.A. started for Anacortes this morning. Alf Stacey and I running the ranch. Frosty.
  • Fri Feb 21:  Temp 18°. Spent the day in city & in coming home. Still clear & cold. Temp 20 Bot some lots yesterday.
  • Sat Feb 22:  Temp 20. Still cold. Sent a letter to Geo this A.M. about moving. Went to LaC. Walked. Roads very bad.
  • Sun Feb 23:  Clowdy and some warmer. At home all day. Had cold & sore throat.
  • Mon Feb 24:  Strong north wind this A.M. and freezing again. Whitney started for Seattle.
  • Tue Feb 25:  E.A. started again for Anacortes. Self went along part way. Got stuck on mud had hard time. Went for boat last night but did not get it. Mrs Price of Bay View died last night.
  • Wed Feb 26:  Mercury at 10°, coldest of season. Went to Bay view to Mrs Price's funeral.
  • Thu Feb 27:  Alf went to town. Got his trunks and grandma. Temp 28.
  • Fri Feb 28:  Alf and Mr Randall hauling hay in P.M. Warmer cloudy. Looks like rain.
  • Sat Mar 01:  Warmer. Rain some. Walked from Anacortes. Got home at 4 P.M. about sick.

Transcription
  • Sun Mar 02:  At home all day. Alf & the girls went to S.S. at Bay View.
  • Mon Mar 03:  At home sick with cold. Munroe went to Anacortes to work to day.
  • Tue Mar 04:  At home all day grunting(?) with grippe. Misty in the A.M. Alf started a plow team in the P.M. Land works nice.
  • Wed Mar 05:  Had a fearful headache all last night. Alf plowing. No steamer yet for Whitney's folks.
  • Thu Mar 06:  Pleasant weather and looks like spring. Alf plowing. C. Bowden here again this eve.
  • Fri Mar 07:  Very windy day with prospect of rain. Alf plowing. Rain some in the night.
  • Sat Mar 08:  Alf began plowing on the Miller patch. Began raining in the P.M. and rained hard in the evening.
  • Sun Mar 09:  Clowdy cool day. Alf went to S.S. The rest staid at home.
  • Mon Mar 10:  Cooler with nearly an inch of snow. Very disagreeable March weather and no sign of grass growing. No frogs yet.
  • Tue Mar 11:  Still cool and wet. Poor weather to do anything. Will Woodburn here yesterday visiting. Alf went to town yesterday horse back. Lee Bradley home for a visit. Alf went for Miss Esary to day to do dress making.
  • Wed Mar 12:  Pleasant again to day. Alf & self went up to the woods & got some spruce trees. Set them out in the P.M. Heard first frogs tonight. Miss Esary went home this eve. C. Bowden here (last) this eve.
  • Thu Mar 13:  Alf plowing again to day. Land some wet on top, but not bad. Water in the ditches still brackish and it look as though the chances for an oat crop was very slim. However we will hope for lots of rain yet.

1890 Mar 14-23

Image Id: 002-0640
Transcription
  • Fri Mar 14:  Alf plowing. Clowdy & ????? rain. Sowed some more grass seed out in the field by the hay barn. Grass has a very small start for the time of year.
  • Sat Mar 15:  WEnt to town Alf. also. Mist most of the day and the roads were horrible. Got home at 5 P.M. Warm. Grass growing.
  • Sun Mar 16:  George's boy born this A.M. Staid at home all day. Alf came home at 5 P.M. C. Bowden here at supper time.
  • Mon Mar 17:  Set out some rhubarb plants in the A.M. Alf went to town in the P.M. Hauled 3 loads of hay from the hay barn. Alf went for the M.D. for Mrs Burdette at 4.30 P.M. Pleasant drying day.
  • Tue Mar 18:  Alf plowing in the A.M. Rainy P.M. Mending sacks in the P.M. Set out some more pie plant & horse radish.
  • Wed Mar 19:  Alf plowing in the meadow. Self working in the orchard in the A.M. cutting down trees. Planted some cabbage seed. Was out looking at the timothy. Some of it needs reseeding.
  • Thu Mar 20:  Alf plowing 1/2 day. Rain this A.M. & P.M. also land pretty wet now. Went to town in boat. Got some clover seed, garden seeds and new harness. Lee Bradley came home with me.
  • Fri Mar 21:  Sowing grass seed in the A.M. Alf went to Seamans for Will & in the P.M. cleaned seed grain.
  • Sat Mar 22:  Cleaning grain in the A.M. heavy rain all day. Earl Whitney here from Cal.
  • Sun Mar 23:  Visiting with Earl. Windy. Very hard wind last night. Tom Hurds store blew down. Fulk here this P.M. Alf went to LaConner this P.M.

1890 Mar 24-Apr 2

Image Id: 002-0641
Transcription
  • Mon Mar 24:  Alf not working to day. Self helping Fulk clean grain. Drying day. Northwind. Heard of the arrival of a young Sisson in Pa.
  • Tue Mar 25:  Alf plowing all day. Ground in pretty fair order. Self set out some onion sets and strawberries that came from Grahams. Made a kettle cupboard for Ida in the A.M.
  • Wed Mar 26:  Hauling hay & straw in the A.M. Rain some more last night, but quite pleasant to day. Went down to fix a sluice box with Randal in the P.M. Split wood & set out the strawberries. Plowing in the P.M.
  • Thu Mar 27:  Rain a little again last night. Went & took the boat around to the canal. Alf plowing. Finished the 8 acre piece.
  • Fri Mar 28:  Went to LaConner. Alf not working. The plowing all done, but headlands. Stayed all night.
  • Sat Mar 29:  Came home. Alf to LaConner again. Rain some last night. Clowdy to day & cold.
  • Sun Mar 30:  They have been working on the Steam Boat slough bridge this past week. Quite a pleasant day, but cool. All staid at home. Mrs. Burdette very low. Will & his wife here calling. VanValkenburg here this P.M. also.
  • Mon Mar 31:  Another pleasant day. Fulk came after Daisy's calf. Alf came home and got the team to plow for Sam Gray. Self not doing much.
  • Tue Apr 01:  Mrs. Burdette died last night at 8 P.M. Went to the Ridge to get a grave dug. Pleasant drying day. Found my raspberries were all dead. It looks as if I might as well jump this Ranch and go where salt water won't come for a while.
  • Wed Apr 02:  Rain again last night but pleasant to day. Went to the funeral in the P.M. Got a few more raspberries up at Kalsos. Found my trees dying from salt in the soil.

1890 Apr 3-14

Transcription
  • Thu Apr 03:  Hitched up the colt and drove them to the clod masher. They behaved well.
  • Fri Apr 04:  Harrowed some with the colts to break them. In the P.M. went over to the R.R. bridge.
  • Sat Apr 05:  Fixed some ground with the colts & set out about 500 strawberries. Art White here. Cold & clowdy. Alf still in LaConner. Rain in the P.M.
  • Sun Apr 06:  Stormy day. At home all day.
  • Mon Apr 07:  Make hen fence by the old house.
  • Tue Apr 08:  Bred Beasly. Helping Randall grind feed. the girls went and got some duck eggs. John Ball sowing oats.
  • Wed Apr 09:  Alf came home from LaConner. Working with gang plow in the P.M. Self harrowing with colts in P.M. Strawberries came from Childs.
  • Thu Apr 10:  Alf & self hauling hay in the A.M. Got a ton of Whitney's done. Rain last night. Alf plowing with gang in the P.M. Self harrowing with the colts. Strong N.W. wind all day.
  • Fri Apr 11:  Still cold and 1/2 in snow this A.M. Started the seeder cultivating. Self spading around trees in the orchard and harrowing in the P.M.
  • Sat Apr 12:  Frost. Pleasant but cold. Cultivating & harrowing. Ground getting quite dry. Rec'd a letter from Tillinghast. Planted some peas & lettuce.
  • Sun Apr 13:  Went to Bay View to SS & church. Rev. Grey preached. Cool & clowdy. Nels. Ovenell here in the eve or P.M. Wrote to A.G.T. in the P.M.
  • Mon Apr 14:  Pleasant & warmer. Piled up the cabbage fence. Plowed some patches. Cultivating & harrowing in the P.M.

1890 Apr 15-24

Image Id: 002-0643
Transcription
  • Tue Apr 15:  Harrrowing and cultivating. Whitney came over today. I bargained for 30 acres of the ranch for $3000.
  • Wed Apr 16:  Was down to Whitneys a while. Wrote to Geo again for money. Took "Maud" down to Fulks. Rain some at noon. Alf cultivating in the A.M. Working around the house in the P.M. Making flower beds &c. Too rainy to cultivate.
  • Thu Apr 17:  Alf finished cultivating & plowing and harrowed in the P.M. Self choring about and setting strawberry plants. Warm & misty. Quite a rain in the night last night.
  • Fri Apr 18:  Fine day but cool with strong north wind. Alf harrowing with Charley & Frank. Self & Ida went to town. Got a lawn mower, tea, coffee, brown flour, lines hat, soap, Grants clothes &c, clover seed, &c.
  • Sat Apr 19:  Began seeding, sowed 5 acres oats and about 3 of wheat. Fine day. Harrowed them in & Alf went to LaC with Beauty. I mowed the law with the new mower.
  • Sun Apr 20:  Went to SS. Read & wrote letters in the P.M.
  • Mon Apr 21:  Sowing oats. Self sowing grass seed. Seeded the 8 acre piece & some on the Whitney land. Skips.
  • Tue Apr 22:  Harrowing with 2 teams. Alf sowing until noon. Finished seeding this side of the road.
  • Wed Apr 23:  Harrowing & seeding. Began across the road. Lovely weather. Mrs Carter here visiting.
  • Thu Apr 24:  Seeding across the road. Finished harrowing on this side and began rolling.

1890 Apr 25-May 6

Image Id: 002-0644
Transcription
  • Fri Apr 25:  Same as yesterday. Seeding & rolling. Very drying weather.
  • Sat Apr 26:  Seeding & rolling. Ground drying out very fast. Poor prospect for grass unless it rains soon.
  • Sun Apr 27:  Went to Bay View to S.S. & church. Warm & dry. Rev. Grey, Sam Grey & Mr & Mrs Carter here to dinner. R.E.W. here in the P.M.
  • Mon Apr 28:  Harrowing in oats & finished seeding. Al Risbell came & got the seeder at noon. Still dry and a stormy north wind. Got a long letter from G.S.
  • Tue Apr 29:  Harrowing with 2 teams 1/2 day and one all day. Alf sick in the P.M. Ground getting very dry and no sign of rain. Very warm day.
  • Wed Apr 30:  Finished harrowing & began rolling the big field. Still dry.
  • Thu May 01:  Alf went to the island to help W. with Star & Beauty & old "Laci"?. Self rolling with the 3 colts.
  • Fri May 02:  Alf at the island. Self rolling. Still dry grain coming up well on this side of the road. The other field & the grass need rain. Mailed a letter to Geo this A.M. about money. Cabbage seed came.
  • Sat May 03:  Rolling in the A.M. Had to help Art White survey land in the P.M. that I bought of Whitney 30 acres as would have finished. Alf at Whitneys. Dry & warm. Skagit River up but not do damage yet.
  • Sun May 04:  Went to church & S.S.
  • Mon May 05:  Finished rolling and took Charley over to the Island for Alf to work. Saw the bridge. Still dry.
  • Tue May 06:  Rec'd dfts on N.Y. for Geo for 1900. Paid in on note at bank. No sign of rain.

1890 May 7-15

Image Id: 002-0645
Transcription
  • Wed May 07:  Cut the seed potatos in the A.M. and in the P.M. plowed the ground to plant them. Very warm and seems like rain.
  • Thu May 08:  Planted potatos & cut tar weed in the A.M. It began to rain slowly in the A.M. but did not last long. Not doing much in the P.M. Rain about 1/2 inch.
  • Fri May 09:  Finihed planting potatos and planted some corn, beets, peas, &c in same patch. Cut tar weeds until noon. Plowed the carrot ground with cultivator and harrowed it.
  • Sat May 10:  Planted carrots and a general assortment of garden stuff. Clowdy but no rain all signs fail. Went over to Telegraph slough to see them drive piles. John Balls meadow looks lovely. It doesn't need rain.
  • Sun May 11:  Did not got to S.S. or church. Had a nice rain all this A.M. It quit at noon but looks like more.
  • Mon May 12:  Was down to the school in the A.M. and pulling weeds. Set a gate post in the P.M. Fine growing weather.
  • Tue May 13:  Hung a gate to cabbage patch in the A.M. & went over to the Island to see the track laying machine in the P.M. Planted ground cherries. Was out looking at the grain and made up my mind that it would not amt to much.
  • Wed May 14:  Harrowed the cabbage patches. Warm. Good growing weather.
  • Thu May 15:  Cut tar weeds some. Went to Balls & got seed drill. Planted 160 Fottler cabbage and some mangolds and parsnips. It look like rain to night. Clowdy & south wind.

1890 May 16-24

Image Id: 002-0646
Transcription
  • Fri May 16:  Planted some mangolds & parsnips & cut out tar weeds. In the P.M. helped Lee haul fence.
  • Sat May 17:  Went to Telegraph slough in the A.M. & hoed strawberries & mowed the lawn. In the P.M. went to see the track laying machine. Saw Whitney also. Grain looking some better tonight.
  • Sun May 18:  Went to Sunday school. Pleasant. Signs of a storm.
  • Mon May 19:  Went to town, bought a cart & single harness and 100 lbs sugar, &c. Looked like rain all day, but it did not come.
  • Tue May 20:  Pleasant weather but too dry for salt land. Alf & self making fence out by the slough on the new ranch to make more pasture.
  • Wed May 21:  Alf went to town & got the cart. Lola Wallace came with him. Self fixing fence, pulling tar weed and taking up carpet, &c.
  • Thu May 22:  Rainy some but very light. Washed the wagon & light harness. Oiled the harness and put down the carpet. Alf began to work on the road & went up to Callahan's for lunches for school house.
  • Fri May 23:  Rec'd money on dft of Geo. Sisson for $1000 and gave my note for $3000. at 8% & a mortgage for security note to be paid 5 years from May 1st, 1890 or May 1st, 1895. Borrowed twenty five from Peter Miller. Clear & pleasant, with a dry prospect. Ida to B.V. with me. Gaches, Mrs. Carter came down. Alf not working to day.
  • Sat May 24:  Went down & took a lunch for the school house and up to Kalso's. Alf went to town as usual. Miss Turner buried to day. Pulled tar weeds some and drove Jean to the cart. She went very well.

1890 May 25-Jun 5

Image Id: 002-0647
Transcription
  • Sun May 25:  Went to S.S. & church. Grey preached. Warm & dry. Ed's folks here in the P.M.
  • Mon May 26:  Worked on the road 1 1/2. In the P.M. went up to tht sawmill & to Balls.
  • Tue May 27:  Clowdy & windy but no rain. Working the road 1 1/2 days. Self pulled tar weeds in the P.M.
  • Wed May 28:  Clowdy and trying to rain but don't make out much. Went & got a load of wood for S. house and sent alf to Waikles with Beauty.
  • Thu May 29:  Began grading the slough yesterday P.M. in the Whitneys field and worked at it all day to day with team & Alf. Warm & dry.
  • Fri May 30:  Working on slough & bridge all day. Pulled weeds some. Had a little thunder but no rain.
  • Sat May 31:  Alf harrowing the slough bank and the cabbage patches in the A.M. Not working in the P.M. Self pulling weeds and hoeing garden. Had a little sprinkle of rain in the P.M. Alf to town with cart & Star.
  • Sun Jun 01:  Went to Sunday school. Rain some in the P.M. Mr & Mrs Randall and Fred White here.
  • Mon Jun 02:  Rain again but it was fine, probably a little over 1/2 inche all together. Not doing much bu sharpening tools. Alf & Grandma started for Stanwood this eve. Got a letter from Geo.
  • Tue Jun 03:  Pleasant. Grain looking fine on fresh land. Self working at Youngs. Went in the cart.
  • Wed Jun 04:  Clear & pleasant working at Young's.
  • Thu Jun 05:  Alf came home. Brought Maud & Chet Leamer. Working at Young's.

1890 Jun 6-16

Image Id: 002-0648
Transcription
  • Fri Jun 06:  Working at Young's. Alf working on the road. Warm and dry again.
  • Sat Jun 07:  Dito yesterday. Alf working 1/2 day. Very warm. Had a little shower in the eve.
  • Sun Jun 08:  Went to church and Sunday school. Had a house full to dinner. South wind all day.
  • Mon Jun 09:  Working for Young on school house. Alf working on road. Took Jean over to Jenne's. Had a light shower in the P.M. Took dinner at Abbotts. Pearls birthday.
  • Tue Jun 10:  Working on schoolhouse again. Same as yesterday. Clowdy but no rain. Alf on the road. Have worked on road 4 days to date.
  • Wed Jun 11:  Working on S. house. Had a good shower. Alf & Ida went to Ed's and the children staid at home.
  • Thu Jun 12:  Working on S. house. Alf on the road.
  • Fri Jun 13:  Ditto yesterday. Planted some turnips. Pleasant. The Whites & May & E. Howard here in the eve.
  • Sat Jun 14:  Ida 33 to day. Pleasant, fine growing weather. Was out looking at the crop. It is coming up wonderfully. I estimate the field across the road at 50 bushels per acre or more. Working on the S. house. 3/4 day or 10 1/2 (ten one half) days for Young to date. Alf went to LaConner again as normal.
  • Sun Jun 15:  Went to S.S. and in the A.M. went over to see Whitneys grain. It looks well and I guess it at 75 or more per acre. Wrote to Geo.
  • Mon Jun 16:  Went to work on Hawkins house. Had some light showers this P.M. The cenus taker here to night. Rec'd a letter from Geo this eve.

  • Sat Dec 13:  Alf plowing up the cabbage and in the P.M. hauling wood for him self. Went to B.V. got a crout barrell and some can goods of Detweiler. Made crout in the P.M.
  • Sun Dec 14:  Alf went to LaConner. Highest tide of the season so far, but about ten inches short of the "big tide". Windy & showery. Was down to Walters in the eve to sing.
  • Mon Dec 15:  Windy all day. Worked on wagon shed some and made cars for Grant. Alf away canvasing. Another train set on to day from Seattle to Anacortes. Tide falling off and wind increasing.
  • Tue Dec 16:  Owe Kalso $1.58 on threshing account. Worked on shed some. Still very windy.
  • Wed Dec 17:  Mercury at 58. Finished up the shed and went up to the Ridge in the P.M. Got some wheat of McGregor. Still windy. Tide pretty high to day. Sent off some Christmas things to Pa. [margin: Very warm all this week. Grass and oats still green and fresh.]
  • Thu Dec 18:  Still blowing. Had the highest tide in four years. Ran over John Ball's dyke in several places and Lee's hog barn dyke. Done the chores and dug carrots.
  • Fri Dec 19:  Working on dyke in the A.M. Windy as ever. Took a pile down to the big dam for a break water. In the P.M. covered carrots and split some posts. Alf came home in the eve. Tide not quite so high.
  • Sat Dec 20:  Another gale all day. South & south east. Tide not very high. Alf working on dyke some and I also. In the P.M> he went after some wheat to McGregors and got a load of radish(?) from the lower field. We moved the smokehouse for Alf a wood house. Ida up Will Woodburns all day to tea party.
  • Sun Dec 21:  Staid at home. Rain some in the A.M. Walter's folks up here in the eve to sing.
  • Mon Dec 22:  Blossom took a walk. Alf went to LaConner to get Christmas. Pearl also. Self covered cabbage some. Pleasant.
