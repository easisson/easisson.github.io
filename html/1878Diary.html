1877 Dec 30-Jan 13, 1878

Image Id: 002-0388
Transcription
  • Sun Dec 30:  All stayed at home. Al and the folks went home in the A.M. Eva and Ida Conner called in the eve. [margin: Cold.]
  • Mon Dec 31:  Papa chopping wood down at the camp. I went down to Padilla in the P.M. [margin: Thermometer 22° last night.]
  • Tue Jan 01:  Papa, Aunt Lou and myself went down to the camp this morning and got some wood. Took our dinner in the woods by a fire. Came home about 1/2 past 2 o'clock had a very pleasant time. [margin: Very pleasant.]
  • Wed Jan 02:  Papa still cutting wood at the camp. Whitney's men left today. [margin: Pleasant.]
  • Thu Jan 03:  Papa cutting down trees as usual. [margin: The hen has laid 21 eggs.]
  • Fri Jan 04:  Edgar filed saws in the A.M. made windowframes in the P.M. Whitney and Mother went to LaConner in the P.M. Aunt Lou and Eva were here today.
  • Sat Jan 05:  Edgar working on the kitchen. Aunt Lou came up again this morn to help with the work while mother was away. W came home at noon. Dr. Maggs with him.
  • Sun Jan 06:  All stayed at home until late in the P.M. then went down to Whitney's and spent part of the eve. [margin: Very pretty day.]
  • Mon Jan 07:  Edgar went to the woods today back of the White house. [margin: Andis(??) also rain.]
  • Tue Jan 08:  Trying to rain. Edgar and I in pursuit of water. Fearing the rain might blow over. Wet up the slough towards Balls but the tide being too high did not get any. He and Alfie then went down to the siwash camp and got back wood and water. [margin: Blowing quite hard and rain's a very little in showers E made a pen for the pig.]
  • Wed Jan 09:  Edgar finished the pen. In the A.M. cut wood and tinkered around in the P.M. Baby Pearl is 7 months old today. [margin: Raining in showers.]
  • Thu Jan 10:  Edgar worked in the woods until noon, came home in account of the rain. Hanson brought the new boat today. [margin: Rains very hard. Blow last night.]
  • Fri Jan 11:  Edgar worked in the wharf making steps and &c. Also made me a pair of oars. Went down to the beach for wood. [margin: Pleasant.]
  • Sat Jan 12:  Edgar working in the wharf in the A.M. making doors for the safe in the P.M. [margin: Pleasant.]
  • Sun Jan 13:  All stayed at home. Eva, Julia & Harry called in the A.M. Mother went up to Millers. [margin: Very pleasant.]

1878 Jan 14-Feb 1

Image Id: 002-0389
Transcription
  • Mon Jan 14:  We went up to LaConner today to see the Dr. Baby has her first tooth. [margin: Pleasant.]
  • Tue Jan 15:  Came home from La Conner. Mother came with us. Ida not feeling very well.(written by Ida) [margin: Pleasant]
  • Wed Jan 16:  Papa working down at Whitney's working on Jone's house. I quite sick.
  • Thu Jan 17:  Papa still at work on Jones house. Ida no better.
  • Fri Jan 18:  We start for Victoria to day to consult a Dr. The wind blowing very hard. The dyke broke and flooded part of Harry's ranch this morning.[margin: Very high tide.]
  • Sat Jan 19:  We were at Utsalady today. Will take the steamer Dacota for Victoria this evening.
  • Sun Jan 20:  Spent the day aboard the Dacota. Reached Esquimalt at 5 o'clock P.M.[margin: Went to the M.E. chruch in the eve.]
  • Mon Jan 21:  At Victoria the Dr. speak very encouraging. I am daily improving.
  • Tue Jan 22:  On board the North Pacific reached Seattle at 5:30 o'clock PM
  • Wed Jan 23:  Spent the day in Seattle. Went to church in the eve. [margin: High tide. Balls dam went out.]
  • Thu Jan 24:  Spent the day in Seattle. I called on Mrs Chilberg and Rev Damon.
  • Fri Jan 25:  On out way home aboard the steamer Fanny lake arrived at LaConner about 4 o'clock P.M.
  • Sat Jan 26:  Got home about 12 o'clock found every body well and the ranch not flooded.
  • Sun Jan 27:  Mrs. Allen was here today. Edgar and I have sore throats, have caught cold. [margin: Raining a little in the P.M.]
  • Mon Jan 28:  Papa working on Jones house down at the shop. Pearl and (I) went visiting Aunt Lou today. [margin: Pleasant.]
  • Tue Jan 29:  Papa at work on Jones house up on his ranch. Baby was very sick last night. She is cutting her teeth. [margin: Ditto]
  • Wed Jan 30:  Papa still at work on J. house. Eva and Julia went visiting Mrs. D Ball today. Pearl better today. [margin: Threating rain.]
  • Thu Jan 31:  Papa at work on the house. Aunt Lou and Eva spent the P.M. here. [margin: Ditto]
  • Fri Feb 01:  Edgar made a bedstead in the A.M. and went after wood in the P.M. [margin: Rain. The hen had laid 50 eggs up to this date.]

1878 Feb 2-21

Image Id: 002-0390
Transcription
  • Sat Feb 02:  Edgar working on the house laying floor and putting on lanterns(?) & ???? [margin: Rained hard all last night.]
  • Sun Feb 03:  Stormy windy day. Aunt Lou, Alfie & Grandma went up to Rich Balls. Ida had bad sore throat in the eve.
  • Mon Feb 04:  Worked on Jones house. Finish for the Present. Shot two Ducks. [margin: Rainy day.]
  • Tue Feb 05:  Made a sluice box for Callahan. Whitney got home from up Sound. Alexanders child dead with croup.
  • Wed Feb 06:  Worked on window casings for Jones house in the A.M. in P.M. began a smokehouse for Whitney. [margin: Alf. sick with Asthma.]
  • Thu Feb 07:  Worked on smoke house all day.
  • Fri Feb 08:  Worked on Whitneys S. house. fine weather. Mrs Allen here visiting. Aunt Lou also making clothes for Whitney. [margin: Mr. Upson came.]
  • Sat Feb 09:  Worked on the smoke house till 3 P.M. then went to fix Box. Tide not low enough. [margin: Shot a goose.]
  • Sun Feb 10:  Went down to Whitneys and took a boat ride. Aunt Lou & Eva went with us. [margin: Pleasant.]
  • Mon Feb 11:  Made a table in the A.M. worked for W. in the P.M. Upson went home today. [margin: Stormy. worked 4 hours.]
  • Tue Feb 12:  Bring up the meat in the smoke house and began the grain car. Worked on sluice box in the eve. W. went to LaConner. [margin: Worked 9 hours.]
  • Wed Feb 13:  Worked on the car all day. Got a letter from George. Reed my watch from Victoria. Also a present from Aunt of $100.00. [margin: Worked 8 1/2 hours. Rainy night.]
  • Thu Feb 14:  Papa and Alfie went for wood in the P.M. Klingenmaier bought some wood. E and A cutting it up in the P.M. [margin: Windy. Fixed box on "45 ft" slough at night.]
  • Fri Feb 15:  Edgar making doors to the safe. T varnishing the table. Aunt Lou and Eva here this P.M. High wind last night blew over the shed on "Allen 80" damages to W's machinery about $50. [margin: Very high wind last night and tide also.]
  • Sat Feb 16:  Worked for Whitney. made a box for Vinz's trunk. Worked on the car and filed the saw. [margin: Work 4 hours. warm]
  • Sun Feb 17:  All went up to the Ridge. Took dinner with Trimble. Got some current cuttings &c. Cool & clowdy.
  • Mon Feb 18:  Worked for W. in the A.M. and began ditch for self in P.M. Eva & Julia had a birth day dinner. All our folks went. Mrs Leamer went to LaConner with Whitney in the P.M. [margin: Worked 4 1/2 hours. The hen has laid 68 eggs up to this date.]
  • Tue Feb 19:  Ditching. Wilson help me on Whitney's acct. at work near the "45 ft" slough. W. came from LaConner this A.M.
  • Wed Feb 20:  Ditching as yesterday. [margin: Heavy rain & pleasant.]
  • Thu Feb 21:  Ditching as usual.

1878 Feb 22-Mar 12

Image Id: 002-0391
Transcription
  • Fri Feb 22:  Still ditching. A book agent gave us a call this P.M. and took supper with us. [margin: Pleasant.]
  • Sat Feb 23:  Ditching. Dug 28 rds today. [margin: Warm. Springlike.]
  • Sun Feb 24:  All stayed at home in the A.M. Edgar and I went down to W a short time in the P.M. Bought some clams. [margin: Pleasant]
  • Mon Feb 25:  Edgar ditching in the A.M. at home. Working for W. in the P.M. W. killed a beef. [margin: ditto]
  • Tue Feb 26:  Edgar working for W. in the A.M. In the P.M. he and mother, Aunt Lou and Alfie went up to the White house for shrubs, &c. Wilson working for Edgar yesterday and today. N.B. I have quit saying Papa. Hence forth he is to go by the name of Edgar. [margin: Rain in tha A.M.]
  • Wed Feb 27:  Set out strawberries. 200 plants. [margin: Rainy in the morning.]
  • Thu Feb 28:  Stewart began ditching. Harry & I began the wharf. worked 9 hours. [margin: Very pleasant.]
  • Fri Mar 01:  Stewart ditching. Working on Wharf 10 hrs.
  • Sat Mar 02:  Worked on wharf 8 hours. Grandma went up to ... [margin: Stewart ditching.]
  • Sun Mar 03:  Went up to Dick Ball's and took dinner. Darcy was here and paid me $30.00 for Whitney which I gave to Aunt Lou.
  • Mon Mar 04:  Worked on the wharf in the A.M. and filed the saw. Worked for self in the P.M. making sluice box &c. Rainy & clowdy cold wind. Harry killed Rosa cow and took her to LaConner. [margin: Worked 5 hours. Very hard wind last night.]
  • Tue Mar 05:  Had a bad cold. Fixed the boat, filed the saw &c. W. got back from Seattle. Stewart ditching. [margin: Very windy.]
  • Wed Mar 06:  Worked for W. Very windy & Rainy. Shot 2 geese. Enlarging the wharf in front of the barn. Toward up my slough box after quiting work. Stewart ditching. Balls folks began plowing on the Allen "80". [margin: Worked 8 hours. Rainy.]
  • Thu Mar 07:  Worked on W's wharf in the A.M. and worked at my Box by the barn in the P.M. Stewart also. 3 hours. [margin: Worked 4 hours. Rainy in P.M. some.]
  • Fri Mar 08:  Ditching part of the day. Put the box in close to the barn. Mrs. Leamer came home with Whitney. [margin: Stewart worked.]
  • Sat Mar 09:  Stewart started for Callahan's to work for him putting in his dam. We started for Pleasant Ridge. Took dinner with O'Laughlins. Got up to O'lls at 3 P.M. Eva went with us.
  • Sun Mar 10:  Staid on Pleasant Ridge all day. [margin: Hard Rain last night and today.]
  • Mon Mar 11:  Came home from Pleasant Ridge. Got home at 1:30 P.M. Filed the saw on Whitney and fixed the wharf in the P.M. Bought a sail. Stewart working. [margin: No rain.]
  • Tue Mar 12:  The ground very wet. Worked for Whitney 10 hours. Stewart working. Mrs. Leamer went to the Millers last night. W. went to LaConner. Clarence White came home with her. [margin: Showery]

1878 Mar 13-30

Image Id: 002-0392
Transcription
  • Wed Mar 13:  Working for Whitney 5 hours. Very heavy rain in the P.M. Stewart working 1/2 day. Ida very sick with tooth ache. I went to Ball's to get hime to pull it. It stopped aching then. [margin: Mrs. Leamer up to Millers.]
  • Thu Mar 14:  Work for W. 10 hours. Alfie went after a load of wood. Began the black smith shop. Very pleasant drying day. Mother came home with Dick Ball. Stewart working as usual. [margin: Set the hen on 13 eggs.]
  • Fri Mar 15:  Balls plowing in the A.M. Rain in the P.M. Worked for W 10 hours. Stewart ditching. [margin: Planted some little green peas.]
  • Sat Mar 16:  Worked 10 hours for Whitney making roller. Mrs Wallace arrived from San Francisco and move into A. Siegfreds house. The folks went to get her in the P.M. Stewart ditching. [margin: Warm & pleasant.]
  • Sun Mar 17:  Staid at home in the A.M. Aunt Lou, Ida & I went down to see Mrs Wallace in the P.M. [margin: Pleasant.]
  • Mon Mar 18:  Worked for W. 9 hours. He killed an ox and took him to LaConner in the P.M. Worked on roller and plows. Stewart not working any more for me. Ed Stacey came down to live with his mother.
  • Tue Mar 19:  Worked 10 hours for Whitney sawing lumber for walks and Upson's house. The schooner Perry arrived this eve with 28.000 ft of lumber. W. started for Seattle. Alf spaded up dirt for flower beds. [margin: Planted lettuce & radishes]
  • Wed Mar 20:  Helped unload the Perry 28.000 ft of lumber. Ida went up to Allens to a quilting. [margin: Very warm & pleasant.]
  • Thu Mar 21:  Worked for W. 10 hours. Began cutting rafters for Upsons house. Then rigged up plowed started one. [margin: very warm.]
  • Fri Mar 22:  Helped Jones & Otto rig up their plows in the A.M. in the P.M. cut lumber for Upsons house and planted garden seeds. Peas, Onions, Turnips, Bols, &c. [margin: Worked for W 8 hours.]
  • Sat Mar 23:  Whitney's house some today. Made whiffle trees, fixed plows, sawed branches &c. Ida went down to Mrs Wallace's staid all day and I guess all night. Mrs Wallace & her flock staid her last night. [margin: Worked for W. 9 hours. Fine weather with wind.]
  • Sun Mar 24:  We staid at home all day. Whitney's folks went to Dog Island. Alfie & ed went after their cow. Ida sick in the P.M. John Balls and Millers(?) folks called here. [margin: Very pleasant.]
  • Mon Mar 25:  Helped Eddie swim his cow over in the morning then worked for Whitney the balance of the day. [margin: Worked 9 hours. Rain in the P.M.]
  • Tue Mar 26:  Rain all last night and in the A.M. to day. Worked for W. 10 hours. W. went to LaConner.
  • Wed Mar 27:  Worked for W. 10 hours. making Upson's house. W. got home from LaConner. [margin: Pleasant.]
  • Thu Mar 28:  Worked 10 hours on Upson's house as usual. Got the roof pretty near on. Two teams plowing today.
  • Fri Mar 29:  Worked 10 hours. In the shop making window frames &c. Harry & the woodcocks making a house across the slough. Ground very wet. Cold. [margin: Rain all the A.M.]
  • Sat Mar 30:  Peas Radishes & lettuce up. Pleasant day. Ed went to LaConner gen store. [margin: Worked 10 hours setting window frames.]

1878 Mar 31-Apr 18

Image Id: 002-0393
Transcription
  • Sun Mar 31:  Wrote letter to Geo. M.D. Smith & H.L. Blanchard. Cold clowdy day. Whitney & I took a walk down to Klingenmaiers in the P.M. Ida went down to Aunt Lou's and staid the P.M.
  • Mon Apr 01:  Work for Whitney 9 1/2 hours. Carpentering & blacksmithing &c. Alfie down to his mother's. Gertie here. Ed. working for Whitney. Pleasant & warm. Balls & W. plowing.
  • Tue Apr 02:  Worked for W. 10 hours. Began sowing barley for W. Pleasant. Ed plowing my garden. Tied up the cow.
  • Wed Apr 03:  Worked for Whitney 3 hours sowing barley. Worked at home the balance of the day. Setting out fruit trees, &c. Very pleasant day [margin: Baby sick. Ida got a very up tooth ache.]
  • Thu Apr 04:  Sowing grain 4 hours for Whitney. Finished setting out fruit trees. Set out some pie plant & strawberry plants. Sold John Ball my apple trees, all but 13.
  • Fri Apr 05:  Worked for W. 5 hours. Sowing grain. Went up to the Ridge in the P.M. got 3 trees(?) in the marsh garden. Made garden till night. Planted some ???? ??tran peas, some onions, & some Garnet Chile potatos. Down to W's in eve.
  • Sat Apr 06:  2 hours fixed plow for Whitney. Made garden. ????? ???? Buls Cabbage &c. Little rain last night.
  • Sun Apr 07:  Hard rain in the morning. Very high west wind. Blew the shed coff over. Staid at home all day. Took a walk over the Parrish claim in the P.M.
  • Mon Apr 08:  Worked for W. 11 hours. Sowed grain & built wharf. Pleasant but rather cold.
  • Tue Apr 09:  Worked 10 hours sowing grain for Mrs. Allen & and for Whitney. Fine weather.
  • Wed Apr 10:  Worked 8 hours for W. on Barn. Went down to Mrs. Wallaces with Grandma in the evening.
  • Thu Apr 11:  Worked on barn for W. 10 hours. Cold & windy. Mary Wallace has a boy baby. I think Wallace will be around time enough to make another.
  • Fri Apr 12:  Worked for W. on barn 10 hours. Very Cold.
  • Sat Apr 13:  Worked for W. on Barn 8 hours. Very cold and 1/2 inch snow this morning. Started for LaConner at 11 O'clock to get Ida's tooth pulled. Got back at 9 P.M. via Indian Boot slough. Cool evening. Aunt Lou kept the baby.
  • Sun Apr 14:  Frost this morning. Cut Stewarts hair went down to Mrs Wallace's in the P.M. Whitney's hay & seed grain came. Sch. Carrie Hayden.
  • Mon Apr 15:  Worked for w 10 hours on barn.
  • Tue Apr 16:  Worked for W. 9 hours on the barn. Pleasant with frost W went to Whatcom Balls plowing near the house. [margin: Frost.]
  • Wed Apr 17:  Upson's things came. In the A.M. grafted some trees up to the White house. Worked 5 hours for Whitney in the P.M. on Barn. [margin: Fine weather.]
  • Thu Apr 18:  Worked 10 hours on barn & scow. Whitney took his horses over the slough. Land very soft.

1878 Apr 19-May 7

Image Id: 002-0394
Transcription
  • Fri Apr 19:  Worked for W. 10 hours on Boat & Scow. Cold and dry with north winds. Sluice box on 45 ft slough open this evening and slough filled up.
  • Sat Apr 20:  Worked for W. 5 hours on scow. Made sail sticks in the P.M. Fine dry weather. Upson & family came this morning.
  • Sun Apr 21:  Very pleasant Sunday. Attended preaching at Whitneys house at ten A.M. by the Rev Mr. Tade of Fidalgo from text of Mathew 4 Chap & 17 verse.
  • Mon Apr 22:  Worked for W. 10 hours on the scow. Cold & Windy with a very little rain, but good weather for plowing & seeding.
  • Tue Apr 23:  Worked for W. 10 hours on scow. It is ready to turn over. Fine weather. Whitney at LaConner.
  • Wed Apr 24:  Heavy S.E. wind in the A.M. and rain in the P.M. Hauled a boat load of water in the A.M. Fixed sail, cut wood &c. Worked for W. 4 hours spinning Oakum. Working on scow &c.
  • Thu Apr 25:  Worked for W. 10 hours on scow. Fine weather for farming. Morse of the N. Star in Padilla to night.
  • Fri Apr 26:  Worked for W 9 hours on scow. Editor Morse here. I subscribed for the N. Star 6 months. Balls sowing oats here by the house.
  • Sat Apr 27:  Worked for W. 5 hours calking scow. Planted peas and fixed he eve troughs with pitch.
  • Sun Apr 28:  Pleasant Sunday. Staid at home in the A.M. and went boat riding in the P.M. Down to Mrs. Wallace but did not land water too rough. Jake Highbarger & Acu?? here to dinner.
  • Mon Apr 29:  Worked for W. 10 hours on scow. Cold & windy.
  • Tue Apr 30:  Worked for W 8 hours on scow & went after water.
  • Wed May 01:  Worked for W 4 hours on scow and finished it. Hanson went home. In P.M. went out and tore down shed for my self. Filed saws also. Mrs ????? & Siegfreds were here to dinner. Mrs Leamer here also.
  • Thu May 02:  Worked for Sisson today. Sawed lumber for granary and maade a cupboard for the kitchen chimney to stand on.
  • Fri May 03:  Worked on kitchen making cupboard and laying chimney &c. Warm & dry. Whitney took the scow and went after water.
  • Sat May 04:  Fixed the boat. planted some turnips, squashes and sweet corn. Weed onions and am shones(?) [margin: Rain in the A.M. and some just night.]
  • Sun May 05:  Very pleasant Sunday. Mrs. Leamer and Gertie went down to Mrs. Wallace's. We staid at home. Mrs. Dewey call a little while also Mr & Mrs Ball Dick.
  • Mon May 06:  Went to LaConner. Spent the rest of the day sitting around doing nothing. Got home at 8 o'clock.
  • Tue May 07:  Worked at home making cupboard for kitchen and doors for Jones house &c.

1878 May 8-29

Transcription

  • Thu May 09:  Went after water and the sowing machine in the morning. Whitewashed the sitting room &c.
  • Fri May 10:  Went down to Hanson's to see about getting some lumber for barn. Aunt Lou papering the sitting room. [margin: North wind strong.]
  • Sat May 11:  Worked around the house making mop boards, cupboard doors &c.
  • Sun May 12:  Staid at home all day. Ida went to Mrs. Wallace. Pleasant day.
  • Mon May 13:  Helped Whitney load schooner in the A.M. and in P.M. went down and worked on tank plank. Planted beans, &c.
  • Tue May 14:  Worked in garden hoing in the A.M. and in P.M. finished loading schooner.
  • Wed May 15:  Working down at Whitneys on scow and oar. Rain last night and stormy & windy to day.
  • Thu May 16:  Worked on tank most all day. Went choir meeting in the eve.
  • Fri May 17:  Sharpened plows for Whitney. Patched the tank and sowed oats for Callahan.

  • Sun May 19:  Spent most of the day at Whites. Got home at 6 P.M. Pleasant P.M. Rain in A.M. [margin: Rain all night last night.]
  • Mon May 20:  Worked in the garden & made a pig pen. Sowed 8 acres of grain for Balls. Pleasant.
  • Tue May 21:  Went down on Siegfreds land and cut some sleepers for granary and brought up a load of wood at night.
  • Wed May 22:  Sowed 11 acres of grain for Balls and took shakes off from the shed roof. Worked in garden some.
  • Thu May 23:  Went down and got Trimble to haul out my sleepers. Ida went down to Mrs. Wallace's and staid all day.
  • Fri May 24:  Went with Whitney to the Samish to see about threshing job, &c. Staid all night at E Stearns. Fine weather.
  • Sat May 25:  Got home from the Samish at 1 P.M. had a pleasant time. Hoed garden worked on tank some in the P.M. Balls folks got through sowing grain.
  • Sun May 26:  Cold & windy. Had preaching at Whitney's house by Preacher Tade of Fidalgo. Txt Isiah V Ch III, IV & V vs. [margin: Small rain last night.]
  • Mon May 27:  Work in the garden some. Set out some cabbage. Fixed tank some. Clowdy day. [margin: Rain a little.]
  • Tue May 28:  Worked in the garden some setting cabbage plants. And worked on the road some. Went and rafted my sleepers in the P.M. [margin: Rain.]
  • Wed May 29:  Went and fixed a bulge in the slough by Jakes corner in the A.M. W. went to LaConner. Hanson up here. Went down after my raft in the P.M. & put a handle on the broad ax. W. came home from LaConner. Ball hauled a load of stuff off from E side of ranch.

Transcription

  • Fri May 31:  Worked on wood shed and hoed in the garden. Warm & pleasant. [margin: Ida no better.]
  • Sat Jun 01:  Hoed in garden some. began building a chicken house. went up to the Ridge to get a pig. Lithi Upon came up and helped Ida some. [margin: Ida still sick.]
  • Sun Jun 02:  Staid at home all day. Ws folks went to Fidalgo. Mother Leamer came home in the eve. The schooner Perry here after grain.
  • Mon Jun 03:  Finished the hen house and worked in the garden.
  • Tue Jun 04:  Finished the tank and went dow to Whitney's to cut some rafters for granary. [margin: Court week in LaConner.]
  • Wed Jun 05:  Made a bridge across the slough by the shed.
  • Thu Jun 06:  Fixed road by the dyke and too the house. Ida & Aunt Lou went after berries up to the W. house.
  • Fri Jun 07:  Began honing sleepers for th granary. Mr Whites folks came from Fidalgo. W. went after them.
  • Sat Jun 08:  Finished my sleepers. Mr Whites folks here to tea.
  • Sun Jun 09:  Baby Pearl first birthday. Went down to W's in the A.M. and up to the Ridge in the P.M. Ida went home with Mrs. White.
  • Mon Jun 10:  Worked up to the Ridge hewing rails. Pleasant.
  • Tue Jun 11:  Went down to Larry's Slough to get my raft. Got to Hanson's at 1 P.M. Worked getting in ?????? TO P.M. Staid all night with Hanson.
  • Wed Jun 12:  Worked in A.M. loading raft and in P.M. started home. Got to Siegfred's point and then went home at 10 P.M. Fine weather.
  • Thu Jun 13:  Hoed garden some. worked for W. some on roller. Brought up my raft in the P.M.
  • Fri Jun 14:  Worked for W. in the A.M. on Roller & Egine smoke pipe in the P.M. went up and helped pick strawberries for PickNick.
  • Sat Jun 15:  All hand went to the picnic at Saddle Island. ida came home with us from Mr Whites. Had a very pleasant time Considering. Lisle Wallace was here and staid all night with us.
  • Sun Jun 16:  Went up to the White House and picked strawberries & gooseberries. Traveled around and looked at the grain fields and went to take Wallace up Telegraph slound and to Bakers(?) calling. Ed hauling wood.
  • Mon Jun 17:  Worked for Whitney- 5 hours. Worked for self in the P.M. fixing tank, eve troughts &c. Made a new rudder. Rain some.
  • Tue Jun 18:  Piled my Corn. Hanson was here. Ed hauling wood. Upson helped kill the cow. 8 hours. Worked for W. 5 hours.
  • Wed Jun 19:  Worked for W 9 house on kitchen. Ed hauled wood a half a day & a load of water. Dry weather. Cutting W's grain.
  • Thu Jun 20:  Worked for W. 10 hours. Ed hauling wood. The folks went up to Allen's to sowing Bee.

1878 Jun 21-Jul 10

Image Id: 002-0397
Transcription
  • Fri Jun 21:  Worked for W. 10 hours on kitchen. Ed hauling wood 1/2 day. Very warm. Carrie & Frank White came over in the eve.
  • Sat Jun 22:  Ida, Ed Stacey & I went to LaConner. Upson came home with us. Very dry and rain is much needed for late grain.
  • Sun Jun 23:  Staid at home all day. Carrie White & Frank were here a little while. Warm & dry as usual.
  • Mon Jun 24:  Worked for W. 10 hours on house. Went down and settled up accts in the eve.
  • Tue Jun 25:  Worked 10 hours for W. The ?????? all ??? in the P.M. but no rain.

  • Mon Jan 13:  Stormy some. Put a few pickets on the chicken yard. [margin: Snow.]
  • Tue Jan 14:  Finished the chicken yard. Cold and frosty this morning. [margin: Snow.]
  • Wed Jan 15:  Mr Clark eat dinner here today. In the A.M. staid around home and hung an ax and got ready to go to the woods. In the P.M. went up to the woods & cut down a tree for rails. McKown came with the beef just dark. [margin: Snow.]
  • Thu Jan 16:  Cut up and took care of the beef in the A.M. and helped Jones fix up Trimbles old boat after dinner. Cold & stormy.
  • Fri Jan 17:  Snow last night as usual. Went up to the woods. Made a hundred rails and cut another cedar. Lots of snow in the woods. Went to Literary in the evening.
  • Sat Jan 18:  Satid in the house all day did not do anything. Cold, Windy & Rainy.
  • Sun Jan 19:  Staid at home all day. Went down to Whitneys just night. Lisle Wallace was here all day.
  • Mon Jan 20:  Went up to the timber and made rails & pickets.
  • Tue Jan 21:  Making rails as usual. Al & Martha came down this P.M. [margin: Rain last night.]
  • Wed Jan 22:  Al went home this morning. Making rails as usual. Rain in the P.M. Have 815 12 ft rails to date.
