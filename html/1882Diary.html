1881 Dec 2 -Jan 2, 1882

Image Id: 002-0452
Transcription
  • Fri Dec 02:  Rain cold weather. Hauled 3 loads of rails. Mr Upson here to dinner. He started for home today.
  • Sat Dec 03:  Done chores worked on the tank some. Finished hauling rails. Grandma & Gertie Wallace came. [margin: Mercury at 20°]
  • Sun Dec 04:  Alfie Stacey here to day & Darcy's folks in the P.M. Rainy some.
  • Mon Dec 05:  Rainy day did not do much but chores. Set money to Wasthoff per Andrews.
  • Tue Dec 06:  Otto Kalso here helping me kill hogs. Went to LaConner in the P.M. with Whitney.
  • Wed Dec 07:  Cut up the pork & packed it. Shed-up 8 more hogs & began to feed them. White brought the machine home. Got a picture of Garfield(?) of France.
  • Thu Dec 08:  Ditching in the A.M. Helping Whitney in the P.M. Tearing down the Powell house.
  • Fri Dec 09:  Working for Whitney to day. 10 hours all told.
  • Sat Dec 10:  Cold & windy. Ditching to day for myself.
  • Sun Dec 11:  Strong wind last night. Water over the marsh but not very high for this season of the year. Went up to Allens in the P.M.
  • Mon Dec 12:  Digging ditch & doing chores. A little snow on the ground this A.M.
  • Tue Dec 13:  Dig ditch some but had to quit for rain. Cut wood & worked on tank some.
  • Wed Dec 14:  Digging ditch all day & doing chores.
  • Thu Dec 15:  Ditching some & cutting wood.
  • Fri Dec 16:  Ditching in the A.M. & working on the tank in the P.M. Rainy.
  • Sat Dec 17:  Ditto yesterday. Rainy in the P.M.
  • Sun Dec 18:  Harry here in the A.M. & Mr Grey in the P.M. Mrs. Jones went home to day.
  • Mon Dec 19:  Helped Whitney kill hogs & took the scow & engine over to Telegraph slough. Warm & pleasant.
  • Tue Dec 20:  Digging ditch all day. Very windy & blustering weather.
  • Wed Dec 21:  Started for LaConner with Whitney & the engine. Did not get there till nearly night.
  • Thu Dec 22:  Strong S.E. wind yesterday & all day to day. Started for home with the scow & some lumber. Came as far as Kelly's & anchored. Got home at 1 oclock. Staid with Ed Stacey last night.
  • Fri Dec 23:  Strong S.E. wind all day. Jones & I ditching all day on the 4 acre lot on the Allen 80.
  • Sat Dec 24:  Jones & I ditching in the a.M. Clear pleasant day. In the P.M. I began work on the scow.
  • Sun Dec 25:  Mercury at 30 this morning. White frost. Clear & pleasant Christmas day.
  • Mon Dec 26:  Went out to ditch but had to quit for rain. Worked on thee tank some & cut wood. Highest tide of the season so far.
  • Tue Dec 27:  Ditching some in the fore noon. Working in the granary in the P.M. Stormy day.
  • Wed Dec 28:  Ditching to day. Frost this morning. Whitney gone to Seattle. Ed Stacey & Grandma here also Sauer.
  • Thu Dec 29:  Ditching to day.
  • Fri Dec 30:  Went with Wallace after the scow. Jones working here today. Begin work on the scow.
  • Sat Dec 31:  Rainy in the A.M. working in the granary making work bench &c. Working on the scow in the P.M. John Siegfred here last night to but my oats I offered to let him have for 27.00 per ton.
  • Sun Jan 01:  Very pleasant spring like weather. John Siegfred here this morning to tell me I could have 27.00 for my oats.
  • Mon Jan 02:  Went to LaConer & rec $800. payment on my oats. Sent pay for my sacks & 63 dollars to Wilson. Gave(?) Eds. Cov. for 28.00. Went to the N.Y. Ball at the hall a little while to see them love(?).

1882 Jan 3-31

Image Id: 002-0453
Transcription
  • Tue Jan 03:  Staid in LaConner in the A.M. & came home in the P.M. Otto also rec'd $800 on my grain from Gilliland yesterday.
  • Wed Jan 04:  Spent the day weighing grain. Brought Jenni's mare shoed & harness for $180. and paid $8.75 on George's account 188.75 all told.
  • Thu Jan 05:  Working for Whitney to day loading grain.
  • Fri Jan 06:  Finished weight my grain & worked on the scow in the P.M. Harry started east again.
  • Sat Jan 07:  In the A.M. fixed up the tank and went to Whitneys to get hog trough for scalding. In the P.M. went after the mare to Jennes.
  • Sun Jan 08:  Staid at home all day.
  • Mon Jan 09:  Helped load the steamer & schooner with Whitneys grain 5 hours. Done chores the balance of the time.
  • Tue Jan 10:  Went to Utsalady with Capt Olney for lumber. Staid all night in Utsalady.
  • Wed Jan 11:  Came home from Utsalady. Left order for 9500 ft lumber. Got home at 4 P.M. Clear & cool.
  • Thu Jan 12:  Working on scow most of the day. Went down to fix sluice box just night with Whitney.
  • Fri Jan 13:  Steamer Susie & Sch Perry in this morning. She brought the matting & a stick for scow & boat. Ida put down the matting & I worked on scow all day. Settled with John Ball.
  • Sat Jan 14:  Pleasant weather. Otto & Grey helped me kill hogs. Got through at 3 P.M.
  • Sun Jan 15:  Pleasant weather. Staid at home in the P.M. Went down to Klingenmaiers in the A.M.
  • Mon Jan 16:  Frosty. In the A.M. loaded grain for Whitney & cut up and packed the pork in the P.M.
  • Tue Jan 17:  Working on my scow all day too, finished putting on the bottom. Nova Callahan was here helping Ida some. Fine working weather. Tide very low.
  • Wed Jan 18:  Knocked my old harrow to pieces & fixed up the boat. Got a letter from Stewart.
  • Thu Jan 19:  Went out to ditch. Had to quit for rain. Worked on my harrow in the P.M. Rain all day.
  • Fri Jan 20:  Worked all day unloading lumber & loading grain. Went to LaConner in the eve.
  • Sat Jan 21:  Came home this A.M. Rec'd sulky plow from Davis yesterday. Worked on the scow in the P.M. & went after my boat over to the canal.
  • Sun Jan 22:  Warm & spring like but rather windy.
  • Mon Jan 23:  Went and hunted up my horses then worked on scow some. Ditching in the P.M.
  • Tue Jan 24:  Went up & worked on tank for G. Brown in the A.M. & dug ditch for self in P.M.
  • Wed Jan 25:  Got a letter from Wilson. Last night went to LaConner & got the balance of my oat money. Bought orders for 400. dollars to send to Wilson. Snow storm to day.
  • Thu Jan 26:  Cold but good weather to ditch. Ditching most of the day. Jenni & Otto here in the P.M. He Jenni paid me $100. and lifted his note.
  • Fri Jan 27:  Cold & frosty. Done the chores & dug ditch.
  • Sat Jan 28:  Cold & frosty. Mercury at 12 this A.M. ditching.
  • Sun Jan 29:  Cold & frosty. Mr Taylor here to dinner. Was down to Whitneys a little while in the P.M. Warmer.
  • Mon Jan 30:  Snow storm last night & to day. Done chores & worked on the harrow some. Ditching in the P.M. [margin: Stacey's cow calved today.]
  • Tue Jan 31:  Warmer. Ditching & finished the first field.

1882 Feb 1-Mar 7

Image Id: 002-0454
Transcription
  • Wed Feb 01:  Went over to Jenni's & began work on the tank.
  • Thu Feb 02:  Done chores in the A.M. & went to Jennes to work on the tank in the P.M.
  • Fri Feb 03:  Cold windy day. Went to Jennes to calk the tank. Finished at 4 P.M.
  • Sat Feb 04:  Done chores in the A.M. & went to Jennes to work on tank in the P.M.
  • Sun Feb 05:  Staid at home as usual. Otto & Allens girls gave us a call in the evening.
  • Mon Feb 06:  Cold & windy ditching all day.
  • Tue Feb 07:  Rainy most of the day. Cut wood & done chores.
  • Wed Feb 08:  Mrs & Ida Powell staid here last night. Stormy some ditched about 1/2 day.
  • Thu Feb 09:  Stormy day. Hardest wind of the season. Helped Ida wash & done the chores.
  • Fri Feb 10:  Ditching most of the day. Snowy in the A.M. Went down to the hall and played for a little time(?).
  • Sat Feb 11:  Went to LaConner. Stormy day, but not cold. Forwarded the money orders to Wilson for the second time. This has been the toughest week of the winter so far. [margin: Rec'd a letter from George.]
  • Sun Feb 12:  Rather pleasant day went & got my boat at the canal. Otto Kalso here in the P.M. Klingenmaier came up with the wood.
  • Mon Feb 13:  Stormy in the A.M. ditching in the P.M. Helped Klingenmaier unload the scow.
  • Tue Feb 14:  Help Whitney on his bedroom 6 hours. Stormy.
  • Wed Feb 15:  Stormy. Helped Whitney 6 hours on his bedroom.
  • Thu Feb 16:  The Saranack in after hay. Snow storm last night. Clear & cold. Ditching in the meadow.
  • Fri Feb 17:  Mercury down to 12 this morning. Ditching.
  • Sat Feb 18:  Clear & cold. Did not do much to day but sow wood & make a granary door. Dance at Padilla in the evening.
  • Sun Feb 19:  Lyle Wallace, Mary, Ed & Miss Dodge here today & last night. Pleasant day. Warmer.
  • Mon Feb 20:  3 inches more snow this morning. Overhauled my seed oats in the A.M. & worked for Whitney 4 hours in the P.M. making chimney &c. [margin: 4 hours.]
  • Tue Feb 21:  Working for Whitney 6 hours to day.
  • Wed Feb 22:  Working for Whitney 6 hours to day. Windy some snow.
  • Thu Feb 23:  Working for Whitney on bedroom 7 hours.
  • Fri Feb 24:  Pleasant day. Went to Nevils slough with the scow for Hanson to bring ditch timber.
  • Sat Feb 25:  Stormy & hung up the bacon. Done chores & cut wood &c. Windy & little rainy.
  • Sun Feb 26:  Pleasant day. S.W. wind. Begins to act like spring. Began to smoke the meat. Staid at home all day.
  • Mon Feb 27:  Stormy day. Went to LaConner with Otto & Guss Stroble. Traded 27.00 worth to day. Signed a note with O Kalso for $150.
  • Tue Feb 28:  Ditching. Soderburg helping me 9 hours.
  • Wed Mar 01:  Ditching. Soderburg working 9 hours. White cow calved to day. Gave it to Callahans.
  • Thu Mar 02:  Ditching. Soderburg working 8 hours.
  • Fri Mar 03:  Ditching in the A.M. Soderburg working 4 hours. Made a sluice box in the P.M. Pleasant.
  • Sat Mar 04:  Very pleasant weather. Making fence & gates. My ditch timber came this P.M. from Hansons.
  • Sun Mar 05:  Fine drying weather. Darcy's folks & Mrs Jones called on us to day. Was down to Whitneys in the P.M.
  • Mon Mar 06:  Splitting ditch timber all day.
  • Tue Mar 07:  Mrs Whitney had a girl baby this morning. or in other words occured in the annual celebration. Washed & greased harness all day. Stormy some.

1882 Mar 8-Apr 5

Image Id: 002-0455
Transcription
  • Wed Mar 08:  Done chores. Finished the sluice box and began digging a place to put it in. Snow storm this morning. Paid Klingenmaier $20. 10 for Jenni & 10 for me. I owe Jenni 4 yet on old account for Geo Brown.
  • Thu Mar 09:  Digging for sluice box & putting it in. Soderburg & Guss worked 6 hours. Windy.
  • Fri Mar 10:  Splitting ditch timber in the A.M. and making apron & ditching in the P.M.
  • Sat Mar 11:  Helping Soderburg make a tank to day. Fine weather for the past 8 days. Frosty nights.
  • Sun Mar 12:  Fina weather. Got a letter from Vinz. Fine prospect for farming soon.
  • Mon Mar 13:  Soderburg helping me ditch in return for Saturday's work. Plesant.
  • Tue Mar 14:  Rigged up a team and hauled out 4 loads of ditch stuff. Clowdy and a rainy prospect.
  • Wed Mar 15:  Hauled out the balance of the first scow load of ditch stuff and filled ditches in the P.M. A dance at Padilla in the P.M. did not go. Ida not well enough.
  • Thu Mar 16:  Filling & digging ditches. Rain last night.
  • Fri Mar 17:  Rainy in the A.M. working around the barn in the A.M. and at the ditches in the P.M. Have put 34 days works on my ditches up to date. OLoughlin here assessing this eve. He put my stuff at $2150. which make cash taxes at 34.40 and poll tax
  • Sat Mar 18:  Ditching or covering ditch. Cold north wind and some snow in the eve. Otto had old jim to work this P.M.
  • Sun Mar 19:  Very pleasant. Was down to Whitneys a few minutes.
  • Mon Mar 20:  Ditching. Strong S.E. wind.
  • Tue Mar 21:  Trimming trees in the A.M. Went down to Klingenmaiers. Made a wagon jack & greased the wagon.
  • Wed Mar 22:  Rigged up the team & tried the sulk plow. Land most too wet for a good test. Shall not plow any more until it is dryer.
  • Thu Mar 23:  Began deepening an old ditch out by the meadow. It rained in the P.M. Fixed a plane for gutter & dug out one. Isaacson here to get a job.
  • Fri Mar 24:  Rained all the A.M. Land full of water. No plowing this month. made & put up gutters for the granary. Ditching 3 hours in the P.M.
  • Sat Mar 25:  Rainy day. Helping Whitney make eave troughs 7 hours.
  • Sun Mar 26:  Cold & wet.
  • Mon Mar 27:  Rainy in the A.M. Ditching in the P.M. [margin: Sent letters to A.G.T. & Lupton & Co, New York]
  • Tue Mar 28:  No rain but heavy W. wind. Got a letter from George. Cleaning out ditch in the A.M. & digging a place for the scow in the P.M.
  • Wed Mar 29:  Clowdy & cold. Started for LaConner against S.E. wind got there at 3.30 P.M. Staid all night with Mary Wallace, Ida also.
  • Thu Mar 30:  Came home from LaConner at 3 P.M. Pleasant.
  • Fri Mar 31:  Windy day. Rain in the P.M. Working some at the scow slip.
  • Sat Apr 01:  Rainy day. Took the scow down to Klingenmaiers in the morning. And brought the ditch stuff up in the P.M. Shields & Soderburg helped me. The land wet as it ever was. Grass growing fast.
  • Sun Apr 02:  Rainy in the morning. Pleasant in the P.M. Was down to W.s a little while. Brought the scow up to the barn.
  • Mon Apr 03:  Split ditch stuff & went to the school meeting. Shields helped me on Whitneys account.
  • Tue Apr 04:  Splitting ditch stuff. Rain some in the P.M.
  • Wed Apr 05:  Hauling out ditch timber 6 loads. Pleasant day.

1881 Apr 6-May 6Duplicate of next

Image Id: 002-0456
Transcription
  • Thu Apr 06:  Filling ditches all day. Otto plowing to day.
  • Fri Apr 07:  Ditto yesterday. Ground in pretty fair order to plow. Have all the ditch stuff hauled out. 22 cords.
  • Sat Apr 08:  Rainy most of the day. Went to LaConner to view the road from LaConner to the White place. Ida went with me. Took supper with Mary Wallace and came home at 8 P.M. Farmers all plowing about LaConner.
  • Sun Apr 09:  Clowdy day. This is a late spring. Nothing growing much except grass.
  • Mon Apr 10:  Plowed a little in the garden. Plowed in ditches & began with the Sulky plow in the 12 acre piece.
  • Tue Apr 11:  Plowing most all day. Land in fair order, but none to dry. Planted some E.R. potatos.
  • Wed Apr 12:  PLowing drove old Doll today for the first time in nearly a year. Planted Vinz's cabbage seed.
  • Thu Apr 13:  Plowing. Began on the "Allen 80" pretty wet. Put the tule shoes on old Jim this morning.
  • Fri Apr 14:  Rain last night. Filling ditches in the P.M. Working at the scow ditch in the A.M.
  • Sat Apr 15:  Too wet to plow. Went to Fidalgo Island. Called on Bindon's & staid over night at Whites.
  • Sun Apr 16:  Spent the A.M. at Whites and came home in the P.M. Got 2 sacks of potatos of Bindon valued at 3.00. Took him one sack of barley.
  • Mon Apr 17:  Plowing in ditching & plowing with 2 horses. Rather damp.
  • Tue Apr 18:  Plowing with the "Sulky" in A.M. Rainy in the P.M. Run cattle out of the field & fixed fence.
  • Wed Apr 19:  Covered ditch in the A.M. Went down to Klingenmaiers in the P.M. Very heavy rain, hail & snow this P.M.
  • Thu Apr 20:  Windy day but no rain. Covering ditches in the A.M. Made clamps for Roth's Pt file guide in the P.M. Filed the backsaw & cut wood some.
  • Fri Apr 21:  Cold & windy. Made eave troughs & filed saws.
  • Sat Apr 22:  Raining again last night and today. Ground full of water and no prospect of any more plowing this month. This is the coldest, wettest, most disagreeable April on record.
  • Sun Apr 23:  1/2 inch of snow on the ground this morning. Very cold. Ws down to Whitney's in the P.M.
  • Mon Apr 24:  Showery & cold. Ditching all day out by the clover patch.
  • Tue Apr 25:  Went up to the Jenning's Ranch and cut ditch timber. 2 cords. Still wet & cold.
  • Wed Apr 26:  Ditching & cleaning out an old ditch on the "Allen 80" Quite a pleasant day. The land drying up some.
  • Thu Apr 27:  Went to the timber in the A.M. & made a cord of ditch timber. Split timber in the P.M. & hauled out a load by the Cabbage patch ditch.
  • Fri Apr 28:  Drying day. Mud drying up fast- Kalsow's plowing. Hauling ditch timber in the A.M. & putting it in in the P.M. Have pu in 26 1/2 cords up to this date. Have put 50 days works on ditches to this date.
  • Sat Apr 29:  Plowing some with the sulky plow in the A.M. & harrowing in the P.M. Pleasant weather.
  • Sun Apr 30:  Pleasant day. Went to the Singing meeting in the P.M. Billy Hanson came up to work.
  • Mon May 01:  Harrowing 3/4 & seeding 3/4 7 1/4 acre. Fine weather but looks like a storm.
  • Tue May 02:  Rainy day. Soderburg helped me shoe old Dolly. Ida went to LaConner with Whitneys folks.
  • Wed May 03:  Plowed with 1 team in the P.M. Plowing with two teams in the morning. Seeding & harrowing & in the P.M. 11 acres sowed.
  • Thu May 04:  Plowing with one team & harrowing with one. Finished the 11 acre piece. Plowing on the Allen 80 south side.
  • Fri May 05:  Plowing with 2 teams 1 team 3/4 day.
  • Sat May 06:  Rainy in the A.M. Billy went home this morning. Plowing with Sulky plow. Rec'd a letter from Harry. Have about 14 acres more plowed. 25 all told.

1882 May 7-Jun 10

Image Id: 002-0458
Transcription
  • Sun May 07:  Pleasant day. Went to Sunday school & singing in the P.M.
  • Mon May 08:  Plowing in the A.M. with 2 horses harrowing with one. Billy quit at noon. Plowing with the Sulky plow in the P.M.
  • Tue May 09:  Whitney had a horse yesterday & today. Plowing with the sulky plow. Rainy in the P.M. Ida went to town with Whitney. Peter Ball(?) here. Sold him a horse. He did not pay for it. [margin: Peter & John HickMiller(?) arrived here this eve from Penna.]
  • Wed May 10:  Plowing with the Sulky in the A.M. Rainy. 1/2 inch of water fell. Very cold. Cut wood some in the P.M.
  • Thu May 11:  Ground very wet. Sowed some timothy & clover and filled ditch & dug out logs. Plowing with Sulky in the P.M. Peter planted cabbage seed in the P.M. [margin: Nettie weaned today.]
  • Fri May 12:  Drying day. Plowing with Sulky plow & a two horse plow. Had a horse of Whitney.
  • Sat May 13:  Same as yesterday, land drying fast.
  • Sun May 14:  Rain a little in the morning. Pleasant in the P.M. Went to Sunday school. DArcy's folks here.
  • Mon May 15:  Peter plowing. I harrowing. Sowed the W Russian oats.
  • Tue May 16:  Peter plowing. I seeding 9 1/2 acres.
  • Wed May 17:  Peter plowing. I seeding. 6. 28 all told.
  • Thu May 18:  Peter plowing. I plowing in the A.M. Harrowing in the P.M. Quite a hard rain last night.
  • Fri May 19:  Peter plowing. I harrowing after the seeder.
  • Sat May 20:  Whitney went to town for me. Work same as yesterday.
  • Sun May 21:  Very pleasant drying weather.
  • Mon May 22:  Rough harrowing & plowing.
  • Tue May 23:  Seeding & plowing. Finished plowing the old ground.
  • Wed May 24:  Seeding. Peter harrowing after the seeder.
  • Thu May 25:  Began breaking up some timothy land. Harrowing.
  • Fri May 26:  Finished seeding old land 56 acres. Harrowing.
  • Sat May 27:  Harrowing & plowing meadow.
  • Sun May 28:  Went to Sunday school & Singing. Very fine ground weather. Darcy came & got 16 sacks of grain. He is seeding up on Trimbles Ranch.
  • Mon May 29:  Peter harrowing on the 16 acre piece. I finished breaking the sod piece & began rolling it.
  • Tue May 30:  Peter harrowing finished the old ground at noon. I rolling all day. Darcy got the balance of his oats. 48 sacks at $28 per ton. Came to $56.00.
  • Wed May 31:  Peter harrowing on the sod. Whitney helped me kill the calf. Rolling the balance of the day. Warm & dry.
  • Thu Jun 01:  Seeding the sod piece. Sowed 20 bs on 4 acres. Peter harrowing. Went to plow for Whitney but it was a failure. Whitney took the meat to town.
  • Fri Jun 02:  Warm & dry. Finished harrowing & rolled some. Peter plowed & harrowed a patch for cabbage. Began plowing in orchard.
  • Sat Jun 03:  Rolling some this morning. Working in the orchard the balance of the day. Stormy N. wind. Ground very dry.
  • Sun Jun 04:  Very warm & dry. Went to S.S. in the P.M. The Skagit River is getting high. Thunder shower at night. [margin: Gave Davis a note for $86. due in 6 months with 1% interest.]
  • Mon Jun 05:  Went to LaConner Ida and the children went also. Went up to A Leamer's in the evening. Found his ranch and many others covered with water. Rained some last night. [margin: Sowed some barley in the orchard.]
  • Tue Jun 06:  Went to LaConner and from there home. Every body on the Skagit, on the Beaver Marsh & on the East side of Sullivan slough flooded.
  • Wed Jun 07:  Peter setting cabbage plants for Whitney in the A.M. and for us in the P.M. I helping Soderburg fix dyke. Water up to the top of his dyke. All day. [margin: More rain last night.]
  • Thu Jun 08:  Peter helping & I setting cabbage plants in the A.M. & hoing in the garden in the P.M.
  • Fri Jun 09:  Water plants in the A.M. & hoeing orchard. Working on G.S. Browns job in the P.M. 2 hours.
  • Sat Jun 10:  Working on G.S. Brown's 1/2 day for 2 men. Peter working on slough crossing by the barn in the P.M. [margin: I went to LaConner in the P.M. Staid all night. ]

1882 Jun 11-Jul 17

Image Id: 002-0459
Transcription
  • Sun Jun 11:  Spent the day in LaConner went to church morning & eve & to Sunday School in the P.M.
  • Mon Jun 12:  Peter working in the garden. We came home from LaConner in the P.M. fine growing weather.
  • Tue Jun 13:  Went up to Jennings ranch and cut wood. Peter also.
  • Wed Jun 14:  Cutting same as yesterday. Very warm.
  • Thu Jun 15:  Ditto yesterday.
  • Fri Jun 16:  Rain last night & this morning. Setting cabbage some, filing saws, cutting wood in the P.M.
  • Sat Jun 17:  Went down to the new store to work. Peter also.
  • Sun Jun 18:  John Hancock & E. Stacey here last night & today. Rainy this A.M. No Sunday school.
  • Mon Jun 19:  Both working on the saloon. Ida went to town.
  • Tue Jun 20:  Working on the saloon. Rainy day.
  • Wed Jun 21:  Not working in the A.M. Set cabbage in the P.M. Went to Darcys in the eve.
  • Thu Jun 22:  Helped unload the machine in the morning. Worked on the Saloon the balance of the day. Mr Stewart staid all night. Strawberries plenty.
  • Fri Jun 23:  Working on the Saloon as usual. Grain very weedy and not looking well.
  • Sat Jun 24:  Picked the berries & sent them to LaConner. Working on the saloon the balance of the day. Have worked 67 hours to date. Ida came home to day.
  • Sun Jun 25:  Very pleasant day. Took a walk in Whitney's grain also in my own; the late grain is looking well, hay also. Went to Sunday school.
  • Mon Jun 26:  Worked on the saloon. 6 days to date. Al Leamer & Mrs Wallace here this eve and all night.
  • Tue Jun 27:  Down cutting timber for the granary all day. Mosquitos very plenty.
  • Wed Jun 28:  Rafting timber & working on the store job. 6 hours. Fine growing weather.
  • Thu Jun 29:  Helping set up the engine in the A.M. & rafting timber in the P.M. Peter working on the Saloon all day.
  • Fri Jun 30:  Both working on the Saloon all day. Mosquitos thick.
  • Sat Jul 01:  Peter working at the saloon. I went to LaConner. Fine growing weather.
  • Sun Jul 02:  Went to Sunday school & wrote to George. Warm.
  • Mon Jul 03:  Worked about 8 hours on the Saloon & came up to take out the granary timber. Warm growing weather.
  • Tue Jul 04:  Peter & Grandma went to LaConner. We went to Whitneys to dinner. Made window frames in the A.M.
  • Wed Jul 05:  Not well. Did not do anything. Whites folks here in the P.M. The folks home from the 4th.
  • Thu Jul 06:  Peter & I working on Jenni's kitchen. Went for a load of wood at night to the beach.
  • Fri Jul 07:  Peter & I working on Jenni's kitchen.
  • Sat Jul 08:  Peter & I working on Jenni's kitchen.
  • Sun Jul 09:  Went down to the hall but had no Sunday school. Not enough present. Sand a little. Fine weather.
  • Mon Jul 10:  Killed the cow, went to LaConner with the beef in the P.M. Bought a new cook stove. Peter cultivated the cabbage.
  • Tue Jul 11:  I worked over at Jennes house. Peter hoing cabbage.
  • Wed Jul 12:  Both worked at Jennes till 3 P.M. Hoed cabbage till night. Warm & dry, good growing weather.
